## Target Gross Margin

| Category | Target Margin |
|----------|---------------|
| Minimum acceptable | 50% |
| Healthy range | 60–80% |
| Warning zone | Below 50% |

**Margin formula:** (Revenue - Direct Costs) / Revenue

**Direct costs include:** API costs, contractor time, infrastructure, third-party tools

---

## Pricing Rules

1. **Never quote below floor price.** Floor prices are minimums, not targets.
2. **Never share pricing via message.** Pricing is shared only in call or formal proposal.
3. **Pricing requires founder approval before sharing.** This is gate G03.
4. **Scope creep must trigger a change request.** Never absorb scope increase without adjustment.
5. **Payment terms: 50% upfront, 50% on delivery** (adjustable by founder for large deals).
6. **Retainer floor: 8,000 SAR/month.** No retainer below this amount.
7. **Discounting requires written justification.** Document why in the opportunity record.
8. **Estimated values are not verified.** Never represent estimated deal value as committed revenue.

## Pricing by Tier

| Company Tier | Pricing Approach |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Tier A (85+) | Quote at or above typical price. Strong fit = premium justified. |
| Tier B (70-84) | Start at typical price. Room to negotiate on scope, not floor. |
| Tier C (55-69) | Entry offer only. Qualify further before larger investment. |
| Tier D (<55) | Do not quote. Not qualified. |

---

## What to Do When Asked for Discount

1. Understand what's driving the ask (budget, comparison, or test?)
2. Offer scope reduction as alternative to price reduction
3. Never go below floor price
4. If discount is needed: document the business reason, get founder approval, and adjust scope accordingly
