# Weekly Intelligence Review

The single ritual that converts a week of operating data into structured learning. Written every Friday by the AI under founder review.

## Purpose
Close the learning loop. Every week the company runs experiments, hits friction, and produces data. Without a written review, that learning evaporates. This document forces the conversion of activity into doctrine.

## Owner
Sami (Founder) — accountable. AI learning agent — drafter.

## Review Cadence
Weekly, every Friday between 14:00 and 16:00 Riyadh, before the founder's Weekly CEO Review.

## Inputs
- The week's pipeline tracker deltas.
- The week's approval log.
- The week's friction log.
- The week's experiment log.
- The week's daily command briefs.

## Outputs
- A dated review file in `learning/weekly_intelligence_review.md` (private ops).
- A list of proposed doctrine updates.
- A list of proposed scorecard updates.
- A list of proposed offer-ladder updates.

## Rules
- Always written. Never skipped. Never compressed into bullet points alone.
- AI drafts. Founder edits and signs.
- Proposed updates are not applied automatically — they enter the decision queue for founder approval.
- Updates approved this week are applied this week, not deferred.

## Metrics
- Reviews written per quarter (target: 13/13).
- Doctrine updates produced per quarter.
- Experiments concluded per quarter.
- Friction items closed per quarter.

## Evidence
- Dated review file in `learning/weekly_intelligence_review.md` (private ops).
- Decision queue entries linked.
- Doctrine document version history.
- Friction log resolution entries.

## Template

> Week of YYYY-MM-DD

### What happened
The factual record of operating events this week. No interpretation yet.

### What worked
The actions, decisions, or experiments that produced revenue, delivery, trust, or learning. With evidence.

### What failed
The actions, decisions, or experiments that did not produce the expected outcome. With evidence and root cause where possible.

### What bottleneck
The single largest constraint on the company this week. Named, owned, and dated.

### What will change
Two or three concrete changes for next week. Each owned, dated, and verifiable.

### Proposed doctrine updates
- Update to ____ because ____ evidence shows ____.

### Proposed scorecard updates
- ____ moves from ____ to ____ because ____.

### Experiments concluded
- Experiment ____: result ____, decision ____.

### Friction closed
- Item ____: root cause ____, fix ____, evidence ____.
