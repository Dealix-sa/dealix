# Learning OS — Weekly Intelligence Review

The Weekly Intelligence Review is the AI-prepared pack the founder
reads before the Weekly CEO Review. It summarizes the week's signal
from every operating loop and forces the answer to five questions.

## Purpose
Turn the week's raw logs (pipeline, cash, delivery, trust, public
content) into a compressed intelligence pack that makes the Weekly
CEO Review fast and high-signal — and that produces exactly one
documented change for the next week.

## Owner
Sami (Founder). AI prepares; founder confirms.

## Review Cadence
Weekly. Draft generated by AI on Friday morning; reviewed inside the
Weekly CEO Review.

## Inputs
- The week's five Daily Command Briefs.
- Pipeline tracker delta for the week.
- Cash collected for the week.
- Deliveries shipped and signed off for the week.
- Approvals logged for the week (counts and risk distribution).
- Incidents logged in `trust/incident_log.md` for the week.
- Public content shipped and engagement signals.
- Customer-quoted feedback received.

## Outputs
- The completed review document (archived per week).
- The "One Change" for next week (committed in the Weekly CEO Review).
- Adjustments to the offer ladder, pipeline stages, or autonomy
  policy if structural issues surface.

## Rules
- The review must answer all five questions: What happened, What
  worked, What failed, What bottleneck, What will change.
- Every claim in the review must be backed by a log entry or an
  artifact link.
- "What will change" must be exactly one thing, measurable in seven
  days.
- A repeated bottleneck (same answer three weeks running) triggers a
  structural decision — not a fourth attempt at the same fix.
- The review is never skipped; if a week is unusual, the review
  still happens and notes the unusual conditions.

## Metrics
- Number of consecutive weeks with a completed review (target:
  unbroken streak).
- Number of "One Changes" that landed in the following week
  (target: ≥3 of 4 per month).
- Number of repeated bottlenecks promoted to structural decisions.
- Lead-time from incident → policy update (target: under 7 days).

## Evidence
- `weekly_reviews/YYYY-MM-DD.md` (private) — per-week archive.
- `learning/experiment_log.md` (private) — tested changes and outcomes.
- Scorecard delta in `DEALIX_COMPANY_OS_SCORECARD.md`.

## The Five Questions

The review answers exactly these five questions, in this order.
Each answer is concrete, evidenced, and short.

### 1. What happened this week
A factual summary of what moved. Numbers, not adjectives.

```
- Revenue: SAR X collected (+/- vs last week).
- Pipeline: N leads → N contacted → N replied → N proposals → N paid.
- Delivery: N engagements active, N deliveries shipped, N signed off.
- Trust: N approvals, N incidents, N near-misses.
- Content: N posts shipped, N engagement.
```

### 2. What worked
Specific actions that produced evidence this week. "Worked" means
a measurable outcome (a payment, a reply, a delivered artifact,
an approval, a customer quote), not "we did it".

```
- <action> produced <outcome> (<evidence link>).
- <action> produced <outcome> (<evidence link>).
```

### 3. What failed
Specific actions that did not produce evidence, or produced bad
evidence. Be willing to name failures of the founder, not only of
the system. "Failed" is not catastrophic; it's input.

```
- <action> did not produce <expected outcome> because <reason>.
- <action> produced <bad outcome> because <reason>.
```

### 4. What bottleneck
The single biggest constraint this week. If we removed exactly one
thing, what would the week look like? Name the upstream cause,
not the surface symptom.

```
- Bottleneck: <one thing>.
- Evidence: <link>.
- Is this the same bottleneck as last week? Yes/No.
- Is this the same bottleneck three weeks running? If yes, what is
  the structural decision required?
```

### 5. What will change next week
Exactly one change. Measurable in seven days. Owned by name.

```
One Change: <concrete action>.
Owner: Sami.
Measure of success: <observable signal by end of next week>.
Rollback trigger: <what we'll observe if this change is wrong>.
```

## Failure Modes To Watch
- "What worked" filled with activity instead of outcomes → reject
  and rewrite.
- "What failed" left blank when failures clearly happened → reset
  honesty discipline.
- Bottleneck recycled week after week with no structural decision →
  the review is theatrical; promote to A3 decision.
- "One Change" spawning a list → cut to one; record the rest in the
  decision queue.
