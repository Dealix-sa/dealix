# Sales Scripts

> DEALIX — INTELLIGENT DEALS. REAL GROWTH.
> Discovery, demo, and qualification scripts for the Dealix ladder.
> Built on Trust. Driven by Growth. Closing Deals.

Sales scripts at Dealix are not closing scripts. They are evidence
scripts. The goal of a sales conversation is to surface enough
evidence to determine whether the engagement should happen, what
rung it should happen at, and what refusals must be put in writing
before any commitment.

## Operating Principles

- No script promises revenue, sales, or meetings. Outcomes are
  described as evidence, decisions, queues, and drafts.
- No script commits pricing. Bands are referenced; commitments
  require founder approval and are written in the offer document.
- No script collects PII beyond what the buyer explicitly agrees to
  share.
- Every conversation is logged in the Founder Console with the
  agreed next step. Verbal commitments without a written next step
  are not commitments.

## Conversation Skeleton

Every Dealix sales conversation, regardless of channel or rung,
follows the same skeleton.

1. **Anchor** — restate why the conversation exists (the buyer's
   inbound trigger, the founder's referral, or the diagnostic
   outcome).
2. **Evidence** — establish what the buyer already has (pipeline
   shape, current revenue motion, current tooling, current
   evidence of traction).
3. **Refusal contract** — surface, early, what Dealix will not do.
   This filters buyers whose expectations cannot be met.
4. **Rung match** — identify which rung is the right starting
   point. If the right rung is "none today", the conversation ends
   honestly.
5. **Next step** — a named artefact (Diagnostic Engagement Note,
   Sprint Scope Letter draft, Pilot Charter draft) and a date.

## Discovery — R1 Diagnostic

Use when an inbound has not yet been qualified for a paid offer.

Opening:

> "Thanks for the time. Before we go anywhere, two things. First,
> the Dealix Diagnostic is free and evidence-only — we do not
> publish anything about you and we do not collect data you have
> not shared. Second, I will end this call by telling you whether
> we are the right fit, even if the answer is no."

Questions:

- What does your revenue motion look like today? Pipeline shape,
  average cycle, where deals stall.
- What does "growth" mean to you in the next two quarters?
- Where is your evidence today? What can a buyer see about your
  traction without asking you?
- What have you already tried that did not work? Be specific.
- What is the smallest, defensible move you have not tried yet?

Close:

> "Here is what we will do with what you have told me: produce a
> diagnostic brief and a one-page scorecard, plus a refusal list —
> the things we would not do on your account. I will return that
> within seven business days. We will not contact you outside that
> deliverable."

## Discovery — R2 Sprint

Use after the R1 Diagnostic has been delivered and the buyer has
asked to go further.

Opening:

> "We read the Diagnostic. The Sprint is a 3–4 week engagement to
> turn that diagnostic into a working motion. Nothing leaves your
> hands without your approval. Before we scope, let me check a few
> things."

Qualification questions:

- Who, on your side, will sign off on outreach drafts? The Sprint
  produces queues; we need a named approver.
- What data can we read? What data is off-limits?
- Is there anyone we should suppress before we start? Lost deals,
  conflicts, sensitive sectors.
- Do you have a defensible ICP today, or do we need to build it?
- Are you expecting "more meetings" as the success criterion? If
  yes, let us recalibrate together; the Sprint produces a working
  motion, not a meeting count.

Refusal surface:

> "Two things we will not do. We will not execute external sends
> for you — we draft, you approve, your team sends. And we will
> not publish anything about you without recorded approval."

Close:

> "If the Sprint is the right move, the next step is a Scope Letter
> with the deliverables named and the pricing band. The pricing
> stays in band; any deviation needs founder approval and is
> written into the letter."

## Discovery — R3 Pilot

Use after a Sprint outcome or when the buyer has a repeatable
motion that needs instrumentation.

Opening:

> "The Pilot is 6–10 weeks, with a charter, an eval gate, and a
> weekly evidence pack. Either side can exit on any weekly review.
> Before we draft the charter, three questions."

Qualification questions:

- What slice of the business should the Pilot cover? Sectors,
  channels, account list.
- Who, on your side, joins the weekly review?
- What would make you pause the Pilot mid-way? Write down two or
  three kill conditions; we will write them into the charter.

Refusal surface:

> "The Pilot does not work without a charter. We do not run pilots
> whose only success criterion is more meetings. We do not run
> pilots without the eval gate active from day one."

Close:

> "The next step is a Pilot Charter draft. The charter takes about
> a week to prepare. Pricing stays in band — the mid-point payment
> is conditional on a green eval gate, which we will explain in
> the charter."

## Discovery — R4 Retainer

Use after a Pilot has closed and the buyer wants steady-state
operations.

Opening:

> "The Retainer is monthly. Weekly ops, monthly executive review,
> quarterly trust audit. Three things before we sign."

Qualification questions:

- What data scope do we get to work with going forward? This is
  written into the SLA.
- Who owns the kill switch on your side? Anyone with the role can
  pause an agent at any time, without a meeting.
- What is your renewal posture? 30-day cancellation notice; we
  file a renewal memo 60 days before each renewal.

Refusal surface:

> "The Retainer requires a written data scope. We do not run a
> retainer without one. And we do not extend payment terms beyond
> NET-15 without founder approval."

Close:

> "Next step is a Retainer SLA. We can start within two weeks of
> signature."

## Discovery — R5 Console

Use when the buyer wants to operate their own revenue motion in the
Dealix Founder Console.

Opening:

> "The Founder Console is a licensed seat in the same surface we
> use internally. Your policy, your eval gate, your audit ledger.
> Before we provision, two readiness checks."

Qualification questions:

- What policy do you want mirrored? We start with the Dealix
  default; you can override per rule.
- Who, on your side, owns the kill switch? It cannot be a vendor.
- What is your PDPL posture today? Data residency, retention,
  sub-processor disclosure.

Refusal surface:

> "We do not provision a Console seat with the eval gate disabled.
> We do not enable A3 autonomous external action — ever. If those
> are deal-breakers, this is not the right rung."

Close:

> "Next step is a Console Acceptance document with your policy
> mirror and seat list."

## Discovery — R6 Enterprise

Use when the buyer is a multi-BU enterprise.

Opening:

> "The Enterprise OS is a multi-BU deployment with SSO, audit
> export, sector overlays, and a security review baked into
> delivery. Before we scope, we need to do a security pre-read."

Qualification questions:

- Which BUs are in scope?
- Which sector overlays do you need? Which do you not need?
- Who, on your side, leads the security review?
- What is your data residency requirement?

Refusal surface:

> "We do not ship without an enterprise security review complete.
> We do not bypass tenant isolation. We do not enable an overlay
> we cannot defend."

Close:

> "Next step is a security pre-read pack and an architecture
> briefing."

## Discovery — R7 Partner

Use when the conversation is with a consultancy, integrator, or
sector body.

Opening:

> "Our partnership operates inside the Dealix trust contract. Your
> brand can front the engagement; the policy, the eval gate, and
> the audit ledger remain Dealix-grade. Three things before we
> draft."

Qualification questions:

- What is your delivery posture today? Who delivers?
- What brand carve-out do you need?
- What revenue share do you propose?

Refusal surface:

> "We do not allow proof publication, pricing commitment, or A3
> automation outside the partner agreement. Brand carve-out is
> written, not verbal."

Close:

> "Next step is a partner agreement draft. Eval gate certification
> of your delivery team is part of onboarding."

## Demo Scripts

A Dealix demo is a teardown, not a product tour. The script:

1. **Set context** — 30 seconds. "This is the Founder Console. The
   buyer for this demo is you. We will not click anything that
   would touch a real external system."
2. **Show the queue** — 5 minutes. Walk through a queued outreach
   draft. Highlight `claims_flags`, `brand_voice_flags`,
   `approval_state`. Show the policy adapter refusing a draft
   targeting a suppressed identity.
3. **Show the audit** — 3 minutes. Walk through the trust ledger
   entries. Show how an approval is recorded and how an attempted
   external send without approval is denied with a rule id.
4. **Show the eval gate** — 3 minutes. Show a green run and a
   regression. Explain the kill switch.
5. **Show the close memo** — 2 minutes. Walk through the artefact
   the buyer would get at the end of a Sprint or Pilot.

Demos do not include guaranteed-outcome language. They do not
include unapproved customer logos. They do not include screenshots
of unredacted buyer data.

## Qualification Questions Library

A short, recyclable set of questions that show up across rungs.

- "What does evidence look like for you today?"
- "Where do you currently lose deals — the wrong reason, not the
  comfortable reason?"
- "Who, on your side, approves an outbound message before it
  leaves your domain?"
- "What suppression list do we need to honour from day one?"
- "What is the smallest reversible move you have not tried?"
- "What would make you exit this engagement on a Monday morning?"
- "What does your PDPL posture look like today?"
- "What is your relationship with proof — what can be published,
  what cannot?"

## Disqualification Patterns

Dealix declines the engagement when:

- The buyer insists on guaranteed pipeline or meetings.
- The buyer demands external send automation.
- The buyer refuses to write the data scope.
- The buyer wants the eval gate disabled.
- The buyer wants to enable A3 autonomous external action.
- The buyer's sector is on the founder-set refusal list.
- The buyer's economics do not justify the floor of the relevant
  rung.

Disqualification is delivered honestly, with the rule cited.

## Logging

Every conversation is logged in the Founder Console with:

- Date, channel, participants.
- Rung discussed.
- Refusals surfaced.
- Pricing band referenced (never a committed number).
- Next step (artefact + date).
- Trust ledger reference id.

## What Sales Scripts Are Not

- They are not closing pressure.
- They are not "trial closes" hidden as questions.
- They are not scripts to be read verbatim. They are scaffolding
  for a sober conversation that ends with an honest next step.

Sales at Dealix is the first delivery. A buyer who experiences a
clean, refusal-aware, evidence-first sales conversation has already
seen the operating system at work.
