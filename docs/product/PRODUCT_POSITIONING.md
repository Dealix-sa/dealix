# Product Positioning

Dealix is positioned as a Saudi B2B Revenue Operating System. It is not a CRM, not an outreach tool, not a chat product. The positioning anchors how every offer, page, and proposal is written.

**Source of truth:** `docs/00_foundation/DEALIX_POSITIONING.md` (canonical) extended here
**Owner:** Founder
**Trust gate:** A2 — positioning changes require founder approval and review against `docs/01_category/`.

## One-sentence

Dealix is the trust-gated Revenue Operating System for Saudi B2B teams who want intelligent deal flow without losing control of customer trust.

## What it is

A connected factory: signal capture, qualification, sample, proposal, close, invoice, cash. Plus the trust plane that ensures no external action happens without founder approval, no metric is published as fact without verification, and no customer data leaves without consent.

## What it is not

| Confused with | How Dealix differs |
|---------------|--------------------|
| CRM | A CRM stores records. Dealix runs the factory and gates external action. |
| Outreach tool | An outreach tool sends messages. Dealix never sends external messages without explicit founder approval. |
| AI agent platform | A platform is a workbench. Dealix is a product with opinionated workflow and trust gates. |
| Consultancy | A consultancy is people-only. Dealix is an OS with humans in the loop. |

## Audience

The buyer is a Saudi B2B founder or revenue leader running an organisation between SAR 5M and SAR 200M in annual revenue. They are growth-minded but trust-sensitive. They have been burned by cold automation. They run sales by relationship and need a system that respects that.

## Promise

Dealix produces evidenced opportunities through a Revenue Factory whose every station is gated and audited. We do not promise revenue; we promise a factory that the founder can run, audit, and trust.

## Anti-positioning

We are explicit about what we refuse (`docs/00_foundation/WHAT_DEALIX_REFUSES.md`):

- No cold WhatsApp automation.
- No scraped lists.
- No LinkedIn automation.
- No bulk outreach.
- No guaranteed revenue claims.
- No A3 autonomous external action.

This anti-positioning is not a marketing posture; it is enforced by policy as code in `policies/dealix_control_policy.yaml`.

## Failure modes

- **Drift to "AI productivity":** copy slides toward generic AI productivity claims. Detection: monthly copy audit. Recovery: rewrite against `docs/marketing/COPYWRITING_RULES.md`.
- **Drift to "outreach tool":** an offer description implies bulk send. Detection: proposal review. Recovery: rewrite; surface to Brand Guardian.
- **Drift to "no humans":** copy implies full automation. Detection: copy audit. Recovery: re-anchor on trust gates.

## Recovery path

If positioning drifts in multiple artifacts, the founder runs a positioning re-grounding session and re-publishes `docs/00_foundation/DEALIX_POSITIONING.md` as the canonical reference.

## Metrics

- Positioning audit score by quarter (qualitative).
- Confused-message incidents per month (when a prospect describes us as something we are not).
