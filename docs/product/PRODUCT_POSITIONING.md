# Product Positioning

> DEALIX — INTELLIGENT DEALS. REAL GROWTH.
> Per-offer positioning for the seven-rung ladder.
> Saudi B2B Revenue Operating System.

This document gives Dealix a single, sober positioning statement for
each offer on the ladder. Positioning is not a tagline. It is a
contract about who the offer is for, what it does, what it refuses to
do, and how it differs from the alternatives a buyer might consider.

## Master Positioning Statement

For Saudi B2B founders and enterprise revenue leaders who are tired of
agency promises and feature-rich tools that do not move the deal, the
Dealix Revenue Operating System is a trust-gated stack of agents,
playbooks, and reviews that turns existing pipeline into evidence-based
decisions. Unlike outsourced SDR shops and generic CRM add-ons, Dealix
refuses to send anything externally without explicit founder approval,
refuses to publish proof without recorded consent, and refuses to
promise revenue. The pillars are: Built on Trust. Driven by Growth.
Closing Deals. Focused on Results. Global Mindset, Local Impact.

## Per-Offer Positioning

### R1 — Free Sample / Diagnostic

- **Who it is for:** Saudi founders and revenue leaders evaluating
  whether Dealix is worth a paid conversation.
- **What it is:** a short, evidence-only diagnostic of the buyer's
  current revenue posture. No PII collection beyond what the buyer
  chooses to share.
- **What it is not:** a sales pitch, a benchmark report, or a free
  trial of software.
- **What it refuses:** to promise meetings or pipeline; to publish
  anything about the buyer; to collect data the buyer did not
  authorise.
- **Differentiator vs. alternatives:** consultancy "free audits"
  often function as long-form pitches. The Dealix Diagnostic ends
  with a refusal list — the things Dealix will not do on this
  account — which is rare in the category.

### R2 — Revenue Sprint

- **Who it is for:** SMB and mid-market companies with an active but
  inconsistent revenue motion.
- **What it is:** a 3–4 week engagement that converts the diagnostic
  into a working revenue motion (ICP, scoring, queues, content,
  trust posture upgrade).
- **What it is not:** an outsourced SDR team, an agency campaign, or
  a do-it-for-you content shop.
- **What it refuses:** external send automation; guaranteed-outcome
  wording in any artefact; proof publication without approval.
- **Differentiator vs. alternatives:** agency engagements usually
  produce assets and dashboards. The Sprint produces a working loop:
  drafts, queues, reviews, evals. The buyer's team can run it on
  Monday morning.

### R3 — Managed Pilot

- **Who it is for:** mid-market companies with a repeatable motion
  that is under-instrumented.
- **What it is:** a 6–10 week pilot where Dealix operates the
  Revenue Sprint loop alongside the buyer's team with weekly
  review evidence.
- **What it is not:** a managed service contract, an open-ended
  consulting retainer, or an AI experiment.
- **What it refuses:** pilots without a charter; pilots without an
  eval gate; pilots whose only success criterion is "more meetings".
- **Differentiator vs. alternatives:** most managed services blur
  the line between buyer and vendor. The pilot enforces a charter,
  a kill switch, and a weekly evidence pack. The buyer can exit
  cleanly on any review.

### R4 — Revenue Desk Retainer

- **Who it is for:** founder-led revenue organisations with fewer
  than 30 reps that prefer an embedded operator over hiring a Head
  of Revenue Ops.
- **What it is:** a monthly retainer that operates the buyer's
  revenue loop in a steady cadence (weekly ops, monthly review,
  quarterly trust audit).
- **What it is not:** a fractional executive arrangement, an SDR
  outsource, or a tool subscription.
- **What it refuses:** retainers without a signed data scope; any
  request to execute external action automatically; retainers that
  bypass the eval gate.
- **Differentiator vs. alternatives:** fractional revenue operators
  produce reports; agencies produce campaigns. The Retainer keeps
  the operating loop alive and writes the audit trail that lets the
  buyer trust what is happening on their behalf.

### R5 — Founder Console

- **Who it is for:** founders and CEOs who want to operate their own
  revenue motion with AI assist rather than hand it off.
- **What it is:** a licensed seat in the Dealix Founder Console —
  the same surface Dealix uses internally — with policy-as-code
  mirroring the buyer's own rules.
- **What it is not:** a CRM, a marketing automation platform, or a
  generic AI assistant.
- **What it refuses:** to disable the eval gate; to enable A3
  autonomous external action; to operate without a tenant policy.
- **Differentiator vs. alternatives:** CRMs store records. Marketing
  platforms execute campaigns. Generic AI assistants produce drafts
  with no governance. The Founder Console binds the agents, the
  policy, the eval gate, and the audit ledger together so the
  founder can make decisions that hold up under scrutiny.

### R6 — Enterprise Revenue Intelligence OS

- **Who it is for:** Saudi and GCC enterprises with multiple
  revenue-bearing business units and regulated data.
- **What it is:** a multi-business-unit deployment of the Founder
  Console with enterprise-grade controls (SSO, audit export, sector
  overlays, sandboxed integrations).
- **What it is not:** a CRM replacement, a marketing cloud, or an
  enterprise AI platform sold by the seat.
- **What it refuses:** to ship without an enterprise security
  review; to bypass tenant isolation; to enable sector overlays for
  which Dealix has no capability.
- **Differentiator vs. alternatives:** enterprise AI platforms tend
  to be general-purpose. The Enterprise OS is specific: revenue
  intelligence with PDPL-aware controls and Saudi sector overlays.

### R7 — Partner / White-label Revenue OS

- **Who it is for:** consultancies, integrators, and sector bodies
  that want a Dealix-grade operating layer under their own brand or
  co-brand.
- **What it is:** a bespoke licensing arrangement with brand
  carve-out, policy mirror, eval gate certification, and a written
  revenue share.
- **What it is not:** a reseller program, a referral scheme, or an
  open-source release.
- **What it refuses:** partners who attempt to publish proof,
  commit pricing, or enable A3 automation outside the partner
  agreement.
- **Differentiator vs. alternatives:** white-label SaaS programs
  trade on logo. The Dealix partnership trades on policy: the
  partner agrees to operate inside the Dealix trust contract.

## Positioning Statements (Short Forms)

For sales conversations, the short forms are deliberate, sober, and
free of guarantees.

- **R1:** "A free, evidence-only diagnostic. It ends with a list of
  what we will not do on your account."
- **R2:** "A 3–4 week sprint that turns your diagnostic into a
  working revenue motion. Nothing leaves your hands without your
  approval."
- **R3:** "A 6–10 week pilot. Charter, eval gate, weekly evidence.
  You can exit on any review."
- **R4:** "An embedded revenue operator delivered monthly. Audit
  trail, trust audit, no external automation."
- **R5:** "A founder console with the same governance we use
  internally. Your policy, your eval gate, your audit ledger."
- **R6:** "An enterprise deployment with SSO, audit export, sector
  overlays, and a security review baked into delivery."
- **R7:** "A partner arrangement where you front the brand and we
  retain the trust contract."

## Anti-Positioning

It is as important to say what Dealix is not.

- Dealix is not a tool. The tools serve the operating system.
- Dealix is not an agency. Agencies execute; Dealix operates the
  loop and keeps the founder in the decision chair.
- Dealix is not a consultancy. Consultancies produce reports; the
  Revenue OS produces working artefacts and queues.
- Dealix is not a benchmarking firm. Benchmarks decorate slides;
  Dealix produces the next decision.
- Dealix is not a generalist AI platform. Dealix is specifically a
  Saudi B2B Revenue Operating System with PDPL-aware controls.

## Local Anchoring

The "Global Mindset, Local Impact" pillar shows up in positioning in
specific ways:

- All offers are PDPL-aware by default. Data residency and retention
  windows are documented per rung.
- Sector overlays cover Saudi-specific patterns (regulated sectors,
  Vision 2030 priorities, sovereign-cloud readiness).
- Arabic-language content quality is a first-class brand-voice
  requirement, not a translation afterthought.
- Founder-led content references the Saudi B2B environment honestly,
  including its constraints.

## How Positioning Travels Through Distribution

Every channel, every draft, and every meeting note in the Founder
Console must trace back to one of the positioning statements above.
The Brand Guardian agent scans drafts and flags anything that drifts
from the master statement or the per-offer statement. Drift is not
the same as iteration; iteration changes the wording but preserves
the contract. Drift breaks the contract.

## Review Cadence

Positioning is reviewed quarterly. Reviews are recorded in
`product/positioning_review.md`. A review either reaffirms the
positioning, refines wording, or — rarely — opens a structural change
which requires founder sign-off, an updated Brand Guardian prompt,
and an updated claims-safety eval.
