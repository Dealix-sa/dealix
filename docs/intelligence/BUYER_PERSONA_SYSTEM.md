# Buyer Persona System

**Owner:** Strategy Office + Founder
**Source of truth:** This doc + `docs/intelligence/ICP_SEGMENTATION_SYSTEM.md`

## Purpose

Inside each ICP, Dealix engages a specific buyer role. The buyer persona system names that role, its decision logic, its pain language, and the channels it actually uses. Without persona discipline, outreach drafts read as generic and account scoring weights wrong signals.

## Active personas

Dealix maintains three primary buyer personas at launch stage.

### Persona 1 — The Founder

**Profile.** Operates a Saudi B2B services company, 5-50 FTE. Owns revenue. Closed the last three deals personally. Reads every outbound message before it ships.

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Role title | Founder / CEO / Managing Partner |
| Team relationship | 0-3 commercial direct reports |
| Decision speed | Days to weeks (depends on cash impact) |
| Budget authority | Full |
| Sign-off pattern | Personal sign-off; rarely procurement |
| Pain language | "Pipeline is empty mid-month." "I'm doing all the deals." "Drafts pile up, none go out." |
| Buying trigger | Recent missed quarter, recent hire, recent funding, recent agency disappointment |
| Channel | Direct LinkedIn message, founder-to-founder intro, sector-event introduction |
| Off-channel | WhatsApp cold, mass email, paid display |
| Trust signals | Named sprint catalog, dated Proof Pack, explicit exclusions |
| Disqualifiers | "Just send me the price," "guarantee me X meetings" |

Default first offer: Lead Intelligence Sprint or Diagnostic.

### Persona 2 — The Head of Sales

**Profile.** Saudi B2B firm, 50-200 FTE. Accountable for pipeline and revenue. Manages 3-10 sellers. Has tried Salesloft or HubSpot. Has been pitched twenty AI tools this year.

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Role title | Head of Sales / Sales Director / Commercial Director |
| Team relationship | 3-10 sellers, often 1-2 SDRs |
| Decision speed | Weeks to months (founder sign-off on contract) |
| Budget authority | Partial — recommends, founder approves |
| Sign-off pattern | Founder sign-off on contract; Head of Sales owns the engagement |
| Pain language | "We have a CRM but pipeline still leaks." "Outbound is noisy but flat." "Sellers are not following up." |
| Buying trigger | Quarterly pipeline review showing flat coverage, new product launch, new market entry |
| Channel | LinkedIn message (warm), sector forum, peer referral |
| Off-channel | Cold call, paid display, WhatsApp |
| Trust signals | Named sprint outputs, integration with existing CRM, gate architecture |
| Disqualifiers | "We need a custom tool built from scratch," "we want to white-label this" |

Default first offer: Sector Map Sprint or Trigger Activation Sprint.

### Persona 3 — The Head of GTM (regional SaaS expanding into KSA)

**Profile.** Regional or international B2B SaaS company, expanding into Saudi. Reports to a CRO or CEO outside KSA. Needs KSA-specific revenue infrastructure fast.

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Role title | Head of GTM / VP Sales / Regional VP |
| Team relationship | 0-5 KSA-based commercial staff (often hiring) |
| Decision speed | Months (regional + KSA sign-off) |
| Budget authority | Partial — recommends, CRO/CEO approves |
| Sign-off pattern | Regional CRO + KSA Country Manager |
| Pain language | "Our global playbook does not work in KSA." "We need local proof to convince our board." "Hiring KSA sales is slow; we need leverage now." |
| Buying trigger | Annual planning, KSA office opening, regional sales miss, board pressure for KSA traction |
| Channel | LinkedIn (cold-warm), industry analyst introduction, partner referral |
| Off-channel | WhatsApp, paid display, mass cold email |
| Trust signals | KSA Sector Scorecard, PDPL governance posture, partner network depth |
| Disqualifiers | "We just need lead lists," "we want offshore sales reps" |

Default first offer: KSA Entry Sprint or Sector Scorecard Sprint.

## Persona-to-sprint matrix

| Persona | First sprint | Second sprint | Retainer trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Founder | Lead Intelligence | AI Quick Win | After 2 sprints + 1 Proof Pack |
| Head of Sales | Sector Map | Trigger Activation | After 1 sprint + visible pipeline lift |
| Head of GTM | KSA Entry | Partner Activation | After 1 sprint + 1 sourced deal |

## Persona maintenance

- Monthly: review messages that produced replies vs no replies. Update pain language.
- Quarterly: confirm trigger event mappings against actual closed-won evidence.
- Annually: review whether to add or retire a persona.

## What persona discipline forbids

- Generic "decision maker" targeting.
- Reusing one outbound draft across three personas with no rewrite.
- Putting all three personas on the same content cadence.
- Inventing a fourth persona to justify chasing a tempting account.

## Trust gate

| Action | Approval class |
|---|---|
| Persona language update | A1 — Strategy Office |
| Add or retire persona | A2 — Founder + Strategy Office |

## Failure mode

- Outreach draft uses Founder pain language sent to a Head of Sales (or vice versa). Conversion collapses.
- Persona definitions are aspirational, not evidence-backed.
- A "VP Marketing" persona creeps in without sprint-fit evidence.

## Recovery path

1. Re-anchor outreach drafts to the persona-specific pain language.
2. Re-evidence each persona from the last 10 closed-won and closed-lost engagements.
3. Retire any persona that has not produced a closed sprint in two quarters.

## Disclaimer

Personas are directional, not predictive. Dealix does not guarantee that any named persona will respond, engage, or convert. Estimated value is not Verified value / القيمة التقديرية ليست قيمة مُتحقَّقة.
