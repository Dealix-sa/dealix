# Saudi B2B Market Map

**Owner:** Strategy Office + Founder
**Source of truth:** This doc + `docs/intelligence/SECTOR_RANKING_SYSTEM.md` + `docs/02_saudi_positioning/SAUDI_SECTOR_TAXONOMY.md`

## Purpose

This is Dealix's working map of Saudi B2B sectors. It defines the sectors Dealix considers in scope, the working hypothesis for each, and the current tier (refreshed quarterly via the Sector Ranking System).

The map is not a market research report. It is an operating instrument that tells operators which sectors to source accounts from this week.

## Sectors in scope

### Cybersecurity B2B

- **Buyers:** Saudi cybersecurity vendors, MSSPs, security consultancies.
- **Pain pattern:** Long enterprise sales cycles; small inside-sales teams; need for sector-credentialed outreach.
- **Sprint fit:** Sector Map Sprint, Trigger Activation Sprint.
- **Trigger families:** Funding rounds, new HoS hires, new product launches, sector regulator decisions.
- **Default tier:** A.
- **Saudi regulators of note:** NCA (National Cybersecurity Authority), SDAIA.

### ERP/CRM consulting and implementation

- **Buyers:** Implementation partners for Oracle, SAP, Microsoft, Salesforce, Zoho, Odoo in KSA.
- **Pain pattern:** Project-based revenue; pipeline gap between projects; need for repeatable Sprint-shaped offers.
- **Sprint fit:** Lead Intelligence Sprint, Sector Map Sprint.
- **Trigger families:** Vendor partner-tier promotions, new enterprise customer wins, new geo expansion.
- **Default tier:** A.

### B2B agencies (marketing, growth, content)

- **Buyers:** Saudi B2B agencies, growth shops, content agencies serving B2B clients.
- **Pain pattern:** Their own pipeline is leaky while they sell pipeline services; productization gap.
- **Sprint fit:** Lead Intelligence Sprint, AI Quick Win Sprint, Partner Activation Sprint.
- **Trigger families:** New offering launch, new sector vertical, new hire.
- **Default tier:** A.

### Enterprise logistics

- **Buyers:** B2B logistics providers, freight forwarders, supply-chain SaaS, last-mile B2B.
- **Pain pattern:** High concentration on a few enterprise customers; diversification need; long enterprise procurement cycles.
- **Sprint fit:** Sector Map Sprint, Trigger Activation Sprint.
- **Trigger families:** New corridor opening, Vision 2030 contract wins, M&A.
- **Default tier:** B.

### Boutique consulting (management, ops, sector-specialist)

- **Buyers:** Saudi boutique consultancies (under 50 FTE) selling into mid-market and enterprise.
- **Pain pattern:** Partner-led sales; no repeatable inbound; founder time-tax on revenue.
- **Sprint fit:** Lead Intelligence Sprint, AI Quick Win Sprint.
- **Trigger families:** New partner addition, sector entry, public RFP.
- **Default tier:** B.

### B2B SaaS (regional and Saudi-native)

- **Buyers:** Regional SaaS expanding into KSA; Saudi-native B2B SaaS startups.
- **Pain pattern:** Generic playbooks fail in KSA; need for local sector intelligence and KSA-specific motion.
- **Sprint fit:** KSA Entry Sprint, Sector Map Sprint, Partner Activation Sprint.
- **Trigger families:** Funding, KSA office opening, KSA Country Manager hire.
- **Default tier:** A.

### Enterprise services (BPO, managed services, professional services at scale)

- **Buyers:** Saudi BPOs, managed services providers, large professional services firms.
- **Pain pattern:** Pipeline coverage is uneven by service line; cross-sell motion underdeveloped.
- **Sprint fit:** Sector Map Sprint, Trigger Activation Sprint.
- **Trigger families:** New service line launch, M&A, leadership change.
- **Default tier:** B.

### High-ticket B2B (catch-all for niches not above)

- **Buyers:** Niche B2B vendors with deal sizes above SAR 80,000 not captured by the above sectors.
- **Pain pattern:** Sector-specific; varies.
- **Sprint fit:** Diagnostic first, then sector-specific sprint.
- **Trigger families:** Sector-specific.
- **Default tier:** C.

## Sectors explicitly out of scope

- B2C (any consumer-facing business).
- Healthcare with patient-data dependence (PDPL clinical-data tier requires specialist counsel; outside launch scope).
- Government direct procurement (procurement cycle incompatible with sprint velocity at launch).
- Gambling, adult, weapons, or sanctioned industries.
- Crypto trading platforms.

Healthcare-adjacent B2B (e.g., clinic operations software vendors selling to clinic owners) may be considered after the launch period — see `docs/growth/GROWTH_MACHINE.md`.

## Map maintenance

- Each sector entry is reviewed quarterly via the Sector Ranking System.
- Tier movements are logged in the Strategy Office register.
- Sector entries with no closed sprint for two consecutive quarters drop one tier; with four consecutive quarters, exit the map.

## How operators use this map

1. Pick a sector from the A or B tier list.
2. Pull the ICP definition for that sector from `ICP_SEGMENTATION_SYSTEM.md`.
3. Pull the buyer persona for that sector from `BUYER_PERSONA_SYSTEM.md`.
4. Run account scoring on candidate accounts.
5. Route through the Offer-Channel Fit matrix.

## Failure mode

- The map fossilizes; new sectors of interest are not added.
- A sector with no Proof Pack sits at Tier A for a year.
- Public scorecards drift into customer-identifying detail.

## Recovery path

1. Re-run quarterly review.
2. Force tier drop for stale entries.
3. Re-anonymize any leaky public scorecard.
