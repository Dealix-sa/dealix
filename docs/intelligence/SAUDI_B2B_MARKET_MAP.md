# Saudi B2B Market Map

This document is the working map of the Saudi B2B sectors Dealix
operates in or plans to operate in. It is intentionally narrow — we map
sectors we can actually serve with a credible offer and a delivery path
within 30 days. Anything outside is parked.

Wordmark: DEALIX. Tagline: INTELLIGENT DEALS. REAL GROWTH.
Positioning: Saudi B2B Revenue Operating System.

## 1. How this map is used

The map feeds the Sector Ranking System. Each sector below is scored on
the 11-dimension rubric defined in `SECTOR_RANKING_SYSTEM.md`. Sectors
above threshold enter the active operating list; the rest are watchlisted
or parked. The map is reviewed monthly.

## 2. Sector list

### 2.1 ERP and CRM implementers

- Profile: System integrators and consultancies that implement ERP
  (Odoo, Microsoft Dynamics, Oracle) and CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho)
  for Saudi mid-market and enterprise.
- Buyer: Founder/CEO, Head of Sales, Partner Manager.
- Pain anchors: Pipeline pressure, repeatable client acquisition,
  productisation, partner ecosystem coverage.
- Dealix fit: Strong. Implementers buy revenue capability they can
  resell or use internally.
- Trust risk: Low to medium; depends on client portfolio sensitivity.
- Notes: Partner potential is high.

### 2.2 Cybersecurity vendors and services

- Profile: Local and international cybersecurity vendors, MSSPs,
  consulting firms serving Saudi enterprise.
- Buyer: CRO, COO, Head of Strategy.
- Pain anchors: Long sales cycles, technical-to-business translation,
  enterprise procurement.
- Dealix fit: Strong on outbound-to-named-buyer; trust posture is a
  natural match.
- Trust risk: Medium; data residency and reference confidentiality
  matter.
- Notes: Proof artefacts must be bilingual and audit-friendly.

### 2.3 B2B agencies

- Profile: Outbound, content, brand, growth, and SDR-as-a-service
  agencies operating in KSA and broader GCC.
- Buyer: Founder, Head of Growth.
- Pain anchors: Margin compression, retention, productising service.
- Dealix fit: Strong, but with care — agencies can be customers AND
  partners. Engagement is segmented.
- Trust risk: Low.
- Notes: Partner potential is the highest in this map.

### 2.4 Logistics and industrial services

- Profile: 3PL, last-mile, industrial maintenance, equipment services,
  technical staffing firms.
- Buyer: COO, Head of Sales.
- Pain anchors: Account expansion, RFP win rate, partner alignment.
- Dealix fit: Medium to strong; buying clarity is high.
- Trust risk: Low.
- Notes: Bilingual operating reality is mandatory.

### 2.5 Consulting firms

- Profile: Management consulting, transformation consulting, sector-
  specialist consultancies.
- Buyer: Partner, Director, Practice Lead.
- Pain anchors: Project pipeline, productising practice, expansion to
  new sectors.
- Dealix fit: Strong on retainer-conversion offers; partner potential is
  high.
- Trust risk: Medium; client name exposure must be controlled.
- Notes: Proof artefacts must respect client confidentiality by design.

### 2.6 SaaS / B2B software (KSA-active)

- Profile: SaaS vendors serving Saudi B2B, both local and international
  entrants.
- Buyer: CRO, Head of Marketing, Country Manager.
- Pain anchors: Saudi go-to-market entry, pipeline coverage, local
  trust building.
- Dealix fit: Strong; aligned with the RevOS positioning.
- Trust risk: Low to medium.
- Notes: Expansion-to-KSA triggers are common here.

### 2.7 Enterprise services (HR, finance, legal-tech)

- Profile: HR tech, finance and accounting services, legal-tech and
  compliance vendors operating in KSA.
- Buyer: CEO, COO, Head of Sales.
- Pain anchors: Long cycles, compliance alignment, channel partner
  coverage.
- Dealix fit: Medium to strong; PDPL and ZATCA alignment is a wedge.
- Trust risk: Medium.
- Notes: Bilingual content is non-optional.

### 2.8 EdTech and corporate training (B2B)

- Profile: Corporate training providers, technical academies, B2B
  learning platforms operating in KSA.
- Buyer: Head of L&D, CEO, Partnerships Lead.
- Pain anchors: Enterprise sales motion, partner ecosystem, repeat
  business.
- Dealix fit: Medium.
- Trust risk: Low.
- Notes: Often partner-friendly.

### 2.9 Healthcare and clinical services (B2B side)

- Profile: B2B suppliers to clinics, hospitals, medical groups.
- Buyer: CEO, Head of Sales, Partnerships.
- Pain anchors: Procurement cycles, vendor master, trust references.
- Dealix fit: Medium; entry requires sensitive trust posture.
- Trust risk: High.
- Notes: Distress triggers are handled with extra sensitivity.

### 2.10 Real-estate technology and B2B services

- Profile: PropTech vendors, B2B real-estate services, construction-
  adjacent SaaS.
- Buyer: CEO, Head of Sales.
- Pain anchors: Pipeline pressure, enterprise procurement, partner
  coverage.
- Dealix fit: Medium.
- Trust risk: Low to medium.
- Notes: Local references matter.

### 2.11 Energy and utilities ecosystem (B2B)

- Profile: Suppliers, services, and SaaS to energy and utilities; not
  the regulated core itself.
- Buyer: VP Sales, Head of Business Development.
- Pain anchors: Long procurement, RFP quality, multi-year deals.
- Dealix fit: Watchlist; delivery complexity is high.
- Trust risk: High.
- Notes: Entered only via partner.

## 3. Sectors explicitly parked

- Consumer-facing retail.
- Pure B2C apps.
- Heavy government direct sales without an existing partner path.
- Regulated cores (banks, telcos) without partner coverage and proof.

## 4. Map maintenance

| Cadence | Activity |
|---|---|
| Weekly | Add notes from buyer conversations |
| Monthly | Score every sector against the rubric |
| Quarterly | Add or retire sector entries |

The map is owned by the Growth Strategist. Sector additions require a
named source and a credible operating path within 30 days. Sector
removals require a documented reason.

## 5. Saudi context notes

- Trust density is high: a single bad signal can damage multi-sector
  reputation. The map intentionally limits parallel experiments.
- Authority concentration is high: many sectors are run by founder-CEOs.
  ICPs in this map flag this.
- Bilingual default is the rule, not the exception.

## 6. Outputs

- `growth/sector_map.csv` — one row per sector with the structure above.
- `growth/sector_targets.csv` — produced by the ranking system from this
  map.

## 7. Non-negotiables

- No sector is added without a named source.
- No sector is publicised externally without founder approval.
- No "guaranteed" outcome language appears against any sector entry.
- A3 is not used. The map is observed (A1) and updated with approval (A2).

## 8. Failure modes and recovery

| Failure | Recovery |
|---|---|
| Sector added on hype | Trust Guardian quarantines; require source |
| Sector retired with hot trigger | Hold trigger; re-score next cycle |
| Sector overlaps with another | Merge or refactor; partner system updated |
| Distress in a sector | Sector intensity reduced; sensitivity flag |

The market map is a discipline. Its value comes from what it does not
include.
