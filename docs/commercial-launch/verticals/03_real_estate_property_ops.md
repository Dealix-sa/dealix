# Vertical Playbook 03 — Real Estate & Property Operations
# دليل القطاع 03 — العقار وإدارة الأملاك

> AI recommends and drafts. Founder approves. Nothing is sent automatically.
> Draft-only. Every email draft includes an opt-out.

## ICP — العميل المثالي
Property operators and asset managers (20–2,000 employees) with multi-asset
portfolios, leasing, and owners-association operations. مشغّلو أملاك ومديرو أصول.

## Buyer personas — شخصيات المشتري
Operations director (portfolio uptime), asset manager (yield), leasing manager
(occupancy/renewals).

## Buyer titles — المسميات
EN: Property Operations Director, Asset Manager, Leasing Manager, Owners Association Manager.
AR: مدير عمليات الأملاك، مدير الأصول، مدير التأجير، مدير اتحاد الملاك.

## Company size fit — حجم الشركة
20–2,000 employees, multi-asset portfolio.

## Geography fit — النطاق الجغرافي
KSA primary; UAE/GCC secondary.

## Pain map — خريطة الألم
- Manual tenant/lease lifecycle tracking — تتبع يدوي لدورة العقد.
- Slow collections and renewal follow-up — بطء التحصيل والتجديد.
- Fragmented maintenance request handling — تشتت طلبات الصيانة.
- No consolidated portfolio reporting — غياب تقرير محفظة موحد.

## Trigger events — أحداث محفّزة
Portfolio acquisition; new community handover; occupancy drop; system migration.

## Discovery questions — أسئلة الاكتشاف
1. How is the lease lifecycle tracked today?
2. Where do collections/renewals slip?
3. How are maintenance requests routed?
4. What does portfolio reporting currently miss?
5. Which manual task eats the most operations time?

## Qualification criteria — معايير التأهيل
Multi-asset portfolio; an operations/asset owner; named reporting or collections
pain; data exists.

## Disqualification criteria — معايير الاستبعاد
Single small unit; no operations owner; expects guaranteed occupancy; wants no-human automation.

## Offers — العروض
Entry audit (499–2,500); collections/renewals pilot on limited dataset (5,000–25,000);
operations retainer (3,000–25,000/mo); enterprise property-ops OS (150,000+).

## Objection handling
- "We have a property system." → We sit on top to unify and speed it. Next: audit.
- "Tenant data is sensitive." → Limited dataset, your data stays with you, human
  approval on every step. Next: scope first.

## Proof assets required
Operations workflow map, anonymized renewal/collections example (only if real), audit template.

## Delivery scope
Audit maps tenant/lease/maintenance/reporting flow, diagnoses top pains,
recommends a pilot, lists risks. No production-system connection during audit.

## Implementation timeline
Audit 3–7 days; pilot 14–30 days; department OS 30–90 days.

## Success metrics
Faster collections/renewals, fewer dropped maintenance requests, one consolidated
report. Measured in pilot, never guaranteed up front.

## Compliance notes
Consented/public contacts; opt-out on every email; no tenant personal data in
first touch; no guaranteed-occupancy claims.

## Risk notes
Never promise occupancy or revenue. Keep human-in-the-loop. Handle tenant PII carefully.

## Founder review notes
Prioritize operators after acquisition or handover, or with falling occupancy. Verify before send.

## No-go claims
No guaranteed occupancy, no "replace your team", no "full automation", no data-access implication.

## What not to say
Don't imply access to tenant data; no fake urgency; one offer per message.
