# Vertical Playbook — Facilities Management & Maintenance
## إدارة المرافق والصيانة

### Sector thesis
FM operators run high-volume, SLA-bound work across many sites with thin
margins. The work is real; the operating system around it is fragmented across
WhatsApp, calls, spreadsheets and email. Dealix installs a workflow OS that
captures requests, ranks work, and turns activity into client-ready reporting —
with the founder/operator approving every external step.

### Why now
Vision 2030 facility growth, ISO/quality audits, and SLA penalties make manual
coordination expensive. Buyers feel the pain monthly.

### ICP
Mid-size FM & maintenance contractors (50–1,000 staff) managing multiple
contracts/sites in KSA/GCC.

### Excluded ICP
Single-handyman operations, pure product resellers, firms with no recurring
SLA contracts.

### Buyer personas & titles
- **Decision maker:** Operations Director / FM Director / General Manager
- **Influencer:** Maintenance Manager, QA/HSE Lead
- **User:** Dispatchers, supervisors
- **Budget owner:** GM / Owner

### Company size & geography fit
50–1,000 staff; Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam, Khobar and wider GCC.

### Top workflows
Work-order intake → triage → assignment → SLA tracking → completion proof →
client reporting → preventive scheduling.

### Top 10 pains
1. Requests scattered across WhatsApp, calls and email
2. No single view of SLA breaches / overdue work orders
3. Quotes & approvals stuck waiting on managers
4. Reactive instead of preventive maintenance
5. Manual, inconsistent client reporting
6. No proof-of-completion trail
7. Technician utilization invisible
8. Spare-parts and vendor follow-up leaks
9. Escalations missed until the client complains
10. Renewals negotiated without performance evidence

### Trigger events / buying signals
New contract win, SLA penalty incident, ISO/quality audit, portfolio expansion,
new Ops Director hire, client churn warning.

### Disqualification signals
No recurring contracts, no SLAs, unwilling to share even anonymized workflow
data, expecting fully automated outreach.

### Offer ladder mapping
- **Entry:** AI Workflow Audit (499–2,500 SAR) — map intake→reporting, find SLA leak points.
- **Pilot:** Paid Pilot (5,000–25,000 SAR) — one contract/site, approval gates.
- **Department OS:** 25,000–150,000 SAR — production work-order + reporting OS.
- **Retainer:** 3,000–25,000 SAR/mo — monitoring, improvements, reporting.
- **Enterprise:** 150,000+ SAR — multi-site governance + integrations.

### Pain-to-offer mapping
SLA breaches & reporting pain → Audit then Pilot on the worst contract → scale
to Department OS once measurable.

### Follow-up example
Short, value-add, references the prior touch only if one genuinely happened.
Never "as discussed" if no conversation occurred.

### LinkedIn manual example
A 2–3 line note copy/pasted manually by the founder. No automation, no
auto-connect.

### Website form example
Reply/intake copy used when a lead submits the contact form — never auto-submitted.

### Discovery questions
- How do work orders arrive today, and who triages them?
- Where do SLA breaches first become visible?
- How long does client reporting take to assemble?
- Which contract hurts most right now?

### Proposal angle
Anchor on the worst contract's SLA leakage; propose Audit → Pilot with explicit
acceptance criteria.

### Proof asset needed
Before/after workflow map; sample SLA dashboard; one anonymized work-order trail.

### Delivery scope, timeline, success metrics
Audit (3–7d): workflow map + bottleneck + risk map. Pilot (14–30d): working
intake→reporting flow on one contract. Success: time-to-report ↓, SLA visibility
↑, overdue WOs ↓ (targets, not guarantees).

### Risks & compliance notes
Operational data is sensitive; use data minimization and explicit consent.
Public business contact only for outreach; no scraping of individuals.

### What not to say
No guaranteed ROI. No "replace your team". No "from our database".

### Objection handling
"We have a CRM" → CRMs track contacts, not SLA-bound work execution.
"We have IT" → we install the workflow OS; IT keeps the infrastructure.

### Founder notes
FM is the warmest wedge: tangible pain, repeatable OS, strong proof potential.
