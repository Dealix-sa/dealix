# Vertical Playbook 02 — Contracting & Project Controls
# دليل القطاع 02 — المقاولات وضبط المشاريع

> AI recommends and drafts. Founder approves. Nothing is sent automatically.
> Draft-only. Every email draft includes an opt-out.

## ICP — العميل المثالي
Contractors and project-driven firms (100–10,000 employees) with active project
portfolios and a PMO. مقاولون وشركات مشاريع لديهم محفظة مشاريع نشطة ومكتب إدارة مشاريع.

## Buyer personas — شخصيات المشتري
Project-controls owner (cost/schedule), PMO lead (governance), commercial manager
(variations/claims).

## Buyer titles — المسميات
EN: Project Controls Manager, PMO Lead, Commercial Manager, Operations Director.
AR: مدير ضبط المشاريع، قائد مكتب إدارة المشاريع، المدير التجاري، مدير العمليات.

## Company size fit — حجم الشركة
100–10,000 employees, multiple concurrent projects.

## Geography fit — النطاق الجغرافي
KSA primary; wider GCC secondary.

## Pain map — خريطة الألم
- Cost/schedule overruns with late warning — تجاوزات مع إنذار متأخر.
- Manual progress reporting across contractors — تقارير تقدم يدوية.
- Fragmented document/submittal control — تشتت ضبط المستندات.
- Slow variation/claim handling — بطء أوامر التغيير والمطالبات.

## Trigger events — أحداث محفّزة
New mega-project award; schedule slippage; PMO stand-up; a claims dispute.

## Discovery questions — أسئلة الاكتشاف
1. How early do you see a schedule slip today?
2. How is progress consolidated across contractors?
3. Where do submittals get stuck?
4. How long does a variation take to process?
5. What does the leadership project report miss?

## Qualification criteria — معايير التأهيل
Active multi-project portfolio; a PMO or controls owner; reporting pain named;
data exists.

## Disqualification criteria — معايير الاستبعاد
Single tiny project; no controls owner; expects guaranteed delivery dates; wants
automation without approval.

## Offers — العروض
Entry audit (499–2,500); progress/claims pilot on limited dataset (5,000–25,000);
controls retainer (3,000–25,000/mo); enterprise PMO OS (150,000+).

## Arabic messaging angles
"نساعد فرق ضبط المشاريع على رصد تجاوزات الكلفة والجدول مبكرًا عبر نظام يعمل تحت
إشرافكم — الذكاء يقترح وأنتم تقررون."

## English messaging angles
"We help project-controls teams catch cost/schedule slips earlier with a system
that runs under your control — it drafts, you decide."

## Cold email draft example (review-only)
Subject: Project controls: earlier slip warning
Body: Hi {PMO Lead} at {Company}. We help project-controls teams catch cost and
schedule overruns earlier with an AI operations system that runs fully under your
control — it drafts, you decide, your data stays with you. We start with the Entry
Diagnostic within days.
CTA: Would a short 15-minute call this week work for you?
Opt-out: Reply STOP to opt out.

## Follow-up draft example
Following up on my earlier note — earlier slip-warning is still the wedge,
starting with a short diagnostic. Should I send a one-page brief? Reply STOP to opt out.

## LinkedIn manual draft example
{Title} at {Company} — we build AI controls support that flags slips earlier,
under your full control. Happy to share a short overview?

## Website form draft example
This is Dealix, an AI revenue & operations system. We help project-controls teams
flag overruns earlier under your control, starting with a short diagnostic. We'd
welcome a brief call.

## Objection handling
- "We have Primavera/ERP." → We sit on top to surface signals earlier. Next: audit.
- "Our data is sensitive." → Limited dataset, your data stays with you, human
  approval on every step. Next: scope first, not data.

## Proof assets required
Controls workflow map, anonymized early-warning example (only if real), audit template.

## Delivery scope
Audit maps cost/schedule/document flow, diagnoses top pains, recommends a pilot,
lists risks. No production-system connection during audit.

## Implementation timeline
Audit 3–7 days; pilot 14–30 days; department OS 30–90 days.

## Success metrics
Earlier slip detection, faster variation processing, cleaner submittal tracking.
Measured in pilot, never guaranteed up front.

## Compliance notes
Consented/public contacts; opt-out on every email; no guaranteed-delivery claims.

## Risk notes
Never promise on-time delivery or fixed savings. Keep human-in-the-loop.

## Founder review notes
Prioritize firms after a mega-project award or visible slippage. Verify before send.

## No-go claims
No guaranteed schedules, no "replace your PMO", no "full automation", no data-access implication.

## What not to say
Don't imply access to their project data; no fake urgency; one offer per message.
