# First 14 Days Client Delivery

> **Status:** Default delivery shape. Adjust per offer.
> **Companion:** `CLIENT_ONBOARDING_PLAYBOOK_AR.md` + `CLIENT_VALUE_REPORTING_AR.md`.

## The principle

> The first 14 days are the proof. The client sees the system work, or they don't. There is no second chance to make a first impression.

## The 14-day structure (in detail)

### Day 1 — Kickoff

- 60-min call.
- Re-cap the proposal.
- Confirm the proof metric.
- Walk the access checklist.
- Book Day 8 + Day 14.

### Day 2 — Access

- Client IT provides access.
- Dealix verifies read access.
- Consent record is signed.
- Data flow diagram is shared.

### Day 3 — Audit re-cap

- 1-page summary of the audit.
- Sent to the client.
- 30-min call to walk it.

### Day 4–5 — First sprint

- Routing rules drafted.
- Tag taxonomy defined.
- Approval queue stubbed.
- Daily digest format agreed.

### Day 6 — Training

- 60-min team training.
- 4 sections: routing, tags, approval, digest.
- Recording sent to the client.

### Day 7 — First digest

- Daily digest in production.
- 5 items, no more, no less.
- The client opens their inbox and sees 5 follow-ups.

### Day 8 — First review

- 30-min review.
- Did the digest work? Are the 5 items right?
- Tune the threshold.

### Day 9–10 — Second sprint

- Approval queue in production.
- Digest refined.
- First retrospective written.

### Day 11 — Pilot survey

- One open-text question.
- Anonymized.
- 1 day to answer.

### Day 12–13 — Tuning

- Apply survey feedback.
- Tune routing, tags, digest.

### Day 14 — Pilot review + decision

- 60-min call.
- What worked, what didn't, the proof metric, the decision.
- Continue → expand. Pause → exit. Pivot → new scope.

## The deliverables by day 14

- The daily digest in production.
- The approval queue in production.
- The team trained.
- The pilot decision made.
- The next-step offer proposed (if continue).

## The success criteria

By day 14, the client should be able to say:

- "The digest is in my inbox every morning."
- "My team is using the approval queue."
- "I can see the 5 things I need to do today."
- "I want to keep using this."

If any of these are missing, the pilot is at risk. Tune in the next 14 days.

## The failure modes

| Failure | Detection | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Digest never reaches the inbox | Day 7 check | Tune the channel; check spam |
| Digest is empty | Day 7 check | Loosen the threshold |
| Digest has 50+ items | Day 7 check | Tighten the threshold |
| Team ignores the approval queue | Day 10 check | Re-train; simplify the rules |
| Client cannot name the proof metric | Day 8 check | Re-cap; re-align |
| Client cancels before Day 14 | Day 11 check | Founder call; understand |

## The escalation

- Day 8: if the digest is not working, founder calls the client.
- Day 11: if the survey is negative, founder calls the client.
- Day 14: if the decision is "pause", founder handles the exit personally.

## The handoff to renewal (Day 14, if continue)

- The pricing model is reviewed.
- The next offer is proposed (move up the ladder).
- The next 14-day cycle is planned.
- A new kickoff is scheduled.

## When to update

- After every 5 deliveries: which day had the most friction?
- After every 10: rewrite the timing based on real client experience.
