# Renewal and Upsell Playbook

> **Status:** Renewal is a renewal, not a re-pitch.
> **Companion:** `docs/offers/DEALIX_OFFER_LADDER_AR.md` + `CLIENT_VALUE_REPORTING_AR.md`.

## The principle

> The renewal happens because the value is undeniable. The upsell happens because the next layer is needed. Neither happens because the founder pushed.

## The renewal conversation (Day 75 of the engagement)

90 days after the kickoff, the founder calls the client for the renewal conversation. Not an email. A call.

### The 30-min agenda

1. **The 90-day recap** (10 min). What we shipped. What we measured. What the team learned.
2. **The proof metric** (5 min). Did we hit it? Why / why not?
3. **The next 90 days** (10 min). What we propose. Which offer is next.
4. **The pricing** (5 min). Founder-approved range for the next 90 days.
5. **The decision** (5 min). Continue, pause, or pivot.

### The 3 outcomes

- **Continue.** Move to the next offer. The 14-day cycle repeats.
- **Pause.** Exit gracefully. Renewal conversation in 90 days.
- **Pivot.** New scope. New proposal.

## The upsell motion (between Days 30 and 75)

The upsell is the natural movement up the offer ladder:

- After Audit → WhatsApp OS or Command Center.
- After WhatsApp OS → Command Center.
- After Command Center → Proposal OS.
- After Proposal OS → AI OS for SMB.
- After AI OS for SMB → Custom Enterprise OS.

The upsell is **proposed by Dealix, accepted by the client**. Not the other way around.

## The upsell signals (when to propose)

- The client asks "what's next?" → propose the next offer.
- The client is consistently using the daily digest → propose Command Center.
- The client is sending 5+ proposals/month → propose Proposal OS.
- The client has hit 30+ people → propose AI OS for SMB.
- The client is in a regulated sector → propose Custom Enterprise.

## The upsell anti-signals (when NOT to propose)

- The client has not adopted the current offer.
- The client is in churn mode (cancelling meetings, ignoring digests).
- The proof metric was missed and the client is unhappy.
- The client is in a budget freeze.
- The renewal is less than 30 days away (wait for the renewal conversation).

## The pricing in the renewal

- The current pricing may be renewed at the same range, or adjusted based on scope.
- The discount policy (`docs/offers/PRICING_LOGIC_AND_APPROVAL_POLICY_AR.md`) applies.
- The founder signs the new range.

## The exit (when the renewal is "no")

If the client says no:

- The exit is graceful.
- The configuration is handed over.
- The data export is provided.
- The case study (if any) is offered in anonymized form.
- The renewal conversation is offered in 90 days.

No hard feelings. No fake case study. The relationship is more important than the deal.

## The 5 things to never do in renewal

1. **Don't re-pitch.** The renewal is a recap, not a sales call.
2. **Don't add new offers in the renewal call.** Propose the next offer, not a suite.
3. **Don't discount on the call.** Founder-approved range, not a call-time cut.
4. **Don't promise new outcomes.** The renewal is about what we already delivered.
5. **Don't skip the decision.** A renewal without a decision is a leak.

## The 5 things to do in renewal

1. **Send the 90-day recap in advance.** The call is the discussion, not the discovery.
2. **Bring the proof metric to the call.** Real numbers, not vibes.
3. **Propose ONE next offer, not three.** The ladder is a sequence.
4. **Make the pricing founder-approved before the call.** No on-call negotiation.
5. **End with a specific date for the next kickoff (or exit).** No "let's see."

## The 5 renewal signals that mean "yes"

1. The client opens the recap email and replies within 24h.
2. The client asks "what's next?" before you propose.
3. The client names a specific decision-maker for the renewal.
4. The client shares internal data showing the value.
5. The client introduces you to a peer.

## The 5 renewal signals that mean "no"

1. The client ignores 2+ recap emails.
2. The client asks for a discount you did not offer.
3. The client introduces a new objection that was not in the original.
4. The client wants to "loop in someone" but cannot name who.
5. The client says "we're happy with what we have" without engaging.

If 3+ signals, walk the renewal gracefully. Do not chase.

## When to update

- After every 5 renewals: which signals predicted correctly?
- After every 10: which offer up-sold most often? Which never?
- After every 20: rewrite the upsell motion based on real data.
