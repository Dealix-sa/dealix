# Upsell Playbook

> Earn the right to expand by delivering. Then ask, with evidence.

## The Retainer Ask (post-Sprint)

At the handoff call, after the customer has seen the Proof Pack:

> "Based on this Sprint, we can deliver [N qualified accounts] every
> month, plus [outcomes]. The Revenue Desk for that scope is [price] per
> month. Should I send the scope?"

The ask is **explicit**, **priced**, and **followed up** in writing.

If the customer says no:
- Ask: "What would have made this a yes?"
- Record the answer in the Friction Log.
- Offer the smallest reasonable next step (e.g. one more sprint).

## The Expansion Ask (existing retainer)

At a healthy monthly review (Tier A or B):

> "Based on what is working in [segment], we can extend to [new segment]
> for an additional [SAR] per month. Want to scope it?"

Triggers for an expansion ask:

- Customer asks for accounts outside the original ICP.
- Customer's CRM shows our work is producing opportunities.
- Customer's leadership has visibly engaged with our reports.

## Forbidden Upsells

- Bundling a feature we do not have.
- Promising a deliverable that requires a workflow not yet at 5
  successes.
- Pre-selling a SaaS product that does not exist.
- "Strategic partnership" framed as an upsell.

## Pricing for Expansions

- Expansion pricing follows `OFFER_LADDER.md` floor + ceiling.
- Discount on bundle: maximum 15% off list, with named reason.

## Logging

Every retainer ask and expansion ask is logged in:

- `dealix-ops-private/client_success/retainer_asks.csv`

With:
- date
- client
- ask price
- outcome (yes / no / pending / counter)
- reason if no
- next action

## Anti-Patterns

- Asking for the retainer in passing, in chat.
- Asking without a price.
- Asking without following up in writing.
- Asking the same person multiple times without new evidence.
