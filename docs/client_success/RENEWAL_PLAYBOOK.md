# Renewal Playbook

> Renewals are negotiations. Treat them like work.

## Renewal Timeline

| Days from contract end | Action |
|------------------------|--------|
| -45 | Founder reviews health score, value delivered, gaps |
| -30 | Founder + decision-maker conversation: renew, expand, exit? |
| -21 | Founder drafts renewal proposal |
| -14 | Renewal proposal sent in writing |
| -7 | Founder follow-up call |
| 0 | Signed renewal or formal end |

## Renewal Proposal Contents

- Recap of value delivered (counts + evidence).
- Continuation scope, price, term.
- Any change (price, scope) with explanation.
- Decision deadline.

## Price Increases at Renewal

Allowed when:

- The retainer has been at Tier A for 6+ months.
- The market price for equivalent services has moved.
- Scope has materially expanded informally and we are formalising it.

Rules:

- Communicated in writing at least 30 days before renewal.
- No silent escalation.
- Maximum increase without explicit re-scope: 15% per year.

## Price Decreases at Renewal

We **rarely** lower price at renewal. If asked:

- Counter with scope reduction (not price reduction).
- If the customer insists on lower price for same scope: graceful exit.

## Multi-Year Commitments

- Default: monthly term.
- Quarterly: 5% pricing benefit allowed.
- Annual: 10% pricing benefit allowed, with quarterly review break-clauses.
- > 1 year: requires T3 decision, and must not lock founder hours below
  the operating capacity envelope.

## Non-Renewal

When we choose not to renew (rare; usually only for Tier D):

- Tell the client at least 60 days out.
- Reason given in plain language.
- Offer a graceful handoff.
- Refer them to a peer if appropriate.

## Renewal Anti-Patterns

- "Auto-renewal" with no review.
- Discounting at renewal without scope change.
- Promising future capability at renewal that does not exist.
- Letting a Tier D retainer roll because cancellation feels awkward.

## Audit

Quarterly:
- Renewal rate this quarter.
- Top reason for non-renewal.
- One change to make for next quarter.
