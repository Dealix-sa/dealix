# Client Tiering

## Tier A
High-value, repeatable, low-risk, retainer potential.

Action: prioritize.

## Tier B
Useful paid sprint, moderate repeatability.

Action: serve well, attempt upsell.

## Tier C
Low-value or unclear fit.

Action: limit customization.

## Tier D
Bad fit.

Action: reject or refer away.

## Rule
Founder time is reserved for Tier A and strategic Tier B clients.

## How Tiering Plugs In

- Tier A clients feed the **Moat System** (case studies, sector
  playbooks, retainer revenue).
- Tier C/D rejections feed the **Kill List** and **Revenue Quality**
  doctrine.
- Tiering is reviewed in the **Monthly Board Pack** under section 3
  ("Customers").
