# Retention Playbook

> Retention is cheaper than acquisition. Treat it like work, not luck.

## Retention Signals (early)

The earliest signs that a retainer is at risk:

- Weekly report not opened / not commented on for 2 weeks.
- Outreach drafts queued for approval not approved within 5 days.
- Monthly review meeting rescheduled twice.
- Decision-maker contact changes (new person on the customer side).
- Customer complains, even casually, about scope or pace.

## Intervention Plan (when client moves to Tier C)

Within 7 days:

1. Founder requests a 30-minute conversation with decision-maker.
2. Founder restates the original goal of the retainer.
3. Founder shares the data: what we have delivered, what is converting,
   what is not.
4. Founder asks: "What would 'great' look like in 30 days?"
5. Founder writes a 1-page restated plan, with concrete deliverables,
   sends it within 48h, asks for written acceptance.

## Restated Plan Template

```
- client: name
- date: yyyy-mm-dd
- original_scope: short
- gap_observed: short
- restated_30_day_plan:
    - deliverable_1
    - deliverable_2
    - deliverable_3
- success_metric: clear, measurable
- review_date: 30 days out
- if_we_miss: refund pro-rata + amicable end
```

## Renewals

A retainer's renewal is decided **before** the contract end:

- 45 days out: founder reviews health score + value delivered.
- 30 days out: founder discusses renewal with decision-maker.
- 14 days out: founder sends renewal proposal.
- 0 days: signed renewal or formal end.

If not renewed, the customer is offered:

- Continued sprint-only relationship (single sprints as needed).
- A handoff document of all materials and accounts.
- An honest debrief (loss reason).

## Defensive Moves We Refuse

- Lowering price to retain a fundamentally mis-fit client.
- Promising features we cannot deliver in 30 days.
- Naming a new "account manager" we do not have.
- Hiding the truth about pipeline progress.

## Audit

Monthly: review the previous month's at-risk retainers.

- Were the interventions executed within SLA?
- Did the score recover?
- If not, was the contract gracefully ended?
