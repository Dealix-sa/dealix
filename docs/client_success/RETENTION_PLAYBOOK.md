# Retention Playbook (Managed Ops)

> How we keep Managed Ops clients past month 3 and into month 12+.

## Why Retention Matters

LTV depends on it. The first 3 months of Managed Ops are net-loss to gross-positive (founder time amortizing). Profit comes from months 4–12.

## Lifecycle Stages

| Months | Stage | Posture |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Honeymoon | Over-deliver, set expectations, capture early wins |
| 2 | Reality | Focus on adoption — are they using what we ship? |
| 3 | Decision | Renewal conversation; soft upsell to 4,999 tier |
| 4–6 | Compounding | Show patterns + sector benchmarks accumulating |
| 7–12 | Anchor | Annual review; consider Custom AI upsell |
| 12+ | Reference | Case study (with consent), referral asks, advisor-style relationship |

## Month 1 — Honeymoon

- Over-communicate
- Send one extra sample artifact unprompted
- Founder-personal check-in at week 2 (not just the weekly report)
- Capture: what's working, what's not (set baseline)

## Month 2 — Reality

- Confirm they're sending the drafts (adoption signal)
- If not: schedule unscheduled call, diagnose blocker
- Adjust message style or sector focus if needed
- Track: client effort (are they reviewing approvals on time?)

## Month 3 — Decision

- Week 11: bring up renewal conversation explicitly
  - "We're approaching month 3. Want to stay on the current plan, expand to the 4,999 tier, or talk about anything that's not working?"
- Week 12: send the renewal proposal (or expanded scope proposal)
- Week 13: confirm decision in writing

## Months 4–6 — Compounding

- Introduce sector benchmarks (now we have data)
- Begin sample artifact upgrades (more sophisticated, more tailored)
- Quarterly review meeting (60 min, strategic — not delivery review)
- Capture wins for case study draft (with consent)

## Months 7–12 — Anchor

- Annual review (full year retrospective)
- If healthy: discuss Custom AI / Revenue Desk fit
- Begin advisor-level relationship (occasional strategic input outside scope)
- Refresh DPA if changes

## Months 12+ — Reference

- Publish case study (with explicit consent each time, even if previously consented)
- Ask for referrals (formal ask, 1–2× per year)
- Maintain quarterly strategic touch

## Save Plays (when retention at risk)

| Signal | Play |
|---|---|
| Approvals delayed > 5 days | Founder calls to ask "everything OK?" |
| Drafts not being sent | Diagnose: too many? wrong tone? client capacity? |
| Weekly report unread | Switch format / channel; ask preference |
| New person involved client-side | Re-onboard them personally |
| Budget pressure flagged | Discuss tier downgrade before they cancel |

## Cancellation Conversation

When a client signals cancellation:
1. Don't argue
2. Ask one question: "What would have to be true for you to stay?"
3. Listen
4. Decide: can we honestly meet that? If yes, propose; if no, exit gracefully
5. Always offer: clean handoff + 30-day support transition + reference if you want to re-engage later

## Anti-Patterns

- Auto-renewing without notice (we send notice 7 days before each cycle)
- "Tar pit" experience (making it hard to cancel)
- Discounting to save a churn (sets a bad precedent)
- Pretending cancellation didn't happen

## Metrics

- Month-3 renewal rate: target ≥ 80%
- Month-6 retention: target ≥ 70%
- Month-12 retention: target ≥ 50%
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) end of year: target ≥ 100%

## What This Refuses

- Lock-in contracts
- Hidden cancellation fees
- "We need to talk to your CFO" pressure
- Trash-talking departing clients (privately or publicly)
