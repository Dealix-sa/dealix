# Testimonial Capture

> How we turn happy clients into trustworthy social proof.
> Consent first. Edit lightly. Never paraphrase into something they didn't say.

## When To Ask

- Day-7 Sprint handoff call (if they used positive language)
- After Managed Ops month 3 renewal (renewal itself is the validation)
- At any unprompted "thank you" or "this is great" moment

## What To Ask

Three questions, in this order:

1. "What was different about working with us compared to alternatives you considered?"
2. "What would you tell a peer who's debating whether to try a Sprint / Managed Ops?"
3. "Can I quote that with your name + company, or pseudonym, or anonymous?"

## Recording

- Live notes during the call
- If permitted: voice recording for accuracy
- Confirm wording in writing (email follow-up) before any use

## Consent Levels

Capture in `clients/{client}/testimonial-consent.md`:

- [ ] Quote with full attribution (name + role + company)
- [ ] Quote with role + company only
- [ ] Quote with sector + region only ("Logistics, Riyadh")
- [ ] Anonymous quote only
- [ ] No public use

Default if unclear: anonymous only. Never assume up.

## Editing

- Light edits OK (grammar, punctuation, length)
- Never re-word the substance
- Never combine multiple quotes into one
- Never add words they didn't say
- Send the edited version back for sign-off before publication

## Storage

- Originals in `clients/{client}/testimonial-original.md` (private)
- Approved-for-use versions in `content/proof_library/testimonials/` (private)
- Published versions referenced from `content/case_studies/` or landing page

## Where Testimonials Go

- Landing page (subject to claim_guard pass)
- Sales decks (subject to consent level)
- Case studies (subject to consent level)
- LinkedIn posts (with explicit one-time consent per post)
- Conference materials (separate consent)

## Refresh Cadence

- Annually: re-confirm consent (people change roles, change minds)
- After incident: pause use of all testimonials from that client until resolved
- If client churns: ask if testimonial can remain; default to remove unless they affirm

## Anti-Patterns

- Composing testimonials from internal team
- Combining multiple clients' quotes into one
- Using a testimonial after the relationship ends without re-confirmation
- Putting words in their mouth (paraphrasing as quote)
- Cherry-picking enthusiastic phrases out of context

## What This Refuses

- Pay-for-testimonial
- Testimonials in exchange for discount (creates pressure)
- Anonymous testimonials presented as named
- Stock-photo headshots
- Using testimonials from suppressed contacts or past incidents

## When To Decline A Testimonial

- Client offers a glowing testimonial that overclaims (would fail claim_guard)
- Client offers a testimonial in exchange for something off-policy
- Client testimonial sounds suspiciously copy-pasted from another vendor

Politely decline + offer to draft something more accurate in their voice.
