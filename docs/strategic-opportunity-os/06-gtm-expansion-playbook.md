# GTM Expansion Playbook

## Purpose
Expand Dealix GTM from founder-led motion into repeatable channels without losing ICP discipline.

## Expansion ladder

| Stage | Focus | Exit criteria |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Founder-led wedge | One ICP and direct selling | Repeatable meetings and clear objections |
| 2. Proof-led selling | Use customer proof to improve conversion | Proof pack used in every serious deal |
| 3. Repeatable outbound | Account list, message, follow-up, CRM hygiene | Predictable meeting generation |
| 4. Partner-assisted GTM | Referral and implementation partners | Partner-sourced opportunities |
| 5. Enterprise motion | Procurement and security readiness | Larger B2B deals progress predictably |
| 6. Category authority | Public narrative and trusted content | Inbound from ICP and partners |

## Channel tests

| Channel | Test | Metric | Owner | Time box |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Founder outbound | 50 high-fit accounts | Meetings booked | Founder/Sales | 2 weeks |
| LinkedIn authority | 10 proof-led posts | Qualified conversations | Founder/Marketing | 4 weeks |
| Partner referrals | 10 partner conversations | Qualified intros | Partnerships | 4 weeks |
| Events/community | 2 targeted appearances | ICP conversations | Founder | 6 weeks |
| Existing network | 30 warm asks | Meetings booked | Founder | 2 weeks |

## Message testing framework

Test three angles:

1. Speed: faster research, qualification, and follow-up.
2. Revenue: better pipeline quality and conversion.
3. Trust: safer AI-assisted GTM with proof and governance.

## GTM operating rules

- Do not expand channels before one message works.
- Do not scale a channel without unit economics review.
- Do not chase non-ICP interest unless strategically approved.
- Every sales objection should update product, proof, or positioning.
- Every win should create a proof asset.

## Weekly GTM review

- Accounts added.
- Messages sent.
- Replies and meetings.
- Conversion by stage.
- Objections.
- Lost reasons.
- Proof gaps.
- Best next channel.

## Expansion decision

Scale a channel only when:

- It creates qualified opportunities.
- It reaches the chosen ICP.
- It produces learnings or proof.
- It does not overload delivery.
- It can be measured in CRM.
