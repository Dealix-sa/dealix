# Board Memo Template

> A memo written every month, even though there is no board yet.
> Forces the founder to think like a fiduciary, not just an operator.

## Why This Exists

You don't have a board. You will, eventually. Writing the memo every month:
- Forces you to articulate progress in non-engineering language
- Creates the artifact you'll need when you raise or hire a CFO
- Reveals when you're spinning vs. compounding

## Format

```markdown
# Board Memo — Month YYYY-MM

## TL;DR
[3 sentences max. What changed, what's next, what I need.]

## Key Metrics (vs. prior month)
- Cash collected:       SAR _____ (Δ ___%)
- MRR:                  SAR _____ (Δ ___%)
- Active customers:     _____ (Δ ___)
- Pipeline value:       SAR _____ (Δ ___%)
- Close rate:           ____% (Δ pts)
- Delivery on-time:     ____% (Δ pts)
- Trust incidents:      _____ (target: 0)
- Runway:               ___ months

## What Worked This Month
- _____
- _____
- _____

## What Didn't Work
- _____
- _____

## Decisions Made
- BUILD: _____
- KILL: _____
- DEFER: _____

## Risks (top 3 from RISK_REGISTER)
- _____
- _____
- _____

## Next Month's One Goal
[Single sentence. Measurable. Time-bound.]

## What I Need From The Board
[Capital · Hires · Intros · Decisions · Nothing — and why]
```

## Discipline

- Write it the last Sunday of the month
- Save to `weekly_reviews/board-memo-YYYY-MM.md` (private repo)
- If you skip a month, write two next month — never let the cadence die
- If you can't fill "What I Need From The Board" with something specific, you're not asking for enough help

## What This Memo Refuses To Hide

- Bad months — always reported with the same precision as good months
- Killed initiatives — listed with the reason, not buried
- Personal cost — if you worked 70 hours, the memo says so (track in `RISK_REGISTER` R-006)

## Audience Right Now

Audience = future you (12 months from now), reading the year as a sequence. Write so that future-you can reconstruct what was actually happening without your memory.
