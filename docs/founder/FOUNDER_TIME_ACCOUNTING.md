# Founder Time Accounting

> Cash is finite. Founder hours are more finite.
> If you cannot say where the hours went, you cannot manage the company.

## The Five Categories

Every founder hour belongs to exactly one of:

1. **Revenue** — outreach, calls, proposals, closing, follow-up, retainer asks
2. **Delivery** — research, scoring, outreach packs, QA, handoff, client review
3. **Trust** — approvals, governance reviews, incident handling, evidence capture
4. **Build** — engineering, templates, playbooks, automations
5. **Admin / Overhead** — finance, legal, hiring, meetings without revenue link

> "Learning" is not a category. Learning happens **inside** the other five.

## Target Mix (current stage: pre-PMF founder-led)

| Category | Target % |
|----------|----------|
| Revenue | 40–50% |
| Delivery | 25–35% |
| Trust | 5–10% |
| Build | 10–20% |
| Admin / Overhead | ≤ 10% |

When MRR > 50,000 SAR, the mix shifts toward Build and Trust.

## How to log

Record at end of day, not real-time. Five-minute discipline.
Write to `dealix-ops-private/founder/founder_time_log.md`:

```
- date: yyyy-mm-dd
  total_hours: 8.0
  revenue: 4.0
  delivery: 2.5
  trust: 0.5
  build: 0.5
  admin: 0.5
  notes: "two proposals out, one QA pass, one approval review"
```

## Weekly Time Audit

In `WEEKLY_CEO_REVIEW.md`, list:

- The **single largest** category overshoot.
- The single largest undershoot.
- One concrete action to correct next week.

## Anti-Patterns

- "Strategy" hours that are not tied to a Go/No-Go decision.
- "Learning" hours with no playbook update.
- "Build" hours that produce no template, automation, or repeatable workflow.
- Meetings without a revenue, delivery, or trust output.

## The 80/20 Hour Question

Once a month ask:

> If I had only 20% of the hours I had this month, which 20%
> would I keep, and what would I cut?

The cuts go on the **Kill List**.
