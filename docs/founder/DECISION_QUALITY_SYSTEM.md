# Decision Quality System

> A decision can be good even if the outcome is bad.
> A decision can be bad even if the outcome is good.
> We score the **decision**, not the outcome.

## Why this exists

Founders confuse luck with judgement. This system forces the
question: was the process sound?

## The Six-Point Decision Quality Rubric

For every decision logged in `decision_queue.md`, score 0/1 per
criterion at the time the decision is **made** (not later):

| # | Criterion | 1 point if… |
|---|-----------|--------------|
| 1 | Framed | The decision is written as a question with at least 2 options. |
| 2 | Evidence-cited | At least one observed fact (not opinion) supports the chosen option. |
| 3 | Cost-bounded | Cash, hours, and opportunity cost are explicit numbers. |
| 4 | Reversibility-aware | Reversal trigger is named, or decision is explicitly marked irreversible. |
| 5 | Time-bounded | A revisit / review date exists. |
| 6 | Bias-checked | At least one steelman of the rejected option is written. |

**Score 6/6:** high-quality decision.
**Score 4–5:** acceptable; note the gap.
**Score ≤ 3:** stop and rework before acting.

## Post-Mortem (90 days later)

For decisions where the outcome is now known, score:

- Outcome: win / partial / loss
- Was the decision quality high? (rubric score)
- Was the outcome consistent with the evidence at the time?
- What signal was missed?
- What goes into a future decision template because of this?

## Common Decision Anti-Patterns

- **Sunk cost** — "We already spent X, so we must continue."
- **Identity capture** — "This is who we are now, so we do it."
- **Narrative override** — "It feels strategic, so the numbers do not matter."
- **Authority laundering** — "X said it on Twitter."
- **Single-source evidence** — "One client asked for it."

## Quarterly Decision Audit

Once per quarter, list every Go decision from the last 90 days.
For each:

- Quality score at time of decision
- Outcome to date
- Did it earn the cost?
- Continue / unwind / replace

The output is the **first input** into the next quarter's Capital Allocation.
