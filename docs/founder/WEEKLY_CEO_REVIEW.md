# Weekly CEO Review

The single weekly ritual that converts a week of activity into a written learning artifact. Written by the founder, every Friday, without exception.

## Purpose
Force the founder to look at the week through the lens of the company's five operating loops and answer five questions: what happened, what worked, what failed, what is the bottleneck, what will change next week. The review is the highest-leverage hour of the week.

## Owner
Sami (Founder).

## Review Cadence
Weekly. Every Friday between 16:00 and 18:00 Riyadh.

## Inputs
- The week's seven daily command briefs.
- The pipeline tracker delta from Monday to Friday.
- The approval log delta from Monday to Friday.
- The cash collected delta from Monday to Friday.
- The weekly intelligence review (if available).

## Outputs
- A single dated file in `weekly_reviews/YYYY-MM-DD.md` (private ops).
- Updates to the Company OS Scorecard.
- A list of decisions for next week, added to the decision queue.
- A list of doctrine updates, applied to operating documents the same day.

## Rules
- Always written by the founder, never by AI alone.
- Never skipped. If the founder is unavailable, the review is moved, never cancelled.
- The review is read first thing Monday morning by the founder.
- Decisions captured here that take more than one week to act on get escalated to the strategic adjustment queue.

## Metrics
- Reviews written per quarter (target: 13/13).
- Decisions captured per review.
- Decisions resolved within one week.
- Doctrine updates produced per quarter.

## Evidence
- Dated files in `weekly_reviews/` (private ops).
- Decision Log entries linked.
- Doctrine document version history.

## Template

> Date: YYYY-MM-DD
> Week ____ of ____

### 1. What happened this week?
Three bullets, factual, no spin.

### 2. What worked?
What produced revenue, delivery, trust, or learning. Why?

### 3. What failed?
What did not produce the expected outcome. Why?

### 4. What is the bottleneck now?
The single largest constraint on revenue, delivery, trust, or learning right now.

### 5. What will change next week?
Two or three specific operating changes. Each change is owned, dated, and verifiable.
