# Hiring Triggers

> A trigger is a measured condition. Not a feeling.

## Trigger Table

| Trigger | Role to consider |
|---------|------------------|
| 100 DMs / week sustained for 4 weeks | SDR or VA (outbound execution) |
| 3 Sprint reports / week sustained for 4 weeks | Delivery Analyst |
| 5 active retainers sustained for 2 months | Operations Manager |
| 10 active retainers | Client Success Lead |
| Engineering build queue > 3 weeks for > 2 months | Engineer / Contractor |
| Content cadence > 5 posts/week sustained, with founder bottleneck | Content Producer |
| Approval queue depth consistently > 5 | Operations Manager / Chief of Staff |

## Pre-Trigger Discipline

Before a trigger fires, do **not** hire. While in the pre-trigger zone:

- Document the workflow (`PRODUCTIZATION_ENGINE.md` Level 1).
- Template the workflow (Level 2).
- Try cheap leverage first: agents, scripts, contractors on a single task.

## Post-Trigger Process

1. **Confirm the trigger** with 2 consecutive months of data.
2. **Write the role description** drawing from `ROLE_MAP.md`.
3. **Decide contractor vs employee.**
4. **Define a 90-day success metric** specific to the role.
5. **Define the kill-by trigger** (if the role does not produce X by
   day 90, end it).
6. **Go through `GO_NO_GO_DECISION_SYSTEM.md`** for the financial commitment.
7. **Hire.**

## Common Bad Triggers (do not act on these)

- "I am tired."
- "Everyone in my YC cohort has hired."
- "It is time to grow."
- "It would be nice to have someone for X."

## Triggers That Do **Not** Lead to Hire

- A single overworked month (not 2-month sustained).
- A retainer customer requested a "named account manager" (let founder
  remain it; rename without hiring).
- A LinkedIn post said hiring is good.

## Review

Triggers are reviewed monthly in the Monthly Strategy Review.
Any sustained trigger not acted on goes into the decision queue.
