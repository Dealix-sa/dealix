# Delegation Rules

When the founder delegates, what stays with the founder.

## Always with the founder
- A2 and A3 approvals.
- Pricing changes.
- Public positioning changes.
- Contracts and NDAs.
- Refund decisions.
- First conversation with strategic prospects.

## Delegable by default
- Lead sourcing and enrichment.
- First-draft outbound messages (with A1 review).
- Research and report drafting.
- Weekly status reports for active clients.
- Bookkeeping and invoice issuance.

## How to delegate cleanly
1. Define the outcome in writing.
2. Define the quality bar (link to the scorecard).
3. Define the boundary — what to escalate.
4. Schedule a review cadence.
5. Trust until a signal says otherwise.

## How to take back cleanly
- Surface the signal in the 1-on-1.
- Re-train, redesign the system, or re-scope before reassigning.
- Avoid silent re-absorption — that breaks trust and clarity.

## Rule
The founder should be doing only what only the founder can do. Everything else is a delegation candidate.
