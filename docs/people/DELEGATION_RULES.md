# Delegation Rules

> During founder-led phase, delegation is a craft.

## What we can delegate now (founder-only phase)

- Account research from a fixed source list (Level 2 workflow).
- Data entry into the pipeline (Level 2).
- Friction-log clustering (Level 2 with founder review).
- Bilingual translation of approved drafts (with founder approval).

## What we cannot delegate yet

- Final scope sign-off.
- Final proposal sending.
- Final case study approval.
- Hiring decisions.
- Pricing decisions outside policy.
- Refund decisions.
- Anything T2 or T3 in the Approval Matrix.

## How to delegate well

1. **Write the workflow first.** Level ≥ 2.
2. **Define the output the delegate produces.**
3. **Define the founder review step.**
4. **Define the failure mode.**
5. **Cap the cost (hours or money) of one cycle.**
6. **Review weekly for the first 4 cycles, then monthly.**

## Delegation Anti-Patterns

- "Just figure it out."
- Delegating without a workflow document.
- Delegating one-off tasks instead of recurring ones.
- Delegating decisions instead of execution.
- Letting the delegate become the only person who understands the
  workflow (founder must remain able to do it).

## Withdrawal of Delegation

When to pull a workflow back to founder-only:

- Quality dropped below QA threshold for two cycles.
- The workflow itself shifted enough that the existing template no
  longer applies.
- The trust risk exceeds the leverage gained.

When this happens:

- Document the reason.
- Update the workflow.
- Re-attempt delegation only after the workflow is stable again.

## Review

Monthly in the Monthly Strategy Review: which workflows became
delegate-ready this month?
