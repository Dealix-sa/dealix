# Partner Outreach Guide

Partners — consultancies, integrators, sector specialists, agencies — are a high-leverage distribution surface when handled carefully. Dealix partner outreach is named, specific, and contract-anchored.

**Source of truth:** `$PRIVATE_OPS/partner_pipeline.csv`
**Owner:** Founder + Partner Lead
**Trust gate:** A2 — partner contracts, revenue-share terms, and white-label rights require founder approval.

## Partner archetypes

| Archetype | Fit |
|-----------|-----|
| Sector consultancy | Sells advisory to a vertical Dealix serves |
| Systems integrator | Implements adjacent platforms (CRM, ERP) |
| Marketing agency | Generates demand for Saudi B2B |
| Reseller / white-label | Brands Dealix under their own brand (rung 7) |

## Pathway

1. Identify candidate via founder network or inbound interest.
2. Initial conversation: founder to founder, no contract talk.
3. Fit assessment: ICP overlap, no-conflict check, trust posture.
4. Pilot agreement: one client, joint delivery, named owners.
5. Pilot review: outcome, friction, joint plan.
6. Partner contract: revenue share, training, IP, branding.
7. Activation: enablement and joint go-to-market.

No partner sends Dealix-branded outreach. Partners introduce; Dealix delivers (or co-delivers per contract).

## What partners get

- Quarterly enablement briefing.
- Sector report co-branding rights (under contract).
- Revenue share per `$PRIVATE_OPS/partner_revenue_share.csv`.
- Named CSM for material relationships.
- Joint case-safe summaries where consented.

## What partners do not get

- Authority to amend Dealix pricing.
- Authority to write contracts.
- The Dealix prompt registry, model identifiers, or eval gates.
- Customer data beyond what is needed for joint delivery.

## Partner outreach guardrails

- Partners never use Dealix's domain to send outbound.
- Partners never send "from" Dealix.
- Partners never publish Dealix client names without Dealix and client consent.
- Partners never imply guaranteed revenue.

## Failure modes

- **Brand misuse:** a partner publishes copy with banned language. Detection: monthly audit. Recovery: ask for removal; revoke co-branding if repeated.
- **Customer-data overreach:** a partner asks for customer data outside the joint engagement. Detection: data access logs. Recovery: deny and document.
- **Channel conflict:** a partner targets a Dealix-direct prospect. Detection: pipeline overlap check. Recovery: per-deal split based on partner contract.

## Recovery path

If a partner repeatedly breaches guardrails, the founder triggers the partner-contract termination clause and pulls all co-branded assets.

## Metrics

- Active partner count.
- Partner-sourced qualified opportunities per quarter.
- Partner-attributed revenue (verified).
- Brand-misuse incidents (target: 0).

## Disclaimer

Partners are independent. Dealix does not guarantee partner-sourced revenue. Estimated value is not Verified value.
