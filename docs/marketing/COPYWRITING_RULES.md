# Copywriting Rules

Apply these to every word that touches a buyer.

## 1. Sentence-level

- Average ≤ 25 words.
- Active voice by default.
- One idea per sentence.
- Lead with the result, follow with the why.
- No exclamation marks.
- No emojis.

## 2. Paragraph-level

- ≤ 4 sentences per paragraph.
- One paragraph = one idea.
- Numbers go in their own sentence when possible.

## 3. Hooks

- Open with a number, an observation, or a question — never with "I'd love to share…".
- Hooks must be specific to the buyer or the sector.
- Hooks may be in Arabic or English. In bilingual pieces, lead with AR.

## 4. CTAs

- Verb-led: "Request a sample", "Start a Sprint", "Open the console".
- No "Click here" / "Learn more".
- No verbs that imply effort the buyer hasn't agreed to ("Sign up now").

## 5. Numbers

- SAR with the comma separator ("12,500 SAR", not "12500 SAR").
- Percentages without space ("32%").
- Time in operator units ("7 days", "30 days", "monthly"), not "two weeks-ish".
- Always cite the period the number covers.

## 6. Bilingual

- Each language stands on its own.
- AR first when in doubt for KSA-targeted pieces.
- Numbers and English brand names stay LTR inside RTL paragraphs.
- The tagline is invariant: `Intelligent Deals. Real Growth.` and `صفقات ذكية. نمو حقيقي.`

## 7. Banned phrases (verifier-enforced)

- "Guaranteed revenue", "guaranteed sales", "guaranteed results".
- "Fully autonomous", "AI that sells for you".
- "10x", "100x".
- "Revolutionise", "disrupt", "game-changing".
- "Cutting-edge", "best-in-class", "world-class" (without source).
- "World's first", "world's only".
- "Synergy", "leverage" (as verb).
- "Unlock value", "unleash potential".

## 8. Brand-safe alternatives

| Banned                          | Use instead                                  |
|---------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Guaranteed revenue              | Measurable revenue lift                      |
| Fully autonomous                | AI-assisted, trust-gated                     |
| Game-changing                   | Specific metric or before/after              |
| Revolutionise                   | Specific change or delta                     |
| Cutting-edge                    | Specific evidence or source                  |
| Best-in-class                   | Specific evidence or source                  |
| Unlock value                    | Specific outcome with metric                 |

## 9. The five-line check

Before any piece goes out, the writer runs the five-line check:

1. Does this claim a guarantee? (If yes → fix.)
2. Does this name a customer without approval? (If yes → fix.)
3. Does this say "fully autonomous"? (If yes → fix.)
4. Does this use a banned superlative? (If yes → fix.)
5. Is the tagline (if used) exactly correct? (If no → fix.)
