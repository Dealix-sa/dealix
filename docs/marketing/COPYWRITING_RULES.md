# Copywriting Rules | قواعد الكتابة

> These rules apply to every word Dealix publishes — landing, email, deck, UI microcopy, social, partner, investor.

---

## 1. The Five Always | الخمسة الدائمة

1. **Always name an outcome.** Every paragraph or block ends at an outcome the buyer cares about.
2. **Always honor the approval gate.** When automation is described, the approval gate is named in the same sentence.
3. **Always cite ranges, conditions, and pilot N.** No naked numbers.
4. **Always pass the bilingual gate.** Arabic is authored, not translated.
5. **Always write to a real chair.** Pick one human and write to them — Founder, GM, Head of Sales, Partner — never to "the market."

---

## 2. The Five Nevers | الخمسة الممنوعة

1. **Never claim guaranteed revenue or specific multipliers** ("guaranteed 5x," "10x your pipeline").
2. **Never describe Dealix as autonomous, fully automated, or "send for you."**
3. **Never publish customer-identifying material** (logo, quote, screenshot) without written customer approval.
4. **Never commit to pricing, contracts, or payment terms** without escalation to a human owner.
5. **Never use hype words** ("revolutionary," "magical," "game-changing," "world-class," "the best," "kill," "destroy," "crush").

---

## 3. Sentence Pattern | نمط الجملة

```
<Outcome>  +  <Approval gate>  +  <Time/condition>  +  <Source/N>
```

- "10 qualified meetings, drafted for your approval, in 30 days, across 7 KSA pilots."
- "Bilingual outreach you control, with every send queued, audited under PDPL."

---

## 4. Headline Patterns | أنماط العناوين

| Pattern | Template | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Outcome verb | "[Verb] [outcome] [trust qualifier]" | "Turn warm relationships into intelligent deals — with your approval on every send." |
| Reframe | "[New thing] without [old cost]" | "Run a 30-day Revenue Sprint without a single auto-send." |
| Trust-first | "[Trust posture] by design. [Differentiator] by default." | "Approval-gated by design. Bilingual by default." |
| Category | "The [category] for [audience]." | "The Revenue Operating Company for Saudi B2B." |

---

## 5. Numbers Discipline | الأرقام

- Use ranges: "6–12," not "10."
- Cite conditions: "in 30 days, at ICP density ≥ X."
- Cite source / pilot N: "across 7 KSA pilots."
- Round honestly: never round up to a friendlier number.
- Currency: "SAR" or "ر.س" per locale; never both in one surface.

---

## 6. Voice Calibration | معايرة الصوت

- **Tone:** calm, precise, evidence-led.
- **Tense:** present and present-perfect; avoid future-promise tense.
- **Person:** "you" (the buyer) and "we" (Dealix). Avoid "I" outside founder-led content.
- **Length:** sentences ≤ 22 words on average; paragraphs ≤ 4 sentences.
- **Cuts:** if a sentence does not name an outcome, an approval gate, or a piece of evidence, cut it.

---

## 7. Bilingual Rules | قواعد ثنائية اللغة

- Arabic is authored natively. Hire and brief Arabic writers; do not translate.
- Brand terms (Dealix, Revenue OS, Founder Console) stay in Latin script inside Arabic with bidi isolation.
- Numerals: Western Arabic (0–9) by default; Eastern (٠–٩) only in editorial Arabic upon approval.
- Match length, scannability, and CTA prominence between languages.

---

## 8. CTA Conventions | الدعوات

- Verb-first, outcome-named: "Book a Free Diagnostic," "Talk to the Revenue Desk."
- Never generic: avoid "Learn more," "Click here," "Submit."
- Approval-context CTAs: "Approve draft," "Queue for review," "Decline."
- Exactly one primary CTA per surface; secondary links never visually compete.

---

## 9. Microcopy | الكتابة المصغرة

- **Empty state:** explain + offer one next step.
- **Error:** what happened, the fix, who to contact.
- **Confirmation:** name the action and the new state ("Draft queued for your approval").
- **Approval prompt:** state what happens on approve **and** on decline.
- **Loading:** name the operation; do not show spinners without context.

---

## 10. Editing Checklist | قائمة التحرير

Before publish:

- [ ] Five Nevers — none present.
- [ ] Five Always — all present.
- [ ] Bilingual parity — both versions clear the voice gate.
- [ ] Accessibility — alt text, contrast, focus, heading order.
- [ ] Non-negotiables — no external sending, no proof publishing without approval, no guaranteed revenue, no pricing/contract commitments, no autonomous framing.
- [ ] Source of every number cited.
- [ ] One primary CTA, verb-first.

A piece that fails any item returns to draft.

## 11. Common Rewrites | تحويلات شائعة

| Off-brand | On-brand |
|---|---|
| "Guaranteed 5x ROI." | "Across 7 KSA pilots, ICP-fit accounts saw 6–12 qualified meetings in 30 days." |
| "Set it and forget it." | "Draft, queue, approve — every action gated by you." |
| "AI sends emails for you." | "Dealix drafts bilingual emails; you approve before any send." |
| "World-class platform." | "The Revenue Operating Company for Saudi B2B." |
| "Best in class." | "Approval-gated by design. Bilingual by default." |
| "Click here to start." | "Book a Free Diagnostic." |
| "Massive pipeline." | "Pipeline you can audit." |

---

## 12. Non-Negotiables | الخطوط الحمراء

- No external sending claims.
- No proof publishing without written customer approval.
- No guaranteed-revenue claims.
- No pricing or contract commitments in copy without escalation.
- No autonomous-agent framing.

---

## 13. Owner & KPI | الملكية والمؤشرات

- **Owner:** Content Lead, with Brand Lead.
- **KPI:** Editing-checklist compliance per shipped piece (target 100%).
- **KPI:** Hype-word incidence in shipped copy (target 0).
- **KPI:** Bilingual parity rate (target 100%).
- **KPI:** Non-negotiable violations (target 0).
