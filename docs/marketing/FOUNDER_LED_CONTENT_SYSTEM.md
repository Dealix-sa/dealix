# Founder-Led Content System

> DEALIX — INTELLIGENT DEALS. REAL GROWTH.
> The founder is the most credible voice Dealix has. This document
> defines how founder-led content is produced, gated, and
> distributed without becoming personal-brand theatre.

Founder-led content is not "personal brand". It is the founder
applying the Dealix operating system to their own voice. The same
trust contract that governs the rest of the Marketing OS applies
here: no guaranteed outcomes, no unapproved proof, no external send
automation, no pricing or contract commitment outside the offer
document.

## Operating Principles

- The founder writes from operating reality, not from theory. If
  Dealix has not seen the pattern in the field, the founder does
  not claim it.
- Posts that name a buyer require recorded approval. Anonymised
  patterns are allowed; named references are not.
- The founder may have an opinion; the founder does not have a
  guarantee. Opinion is labelled as opinion.
- Founder content runs through the same evals as everything else.
  No "founder bypass" exists.

## Content Pillars (Founder-Specific)

The founder rotates through six pillars on a fortnightly cadence:

1. **Operating lesson.** A pattern observed in the operating loop
   — what worked, what did not, what was refused.
2. **Governance teardown.** A specific governance decision (policy
   update, refusal marker addition, eval gate change) explained for
   a buyer audience.
3. **Founder Console teardown.** A behind-the-glass look at a
   piece of the Dealix system, with care taken not to expose buyer
   data.
4. **Proof snippet.** An anonymised data point from the evidence
   loop. No customer names, no unapproved figures.
5. **Partner angle.** A view on how partners and integrators
   operate inside the Dealix trust contract.
6. **CEO perspective.** A short take on Saudi B2B, Vision 2030
   priorities, or the Saudi B2B Revenue Operating System category.

The Content Strategist tracks pillar coverage so no pillar is
silently dropped.

## Founder Content Loops

Founder content participates in all three Marketing OS loops:

- **Objection loop.** Most founder content starts here. A sales-call
  objection becomes a teardown post, a short video, a newsletter
  section.
- **Governance loop.** A new refusal marker, a new policy rule, a
  new eval gate test becomes a teardown for founders.
- **Evidence loop.** An anonymised pattern becomes a proof snippet
  or a sector report data point.

A pillar without a loop tag is a flagged item. Founder opinion
without a backing loop is rare and labelled.

## Drafting Workflow

1. The founder records a pattern (sales call note, governance
   change, evidence point) into the objection inbox.
2. The Content Strategist drafts the founder-led artefact with the
   founder's voice and the relevant evidence links.
3. Claims-safety, brand-voice, and proof-safety evals run.
4. The Brand Guardian agent surfaces tone drift.
5. The draft returns to the founder for review.
6. The founder edits or approves.
7. The approved draft is published manually. The trust ledger
   entry is written.

The Content Strategist does not publish on the founder's behalf.
The founder publishes (or delegates publication to a human operator
who has been delegated by name in the Founder Console).

## Voice Calibration

The founder voice in Dealix has four calibration points:

- **Sober.** No exclamation marks. No hype. No emojis. The voice is
  serious without being grim.
- **Evidence-first.** A claim travels with its source.
- **Refusal-aware.** The voice says what Dealix will not do, often,
  and visibly.
- **Saudi-grounded.** References to Saudi B2B reality are concrete,
  not decorative.

The Brand Guardian eval includes voice-drift detection that flags
posts that read like generic LinkedIn thought-leadership.

## Cadence

- LinkedIn: 4–5 posts per week, Sunday through Thursday.
- Long-form (LinkedIn article, blog, or guest piece): 1–2 per
  month.
- Short video: 1 per week, tied to the LinkedIn loop.
- Podcast / interview appearance: opportunistic, gated by founder
  approval.

Missed days are not back-filled. A week with three reviewed posts
is preferred to a week with five rushed ones.

## Forbidden Patterns

The founder voice may not use:

- "Guaranteed", "promise", "we will get you N meetings".
- "Game-changer", "revolutionary", "10x".
- Manufactured outrage or controversy-mining.
- Unapproved customer references.
- Unredacted screenshots.
- "Closed deal" posts that reveal pricing or contract terms.
- "Vanity metric" posts (follower count, post views, etc.).

The Brand Guardian eval catches these patterns and returns the
draft.

## Approval Markers (Founder Content)

Each founder content artefact carries three markers:

| Marker                | Owner                                  | States                |
|-----------------------|----------------------------------------|-----------------------|
| `claims_safety`       | Brand Guardian + founder review        | PENDING, APPROVED     |
| `brand_voice`         | Brand Guardian + founder review        | PENDING, APPROVED     |
| `proof_safety`        | Proof Safety Agent + founder review    | PENDING, APPROVED, N/A|

A draft cannot be published until all three markers are in their
terminal state.

## Engagement and Replies

Replies to comments and DMs are part of the founder voice and are
governed by the same rules.

- The founder responds personally. Replies are not drafted by an
  agent without founder review.
- Replies that would reveal pricing, scope, or proof require the
  same trust contract as a marketing artefact.
- A DM that crosses into a sales conversation is logged in the
  Founder Console with the next-step artefact (Diagnostic Engagement
  Note, Sprint Scope Letter draft, etc.).

## Promoted Posts and Paid Boosts

Paid boosts on founder content require:

- Paid advertising readiness state confirmed (see Marketing OS).
- Targeting respects PDPL and suppression lists.
- The boosted post has cleared the same evals as an organic post
  and has been approved by the founder.

No boost happens automatically; the human publishes and boosts.

## Localisation

The founder writes in Arabic and English. Both are peer voices,
not translations. The fortnightly pillar rotation alternates
languages so neither audience is starved of cadence.

## Failure Modes

- A founder post is published with a guaranteed-outcome phrase.
  Failure: an eval missed the phrase. Post is retracted; eval is
  upgraded.
- A founder post names a customer without approval. Failure: proof
  safety missed it. Post is retracted; trust ledger records the
  retraction and the approval queue is reviewed.
- A founder post drifts into generic thought-leadership. Failure:
  brand-voice eval drift. Brand Guardian flags the post; the next
  draft is recalibrated.
- A founder post triggers a competitor-bashing thread. Failure: the
  founder steps out of the thread, logs the pattern, and reframes
  the next post on positioning rather than comparison.

## Audit and Eval

- Every founder post writes a trust ledger entry on approval and a
  second entry on publication.
- The Performance Analyst tracks reply quality, qualified
  conversation rate from founder posts, and refusal rate.
- Quarterly, the Content Strategist publishes a founder-content
  audit in `marketing/founder_content_audit.md`.

## Why Founder-Led, Not Founder-Branded

A founder-branded approach turns the founder into a product. A
founder-led approach turns the founder into a credible operator who
applies the same operating system in public that Dealix applies in
private. The latter compounds; the former evaporates the moment the
founder steps away. Dealix is built for the long arc.

The founder voice is part of the trust contract. It does not exist
to manufacture demand. It exists to shorten the distance between a
buyer's question and Dealix's evidence — exactly the same purpose
as every other part of the Marketing OS.
