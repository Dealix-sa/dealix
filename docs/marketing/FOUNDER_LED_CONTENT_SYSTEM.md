# Founder-Led Content System | نظام محتوى المؤسس

> The founder is Dealix's most credible distribution channel. This system makes the founder's voice repeatable, bilingual, and category-defining — without drifting into hype.

---

## 1. Purpose | الغرض

To turn the founder's operating experience into a steady, trust-building content motion that:
- Educates the market on the Revenue Operating Company category.
- Earns warm inbound from Saudi B2B operators.
- Recruits aligned partners and hires.
- Holds the line on every Dealix non-negotiable.

---

## 2. Voice Anchors | مرتكزات الصوت

- **Operator-first.** Speak from the chair of someone who has shipped pipeline this week.
- **Calm precision.** Short sentences, real numbers (with ranges), no rhetorical flourishes.
- **Locally fluent.** Reference KSA buying cycles, sector realities, Hijri/Gregorian planning where relevant.
- **Approval-gated honesty.** Be plain about what we do and do not do — never auto-send, never publish proof without approval, never guarantee revenue.

If the post sounds like a generic LinkedIn thought-leader, it is not Dealix.

---

## 3. Content Formats | الصيغ

| Format | Length | Cadence | Channel |
|---|---|---|---|
| Operator field note | 80–180 words | 2 / week | LinkedIn |
| Category post | 150–300 words | 1 / week | LinkedIn |
| Reframe post | 60–120 words | 1 / week | LinkedIn |
| Long-form essay | 1,000–1,800 words | 1 / month | Site + newsletter |
| Newsletter intro | 200–400 words | 1 / week | Email |
| Podcast guesting | 30–60 min | 2 / month | External |
| Roundtable opener | 5–7 min | 1 / quarter | KSA in-person |

---

## 4. Post Patterns | أنماط المنشورات

### Pattern A — Field note
> "This week, we drafted 38 Arabic outreach messages for a Riyadh logistics ICP. We sent 0. The customer approved 14, edited 11, declined 13. Six replied. Two booked. Here is what the approval queue actually changed."

### Pattern B — Reframe
> "If your sales engagement tool sends without you, it is also publishing without you. In KSA B2B, that is a brand decision, not a productivity decision."

### Pattern C — Category education
> "A Revenue Operating Company is not a tool. It is a company that operates your revenue function with you, on an approval-gated OS, with signed evidence. Here is why Saudi B2B is the right starting market."

### Pattern D — Operator lesson
> "Three patterns we have seen across 7 Saudi B2B pilots: (1) intent signals beat list size; (2) Arabic-native drafts double reply rates vs. translations; (3) approval queues compress, not expand, decision time."

---

## 5. Bilingual Cadence | الإيقاع الثنائي

- Aim for **roughly 50/50 Arabic and English** posts each week, with key category posts published in both languages within 72 hours.
- Arabic posts are authored natively, not translated.
- For long-form essays, the English version ships first; the Arabic companion ships within 7 days.

---

## 6. Source Material | المواد المصدر

- Active pilot field notes (with PII and customer identity stripped unless approved).
- Founder calendar — meetings, observations, decisions.
- Newsletter inbox — questions from operators.
- Product release notes from the rung roadmap.
- Roundtable / event takeaways.

A weekly 30-minute "voice harvest" turns raw notes into draftable hooks.

---

## 7. Drafting Workflow | سير الصياغة

```
Voice harvest  →  Hook bank  →  Draft (Founder)
              →  Bilingual edit (Content Lead)  →  Voice gate
              →  Non-negotiable gate  →  Schedule  →  Publish
              →  Reply window (Founder, ≤ 24 h)  →  Log
```

The founder publishes their own posts; the system supports, but never impersonates.

---

## 8. Engagement Discipline | الانضباط في التفاعل

- Reply within **24 hours** to comments on founder posts.
- Replies stay in the same voice (operator-first, calm, specific).
- Do **not** name competitors in replies.
- Do **not** make pricing, contract, or revenue commitments in replies — escalate to a DM and to the sales owner.
- DM follow-ups are personal, never templated, and never auto-sent.

---

## 9. What the Founder Never Posts | ما لا يُنشر

- Guaranteed-revenue claims or specific multipliers ("10x," "double pipeline").
- Customer logos, quotes, or screenshots without written customer approval.
- Side-by-side competitor comparisons or competitor screenshots.
- Pricing, contract, or payment commitments.
- "Autonomous agent" framing.
- Hype words ("magical," "revolutionary," "world-class," "the best").

---

## 10. Operator-Story Template | قالب قصة المشغّل

```
Context  →  Constraint  →  What we tried
        →  What customer approved/declined/edited
        →  What changed (numbers, ranges, conditions)
        →  What we learned  →  What we are doing next
```

Identifying details are scrubbed or generalized unless the customer has approved attribution.

---

## 11. Newsletter Discipline | انضباط النشرة

- One weekly issue, bilingual sections.
- Opens with a 200–400-word founder note in the operator-story pattern.
- Includes 3–5 curated links (own + external operator content).
- Always ends with a single CTA (usually the Free Diagnostic).
- Consent-based list only; one-click unsubscribe; PDPL-compliant.

---

## 12. Non-Negotiables | الخطوط الحمراء

- No external sending of personal outreach under the founder's identity at scale — every personal message is personal.
- No proof publishing without written customer approval.
- No guaranteed-revenue language.
- No pricing/contract commitments in posts, replies, or DMs without escalation.
- Approval-gated automation only — the founder's content describes this posture honestly.

---

## 13. Owner & KPI | الملكية والمؤشرات

- **Owner:** Founder (with Content Lead support).
- **KPI:** Posting cadence (target ≥ 3 / week founder posts).
- **KPI:** Inbound conversations attributable to founder content (track weekly).
- **KPI:** Bilingual coverage (target ≈ 50/50).
- **KPI:** Reply rate to founder DMs within 24h (target ≥ 90%).
- **KPI:** Non-negotiable violations (target 0).
