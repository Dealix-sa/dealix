# LinkedIn Outreach Guide

## 1. Doctrine

- LinkedIn is **founder-led**. Not "BDR-led" with the founder cc'd.
- No automation tools that violate LinkedIn ToS.
- No scrapers.
- First-touch ≤ 80 words.
- Always cite a public LinkedIn post or activity in the first touch.
- Bilingual posture: AR or EN, whichever the buyer last posted in.

## 2. Connection request rules

- Always include a personalised message.
- ≤ 250 characters.
- Cite the post / activity you saw.
- Do not pitch in the connection request.

Good example:

> Hi Sara — I saw your post about KSA mega-projects pipeline. Same picture as a few CCOs we work with. Happy to share a sector observation if helpful. — Bassam

Bad example:

> Hi Sara, I'd love to learn more about your business and explore synergies. Are you free for a quick call this week?

## 3. First DM rules

- Wait until the connection is accepted (don't InMail blast).
- ≤ 80 words.
- Cite a *fresh* observation (≤ 21 days).
- Offer one specific thing.
- Mention the trust posture briefly.

## 4. Follow-up rules

- Maximum 2 follow-up DMs per connection.
- 10 days minimum between follow-ups.
- Each follow-up brings new evidence or value.

## 5. Engagement (not outreach)

Outreach is one motion. Engagement is another.

- Comment on 2–4 posts/day from buyer-circle accounts.
- Comments must add value, not "great post!".
- Comments are an inbound channel, not an outbound one — we don't push to DM in the same day.

## 6. Refusal patterns

- No bulk InMails.
- No connect-and-pitch in 30 seconds.
- No "I'm sure you're busy, but…" preamble.
- No "synergy", "leverage" (as verb), "unlock".
- No revenue guarantees.

## 7. Schema

LinkedIn drafts use the same `data/marketing/outreach_drafts.csv` schema as email, with `channel = linkedin`.

## 8. Founder voice on LinkedIn (separate from outreach)

The founder posts under their own name 5–7 times/week (see `FOUNDER_LED_CONTENT_SYSTEM.md`). LinkedIn outreach DMs come from the **same** account. There is no "company DM" — Dealix doesn't DM, the founder does.

## 9. Tooling

We use only LinkedIn's native UI in v1. No third-party automation, scraping, or "engagement pods". This is doctrine, not just policy.
