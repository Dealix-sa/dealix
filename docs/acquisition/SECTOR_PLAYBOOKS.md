# Sector Playbooks

> One playbook per Tier 1 sector. Each playbook is empirically built from at least one shipped sprint.
> Until a sprint has shipped in a sector, that section reads "DRAFT" — no claims allowed.

## How To Use

For each prospect in a Tier 1 sector, pull the matching playbook:
- Use the scoring weight overrides
- Use the pain hypothesis library
- Use the messaging patterns
- Use the objection responses

## Sector 1 — Logistics & Last-Mile

**Status:** DRAFT (no shipped sprint yet)

**Why this sector:** High deal volume, manual quoting cycles, multi-stop dispatchers carrying spreadsheets, weak follow-up after first quote.

**Pain hypothesis library:**
- "Your dispatchers chase quotes via WhatsApp; no one tracks which quotes won."
- "You hire SDRs who use Excel and leave after 6 months — the playbook leaves with them."
- "Your repeat shippers don't get prioritized because no one scores them."

**Messaging pattern (LinkedIn DM):**
```
Marhaba {name},

Saw {trigger: hiring page / news / sector report}.
Quick question — when a repeat shipper goes silent for 30 days, what's your process for re-engaging?

Most logistics ops we look at don't have one (and lose ~20% of repeat volume).

I built a productized 7-day Revenue Sprint for cases like this — 499 SAR, evidence-backed, founder-approved on every external send.

Open to a 15-min Free Diagnostic next week?

— Bassam
```

**Top 3 objections + responses:**
- "We have a CRM" → "Great — what % of your reps actually update it weekly? The Sprint shows the gap, not replaces the tool."
- "We're already with an agency" → "All good. Would a 15-min look at your top 10 stalled deals be useful regardless?"
- "Prices are tight in our sector" → "499 SAR for a Sprint that shows you 50 scored prospects + 3 outreach variants is below most one-off consultant fees."

## Sector 2 — B2B Professional Services

**Status:** DRAFT (no shipped sprint yet)

**Why this sector:** Sells time, painful follow-up across long deal cycles, partners as sellers (no SDR culture), highly referenceable when wins land.

**Pain hypothesis library:**
- "Partners forward referrals via email; you have no way to track which referral source converts."
- "Your top 3 partners are 80% of revenue and you have no system for the next 30 prospects."
- "You write proposals one-off each time; the win rate is unmeasured."

**Messaging pattern (LinkedIn DM):**
```
Hi {name},

When you win a partner referral, do you have a single place that tracks
which partner sent it and what closed?

Most of the {sector} firms we look at don't — and they leak 30%+ of
warm referrals because the follow-up dies in someone's inbox.

I run a productized 7-day Revenue Sprint that maps this out (499 SAR,
founder-approved every send, no auto-spam).

Open to a 15-min Free Diagnostic?

— Bassam
```

**Top 3 objections + responses:**
- "We're partner-led, we don't do outbound" → "Sprint isn't outbound-only — it's referral-tracking + scored top-prospect list."
- "Our clients are confidential" → "Sprint uses your data only inside your private workspace. We sanitize anything that leaves."
- "We have a CFO who manages this" → "Great — the Sprint output is a CFO-readable scorecard. Would they want it?"

## Sector 3 — Light Manufacturing & Distribution

**Status:** DRAFT (no shipped sprint yet)

**Why this sector:** Long cycles, repeat orders unmanaged, regional sales reps with no central pipeline, big benefit from scored prospect lists.

**Pain hypothesis library:**
- "Your sales reps drive between cities; the pipeline lives in their notebooks."
- "Repeat customers re-order via phone; nobody scores or sequences them."
- "When a tender goes out, you compete on price because there's no prior relationship layer."

**Messaging pattern (LinkedIn DM):**
```
Hi {name},

If one of your top distributors stopped ordering for 60 days, would
your system flag it for you?

Most {sector} firms we look at find out at end-of-quarter when revenue
dips.

I built a productized 7-day Revenue Sprint that maps this gap and gives
you 50 scored re-engagement prospects (499 SAR, every external send
founder-approved).

Open to a 15-min Free Diagnostic?

— Bassam
```

**Top 3 objections + responses:**
- "We work on ERP" → "Sprint is upstream of ERP — it's where the next-best-action lives, not the order itself."
- "Our reps know the customers personally" → "Yes — the Sprint augments rep knowledge with scored next-90-day prospects."
- "We don't do digital" → "Output is a one-page printable scorecard + a sanitized contact list. Paper-friendly."

## Playbook Maturity

A sector playbook is considered **proven** when:
- ≥ 2 sprints shipped in that sector
- ≥ 1 closed proposal (paid)
- ≥ 1 sanitized evidence pack public

Until then: `DRAFT` watermark stays. No public claims about the sector's results.

## Adding A Sector

Only after:
- 1 paid sprint in Tier 2 sector unprompted (signals demand)
- Founder + advisor review
- Strategy Filter pass

## Review Cadence

- Per sprint: update the sector playbook with what was learned
- Monthly: review playbook freshness — anything > 90 days stale needs a refresh
- Quarterly: add / remove sectors per `ICP_STRATEGY.md`
