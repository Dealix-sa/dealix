# Objection Handling Bible

> **Status:** Draft. The founder internalizes, not recites.
> **Use:** When the prospect raises any of the 25 objections below.
> **Schema:** every response carries `evidence_level` and `approval_required`.

## The 5 universal rules

1. **Acknowledge first, then redirect.** Never argue.
2. **Ask a question before you answer.** "وش خلّى الـ CRM ما يكفي؟"
3. **Use their language, not yours.** Mirror what they said.
4. **Never promise what you can't measure.** Say "we can show you", not "we guarantee".
5. **Know when to walk away.** Some objections are disqualifiers in disguise.

## The 25 objections

### 1. "السعر غالي."

- **What it really means:** they don't see the value yet, or the budget is genuinely tight.
- **Bad response:** "ما هو غالي، قارن بالمنافسين."
- **Best response:** "أفهم. لو الـ audit يطلع 10 فرص ضايعة، كم تساوي عندك؟ لو الرقم أعلى من السعر، نقسم. لو أقل، نوقف."
- **Question to ask:** "كم يخسر فريقك كل أسبوع في الفرص الضايعة؟"
- **Proof to show:** the audit report shape (PDF) + 1 anonymized case.
- **Next step:** re-frame around cost-of-inaction.
- **Walk away if:** they want a price cut without engaging with the value.

### 2. "عندنا CRM."

- **What it really means:** the CRM is not solving the problem, but they don't want to admit it.
- **Bad response:** "أي CRM؟"
- **Best response:** "ممتاز. Dealix يقرأ من CRM ويعطيك الطبقة اللي فوقه. ما نطلب منك تطلع منه."
- **Question to ask:** "وش الـ CRM اللي تستخدمونه؟" (then know its gaps.)
- **Proof to show:** the read-only connector list.
- **Next step:** propose the audit. The audit will show what the CRM is not capturing.
- **Walk away if:** they say the CRM is the answer to everything.

### 3. "عندنا موظف تسويق."

- **What it really means:** the marketer is overloaded; the founder is protecting them.
- **Bad response:** "ما يحتاج marketer معانا."
- **Best response:** "حل ممتاز. Dealix يساعده — تقرير يومي بدل ما يبني كل شيء يدوي."
- **Question to ask:** "كم ساعة يقضي في التقارير كل أسبوع؟"
- **Proof to show:** the daily digest example.
- **Next step:** propose Command Center (offer 3) as the marketer's helper, not replacement.
- **Walk away if:** they cannot give the marketer 2 hours/week for a new tool.

### 4. "نستخدم واتساب عادي."

- **What it really means:** WhatsApp IS their CRM.
- **Bad response:** "لازم CRM."
- **Best response:** "أعرف. WhatsApp صار الـ CRM الفعلي. Dealix يقرأ منه ويرتب. ما نغير شيء عندكم."
- **Question to ask:** "كم thread مفتوح عندك الآن بدون رد؟"
- **Proof to show:** the WhatsApp OS offer card.
- **Next step:** propose the WhatsApp & Follow-up OS (offer 2) after the audit.
- **Walk away if:** they refuse any "routing" because they want it 100% personal.

### 5. "ما نحتاج AI."

- **What it really means:** they've been burned by an AI tool or a consultant.
- **Bad response:** "AI هو المستقبل."
- **Best response:** "موافق. Dealix ما يبيع AI. يبيع رؤية + قرارات. الـ AI خلف الكواليس."
- **Question to ask:** "وش اللي ما عجبك في الأدوات السابقة؟"
- **Proof to show:** the trust policy + the approval queue.
- **Next step:** propose the audit (no AI needed for the audit).
- **Walk away if:** they are philosophically opposed to AI in business.

### 6. "ما نثق بالذكاء الاصطناعي."

- **What it really means:** trust has been broken before. Or they don't know how AI fits.
- **Bad response:** "ما في شي تخاف منه."
- **Best response:** "أتفهم. عشان كذا، Dealix AI-with-humans. كل قرار يراجعه بشر قبل ما ينفذ."
- **Question to ask:** "وش يخوفك بالضبط؟"
- **Proof to show:** the trust preflight + the human approval matrix.
- **Next step:** the audit itself is the proof — they see exactly what we read and what we recommend.
- **Walk away if:** they want a 100% human solution (we are not that; we are AI-with-humans).

### 7. "الخصوصية."

- **What it really means:** they are worried about PDPL, data residency, or client trust.
- **Bad response:** "بياناتك آمنة."
- **Best response:** "حق. Dealix يلتزم بـ PDPL. ما نخزن بدون موافقة. عندنا DPA + consent record."
- **Question to ask:** "وش يخوفك بالضبط — التخزين، الإرسال، أو المشاركة؟"
- **Proof to show:** the privacy policy + the DPA template.
- **Next step:** send the DPA template + the consent record.
- **Walk away if:** they are in a regulated sector without DPA in place (escalate to founder + counsel).

### 8. "هل تضمن نتائج؟"

- **What it really means:** they want certainty. There is none.
- **Bad response:** "نضمن."
- **Best response:** "لا نضمن نتائج محددة. نضمن: تقرير audit محدد + خطة إصلاح. لو ما استفدت، نوقف."
- **Question to ask:** "ما هي نتيجة محددة تقيس عليها النجاح؟"
- **Proof to show:** the proof metric concept in the proposal.
- **Next step:** define a proof metric together.
- **Walk away if:** they want a money-back guarantee tied to a number.

### 9. "كم يستغرق؟"

- **What it really means:** they want to plan.
- **Bad response:** "سريع."
- **Best response:** "الـ audit 5 أيام. الـ WhatsApp OS 2–4 أسابيع. الـ Command Center 4–6 أسابيع. حسب الـ offer."
- **Question to ask:** "متى تحتاج تشوف نتيجة؟"
- **Proof to show:** the timeline in the offer card.
- **Next step:** book based on their timeline.
- **Walk away if:** they want a 1-week delivery on a 4-week engagement.

### 10. "ارسل عرض."

- **What it really means:** they want to compare without engaging. Sometimes legit. Sometimes a stalling tactic.
- **Bad response:** "ها هو العرض." (without discovery)
- **Best response:** "قبل ما أرسل عرض، أحتاج 30 دقيقة discovery. عرض بدون فهم = لا يصلح."
- **Question to ask:** "ما هي المعلومات اللي تحتاجها في العرض؟"
- **Proof to show:** the offer card (1 page) as a teaser.
- **Next step:** book the discovery.
- **Walk away if:** they insist on a proposal without discovery. (See disqualification rules.)

### 11. "نحتاج نفكر."

- **What it really means:** they don't see enough value yet, or there's a hidden blocker.
- **Bad response:** "حاضر، خذ وقتك." (passive)
- **Best response:** "تمام. متى آخر يوم تقدر تقرر فيه؟"
- **Question to ask:** "ما هي المعلومة اللي لو عرفتها الحين، كانت ساعدتك تقرر؟"
- **Proof to show:** the proof pack (1 case study if available).
- **Next step:** book a specific follow-up date.
- **Walk away if:** they cannot name a date. (Stalling with no end = no.)

### 12. "ارجع بعد شهر."

- **What it really means:** they are not ready. Maybe budget cycle, maybe priorities.
- **Bad response:** "حاضر، أتابع." (passive)
- **Best response:** "متى بالضبط؟ أحط في الـ CRM." (specific date)
- **Question to ask:** "ما الذي يتغير بعد شهر؟"
- **Proof to show:** none — they have not earned the right.
- **Next step:** if specific date, calendar it. If vague, walk.
- **Walk away if:** they cannot name a date.

### 13. "ما عندنا ميزانية."

- **What it really means:** this is not a priority, or the budget is genuinely gone.
- **Bad response:** "بس رخيص."
- **Best response:** "أفهم. لو ما في ميزانية، الـ audit ممكن يكون آخر أولوياتك. أرسل لك روابط مجانية لما نكتب عنها، بدون ما نتوقع منك شيء."
- **Question to ask:** "متى تبدأ السنة المالية عندكم؟"
- **Proof to show:** nothing in this round.
- **Next step:** add to a low-touch nurture list. Re-visit in 90 days.
- **Walk away if:** they are not in the active list anyway.

### 14. "نبي نجرب مجاناً."

- **What it really means:** they want proof without paying. Sometimes legit (small biz, cash tight). Sometimes a tactic.
- **Bad response:** "ما نعمل free trials."
- **Best response:** "الـ audit تقدر تجربه بـ [discount / free] في حالة [sector, first 3, etc.]. لو ما استفدت، ما عليك شيء."
- **Question to ask:** "ما الذي لو شفته، يخليك تقول 'هذا يستاهل'؟"
- **Proof to show:** the audit deliverable.
- **Next step:** if the founder approves a free audit, book it with clear success criteria.
- **Walk away if:** they want everything free, forever.

### 15. "عندنا وكالة تسويق."

- **What it really means:** they already pay for marketing execution.
- **Bad response:** "Agency ما تقدر تسوي هذا."
- **Best response:** "Agency تنفذ. Dealix يعطيك visibility. متكاملين، مو متنافسين."
- **Question to ask:** "Agency تعطيك تقرير أسبوعي واضح؟"
- **Proof to show:** the daily digest vs the agency report.
- **Next step:** propose Dealix as a layer above the agency.
- **Walk away if:** the agency is the decision maker and they refuse the layering.

### 16. "وش يفرقك عن HubSpot/Zoho؟"

- **What it really means:** they want to know if you replace or add.
- **Bad response:** "نحن أحسن."
- **Best response:** "HubSpot و Zoho هما الـ CRM. Dealix هو الـ OS layer اللي فوق. نقرأ منهم ونعطيك قرارات يومية. ما نستبدلهم."
- **Question to ask:** "وش الذي ما يغطي الـ CRM عندك؟"
- **Proof to show:** the contrast table in `COMPETITOR_POSITIONING_AR.md`.
- **Next step:** the audit. The audit reads from HubSpot/Zoho and shows what's missing.
- **Walk away if:** they want a CRM, not a layer.

### 17. "وش يفرقك عن بوتات واتساب؟"

- **What it really means:** they are confusing us with chatbots.
- **Bad response:** "ما نحن بوت."
- **Best response:** "البوتات outbound — ترسل. Dealix read-only — يقرأ ويرتب. ما نرسل شيء بدون إذنك."
- **Question to ask:** "كيف كانت تجربتك مع البوتات السابقة؟"
- **Proof to show:** the WhatsApp OS offer card + the trust policy.
- **Next step:** the audit shows where the WhatsApp chaos actually is.
- **Walk away if:** they want a bot. We are not a bot.

### 18. "ما عندنا بيانات منظمة."

- **What it really means:** they are worried about onboarding effort.
- **Bad response:** "ما يهم."
- **Best response:** "أغلب عملائنا ما عندهم. الـ audit ينظم اللي عندك. ما نبني من الصفر."
- **Question to ask:** "وش عندك الآن — WhatsApp + إيميل + أي CRM؟"
- **Proof to show:** the audit scope (we read what you have).
- **Next step:** book the audit. The audit handles the chaos.
- **Walk away if:** they have literally no data and no inbound at all.

### 19. "نخاف من التعقيد."

- **What it really means:** they have been burned by over-engineered tools.
- **Bad response:** "سهل."
- **Best response:** "أتفهم. الـ audit 5 أيام، تقرير واحد. الـ WhatsApp OS 4 أسابيع. لا عقود طويلة."
- **Question to ask:** "ما هي الأداة اللي كانت معقدة وآخر مرة؟"
- **Proof to show:** the timeline + the offer card.
- **Next step:** pilot. Low commitment.
- **Walk away if:** they refuse any structured process at all.

### 20. "ما عندنا وقت."

- **What it really means:** priorities are elsewhere. Or they are not the buyer.
- **Bad response:** "الوقت ضروري."
- **Best response:** "أفهم. الـ audit 5 أيام من وقتك أنت — 60 دقيقة في اليوم 1، 30 دقيقة في اليوم 5. الباقي فريقك."
- **Question to ask:** "متى يكون أول وقت فاضي 60 دقيقة؟"
- **Proof to show:** the audit timeline.
- **Next step:** book when they have time.
- **Walk away if:** they say "maybe next quarter" without a specific date.

### 21. "نبي كل شيء مرة وحدة."

- **What it really means:** they want the full OS, not an audit.
- **Bad response:** "نعملها كلها."
- **Best response:** "أفهم الطموح. لكن الـ audit أولاً يكشف الأولويات. بعده WhatsApp OS، بعده Command Center. كل خطوة تبني على السابقة."
- **Question to ask:** "ما هي الأولوية الأولى؟"
- **Proof to show:** the offer ladder.
- **Next step:** start with the audit. Refuse to skip.
- **Walk away if:** they insist on skipping the audit. (Custom Enterprise can skip; SMB cannot.)

### 22. "في crisis الآن (seasonal) — ما عندنا وقت للـ AI."

- **What it really means:** short-term fire, long-term planning paused.
- **Bad response:** "نقدر نبدأ بسرعة."
- **Best response:** "أتفهم. متى تنتهي الأزمة؟ نرتب بعد [تاريخ]."
- **Question to ask:** "متى يكون أول أسبوع ما فيه crisis؟"
- **Proof to show:** nothing in this round.
- **Next step:** calendar a re-engagement. Walk for now.
- **Walk away if:** they cannot name a date.

### 23. "أحتاج موافقة الـ partner / board."

- **What it really means:** they are not the sole decision maker.
- **Bad response:** "تكلم معاهم وأرجع."
- **Best response:** "تمام. نرتب مكالمة ثلاثية. متى يناسب partner / board؟"
- **Question to ask:** "من هي الأشخاص اللي لازم يكونون في المكالمة؟"
- **Proof to show:** the proposal summary.
- **Next step:** book a 3-way call. Send the proposal to all attendees 24h before.
- **Walk away if:** they cannot name who needs to be in the room.

### 24. "شركة كبيرة ما تتعامل مع شركات صغيرة."

- **What it really means:** they want to feel you are not a startup toy.
- **Bad response:** "ننافس الكبار."
- **Best response:** "أفهم. Dealix يخدم SMB. لو أنتم أكبر من SMB، Custom Enterprise OS هو الخيار — 12+ أسابيع، governance مدمج."
- **Question to ask:** "كم شخص في الشركة؟"
- **Proof to show:** the offer ladder — Custom Enterprise for the larger end.
- **Next step:** if they fit Custom Enterprise, escalate to founder.
- **Walk away if:** they are below the SMB range and above Custom Enterprise range (uncommon but happens).

### 25. "ما عندي مشكلة أصلاً."

- **What it really means:** they don't see the pain. Or they are protecting their ego.
- **Bad response:** "عندك."
- **Best response:** "تمام. ممكن أسألك سؤال واحد؟ في آخر 30 يوم، كم lead دخل عندكم وكم خرج (اشترى أو تقدم)؟"
- **Question to ask:** the conversion question.
- **Proof to show:** nothing. The number is the proof.
- **Next step:** if the number reveals a gap, the pain surfaces. If not, walk.
- **Walk away if:** they are genuinely happy and the math confirms it.

## The walk-away script

When you walk away:

> "أقدّر وقتك. Dealix ما يناسبكم في هذي المرحلة. لو احتجتوني بعد 3–6 أشهر، اعمل reply على أي إيميل سابق."

No pitch. No follow-up. Log the disqualification in the approval queue.

## When to update the bible

- After 20 deals: if 3+ objections repeat weekly, rewrite the response.
- After 50 deals: if a specific objection correlates with a "no", add a "preflight" check before the call.
- After 100 deals: retire the objections that no longer fire.
