## Template structure (apply per sector)

### 1. Sector vital stats (Saudi-specific)

- Market size estimate (cite source or mark is_estimate=True)
- Number of B2B players (CR registry / SDAIA / SAGIA)
- Growth rate vs. global benchmark
- Vision 2030 alignment angles
- Typical revenue tier per company

### 2. Common pain (what every founder in this sector says)

3-5 pain points specific to this sector, in their language.

### 3. Why Dealix is uniquely positioned

What about our doctrine + Saudi-first stance specifically wins
in this sector?

### 4. Recommended entry offer

Which of the 5 offers maps best? Why?

### 5. Outreach hook examples (bilingual)

3 specific opening lines that would resonate in this sector.

### 6. Sector-specific objections

What's likely to come up? Map to OBJECTION_HANDLING.md responses.

### 7. Decision-maker profile

Title, age, decision-making style, channel preference.

### 8. Sample customer story (case-safe)

Real or hypothetical case showing transformation.

### 9. Doctrine angle for this sector

Which of the 11 non-negotiables hits hardest here? Lead with it.

### 10. Success metrics for this sector

What would a great 90-day result look like for a customer here?

## Sector 3: Saudi logistics

### 1. Vital stats
- ~350-500 logistics operators (CR + TGA registry, is_estimate=True)
- Vision 2030: SAR 1.6 trillion logistics + tourism aspiration
- Heavy fragmentation (top-10 ~30% market share)
- Margins thin → ROI on tech needs to be obvious
- Decision-makers: ops-focused, not tech-focused

### 2. Common pain
1. Customer acquisition expensive (CAC > 6 months payback)
2. Existing CRM (often SAP) under-utilized
3. Need to communicate operational reliability to enterprise
   shippers
4. Bilingual reporting required for international clients
5. Limited dedicated marketing function

### 3. Why Dealix wins
- Bilingual reporting (Arabic for local + English for international)
- ROI-focused proof events resonate with ops mindset
- Doctrine #1 (no_live_send) protects their reputation in a
  small industry where bad outreach travels fast

### 4. Entry offer
**Managed Revenue Ops Starter (999 SAR/mo)** — low entry, sticky
because monthly ops align with their cadence.

### 5. Outreach hooks
- AR: "{Their logistics company} يخدم {known shipper}. نتعامل مع
  reporting بالعربية والإنجليزية لـ enterprise shippers. ١٥
  دقيقة؟"
- AR: "ROI واضح خلال ٣٠ يوم. لو لا — refund."
- EN: "Bilingual reporting for international shippers + Saudi-
  built reliability messaging. 15 min worth?"

### 6. Likely objections
- "ROI لازم يكون واضح" → reframe: "Sprint بـ ٤٩٩، refund لو ما
  حصلنا proof events"

  لا يستبدل"
- "ما نشتغل بالإنجليزية أبد" → check: غالبًا يخدمون international
  shippers، Arabic-first يخدمهم تنافسيًا

### 7. Decision-maker profile
- Title: COO or Ops Director (sales falls under ops in logistics)
- Age: 40-55
- Channel: WhatsApp (after warm intro) > Email
- Decision speed: 2-6 weeks
- Budget authority: COO + GM together typically

A mid-size Saudi logistics operator was losing enterprise shipper
RFPs because their bilingual proposals took 2 weeks to produce.
After Dealix Managed Ops: bilingual proposals templated, RFP
turnaround dropped to 3 days, win rate up. (Hypothetical.)

### 9. Doctrine angle
**#6 (no_unverified_outcomes)** + **#8 (no_silent_failures)** —

### 10. Success metrics

## How to use these positions

When the founder books a discovery call:
1. Check the lead's sector
2. Open the matching position above
3. Use the outreach hooks during qualifying questions
4. When pricing comes up, lead with the recommended entry offer
5. When objections arise, use the section-6 prep
6. If a sector isn't here yet: draft a new section using the template
   and add to this file

Sector positions evolve with real customer feedback. Update quarterly
based on which hooks actually convert.
