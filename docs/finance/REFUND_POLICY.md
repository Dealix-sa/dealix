# Refund Policy

> Refunds are a learning event. Treat them seriously.

## When we refund

- We promised X, we delivered less than X, and the customer asked for
  a refund.
- We discovered, mid-delivery, that we cannot meet the scope; we refund
  the unused portion.
- A trust incident on our side caused material customer harm.

## When we do **not** refund

- Customer changed their mind after we delivered the scope.
- Customer did not action our delivery (we delivered; they did nothing).
- Customer asked for outside-scope work and refused to pay change order.
- Customer cited a guarantee we did not offer.

## Refund Quantum

- Full refund: we did not deliver, or trust incident with material harm.
- Partial refund: we delivered part of scope; refund the unused portion
  pro-rata.
- Credit (no refund): customer prefers credit toward future work and we
  both agree.

## Approval

- Refunds ≤ 1,000 SAR: founder approves directly.
- Refunds > 1,000 SAR: T3 approval (Go/No-Go decision recorded).

## Refund Workflow

1. Customer requests refund in writing.
2. Founder confirms scope vs delivery in writing.
3. Founder decides: full / partial / credit / decline.
4. Decision communicated to customer in 48h.
5. If approved: refund issued within 7 days.
6. Customer signs an acknowledgement of refund settlement.
7. Logged in `dealix-ops-private/finance/refund_log.md` with root cause.

## Root Cause Logging

Every refund records:

- What we promised (cite scope)
- What we delivered (cite Proof Pack)
- What the customer expected (verbatim)
- The gap
- The systemic cause (process, agent, template, person)
- The change we will make to prevent recurrence
- Whether the change has been made

## Public Discipline

We do not announce refunds. We do not pre-emptively cite "satisfaction
guarantee". Refund language is in contracts, not marketing.

## Audit

Monthly: review the refund log. If refund rate > 5% of revenue, escalate
to a strategy review.
