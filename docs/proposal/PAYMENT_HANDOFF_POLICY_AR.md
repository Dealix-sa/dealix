# Payment Handoff Policy

> **Status:** Hard rule. No payment link is generated by Dealix.
> **Companion:** `docs/offers/PRICING_LOGIC_AND_APPROVAL_POLICY_AR.md`.

## The principle

> Dealix proposes a number. The founder approves it. The client pays through a third party (the client's bank, the platform of record, or a manual transfer). Dealix never holds money.

## The flow

```
1. Proposal is sent (pricing_status: draft_only or approved_range_required).
2. Client agrees in principle.
3. Founder approves the final number (logged in approval queue).
4. Founder generates the payment handoff document (PDF, not a link).
5. Founder sends the handoff document manually.
6. Client pays through the agreed channel.
7. Payment is confirmed (manual or via the agreed signal).
8. Dealix starts delivery.
9. The handoff is logged with: amount, channel, date, founder signature.
```

## What the bundle never does

- Generate a payment link.
- Call a payment API.
- Store a payment method.
- Refund a payment.
- Handle a chargeback.

These are all the founder's responsibility, through the agreed channel.

## The payment handoff document (1 page)

A PDF with:

- Client name.
- Offer name.
- Pricing (founder-approved).
- Currency (SAR by default).
- Payment channel (bank transfer, Moyasar, manual, etc.).
- Reference number (e.g. invoice number).
- Founder signature + date.
- Expiry (the price is time-bounded).

## The payment channels (allowed)

- **Bank transfer** to a known account (the founder's, not Dealix's).
- **Moyasar** (the platform of record) — see `dealix/payments/`.
- **Manual cash** (in exceptional cases, with founder sign-off).
- **Stripe / Tap** (only with founder + counsel sign-off for international).

The bundle's `scripts/proposal_pack_dry_run.py` does not call any of these. It produces a PDF.

## The forbidden channels

- **Crypto.**
- **Personal PayPal.**
- **Personal bank account of anyone other than the founder.**

## The discount in the handoff

If a discount was approved, the handoff document shows:

- List price.
- Discount (with the rule that authorized it).
- Final amount.
- Founder signature.

## The receipt

After payment is confirmed:

- Founder sends a receipt (PDF).
- The deal moves to `stage: paid` in `templates/launch/sales_pipeline.example.json`.
- The kickoff is scheduled within 7 days.

## The audit trail

Every payment handoff is logged in `templates/launch/approval_queue.example.json` with:

```json
{
  "handoff_id": "ho_001",
  "deal_id": "deal_2024_agency_001",
  "offer": "WHATSAPP_FOLLOWUP_OS",
  "amount_sar": 32000,
  "discount_pct": 0,
  "channel": "bank_transfer",
  "founder_signature": "founder",
  "approved_at_iso": "2024-12-01",
  "expires_iso": "2024-12-15",
  "payment_confirmed_iso": null,
  "receipt_id": null
}
```

## The trust preflight check

The preflight flags any draft that:

- Contains a payment URL.
- Contains a payment API call.
- Contains a "Pay now" button.
- Contains a SAR amount + "auto" or "automatic".
- Contains a third-party payment processor name without the founder's approval note.

## When to update

- When a new payment channel is added to the founder's setup.
- When the discount policy changes.
- When a payment-related incident occurs (chargeback, fraud, etc.).
- When the audit trail format needs an additional field.
