# Proposal Template (English Version)

> **Status:** Mirror of the Arabic version. Same structure, English copy.
> **Use:** when the prospect's primary language is English.

## The structure (12 sections, max 6 pages)

The sections are identical to `PROPOSAL_TEMPLATE_AR.md`. The copy below is a 1-paragraph sketch of each section in English.

### 1. Client context

> Brief profile of the client, the decision makers, the discovery call date, and the pain statement (verbatim from the call).

### 2. Problem

> What we heard. What we observed. The cost of doing nothing for 12 months. Why now.

### 3. Current leakage

> 3–5 specific leaks. Each with: location, frequency, cost, owner. Table format.

### 4. Proposed system

> The offer (which one). What it includes. What it does not. The expected outcome. The proof metric.

### 5. Scope

> Timeline. Phases. Deliverables. Required access.

### 6. Out of scope

> The 3–5 things we explicitly do not do. Cold WhatsApp. Auto-reply. Final pricing. Replacement of CRM / ERP.

### 7. Assumptions

> The 3–5 assumptions. Each: "If false, we re-scope."

### 8. Responsibilities

> What the client does. What Dealix does. What neither does.

### 9. Success metrics

> The proof metric. Secondary metrics. Reporting cadence. End-of-pilot decision.

### 10. Risks

> The 3–5 risks. Likelihood. Impact. Mitigation. Table format.

### 11. Pricing

> `pricing_status: draft_only` (or `approved_range_required` / `founder_approval_required`). The SAR range with "founder-approved" note. Payment handoff policy.

### 12. Next step

> The action: sign + consent + kickoff. The date. The person. The escalation.

## Why this exists

The Arabic version is canonical. This English version exists for:

- English-speaking decision makers in Saudi B2B.
- Regional expansion (UAE, Qatar, Kuwait) — same content, English copy.
- International partners and investors.

The structure is identical to keep the maintenance simple. Only the copy differs.

## When to update

- Same cadence as the Arabic version.
- After 5 English-language proposals, review the tone (Saudi English is different from US English — be aware).
