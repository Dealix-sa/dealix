# Dealix Brand OS

## Purpose

The Dealix Brand OS is the operating system for how Dealix presents itself to the world. It governs positioning, voice, visuals, website messaging, claims, competitor differentiation, and the Saudi market narrative. Dealix is a Saudi B2B AI Operating Systems company — not a chatbot, CRM, or marketing agency. The brand is Saudi-first, PDPL-compliant, and approval-first.

## Owner

Founder / CEO with the content lead. Every public-facing claim must be approved before publish.

## Daily Usage

- Before publishing any copy, check it against CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md.
- Before writing any message, check VOICE_AND_TONE.md and POSITIONING.md.
- Before making any competitive claim, check COMPETITOR_DIFFERENTIATION.md.
- Weekly: review the copy bank for accuracy and freshness.

## Inputs

- `docs/company/DEALIX_SOURCE_OF_TRUTH.md`
- Customer evidence and Executive Proof Packs
- Market and competitor signals
- PDPL and compliance requirements

## Outputs

- Approved public copy (AR and EN)
- Website messaging
- Sales and outreach copy
- Saudi market narrative

## Workflow

1. Define the message intent and audience.
2. Draft in the Dealix voice (see VOICE_AND_TONE.md).
3. Check against POSITIONING.md and CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md.
4. Approve manually before publish (approval-first).
5. Log the published copy in the copy bank.
6. Review weekly for accuracy and freshness.

## Acceptance Criteria

- No public claim contradicts the source of truth.
- No guaranteed revenue, fake testimonials, or fake ROI claims.
- Every published asset is approved and dated.
- Dealix is consistently described as a Saudi B2B AI Operating Systems company.
- Voice and tone are consistent across AR and EN.

## Risks

- Drift between brand docs and public copy.
- Claims that promise guaranteed outcomes damage trust and compliance.
- Inconsistent voice across channels weakens positioning.
- Describing Dealix as a chatbot/CRM/agency dilutes the category.

## What Not To Do

- Do not claim guaranteed revenue or assured returns.
- Do not use fabricated testimonials or results.
- Do not describe Dealix as a chatbot, CRM, or marketing agency.
- Do not bypass PDPL or approval-first rules.
- Do not publish without approval.
