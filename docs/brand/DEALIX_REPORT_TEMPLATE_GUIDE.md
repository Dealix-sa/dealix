# DEALIX Report Template Guide

**Owner:** Founder + Brand Lead
**Source of truth:** `docs/brand/DEALIX_MARKETING_ASSET_GUIDE.md`

## Three report types

| Type | Purpose | Audience | Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| Diagnostic Report | Pre-sprint scope and pricing confirmation | Single customer | 6-10 pages |
| Sprint Outcome Report | Post-sprint evidence and recommendations | Single customer | 8-14 pages |
| Sector Scorecard | Aggregated sector intelligence | Public or partner audience | 12-20 pages |

All three share the cover, footer, and disclosure conventions. They differ in content density and audience.

## Shared conventions

### Cover

- Pipeline gradient backdrop.
- Lockup top-left.
- Report type tag in Emerald Teal Micro caps top-right (e.g., "DIAGNOSTIC REPORT").
- Title centered: "[Customer or Sector] — [Report Subject]".
- Date and version stamp bottom.

### Footer (every page)

- Wordmark left.
- Page number center.
- Report number right (format `DLX-DIAG-YYYY-NNNN`, `DLX-SPRINT-YYYY-NNNN`, `DLX-SECT-YYYY-NNNN`).

### Disclosure block (closing page)

> Estimated value is not Verified value / القيمة التقديرية ليست قيمة مُتحقَّقة.
> Dealix does not guarantee revenue, meetings, or conversion outcomes. Aggregated patterns presented here are methodology-led and never identify individual accounts unless explicitly authorized in writing.

## Diagnostic Report — structure

1. Cover
2. Executive summary (120 words)
3. Customer context — what we heard (1 page)
4. Sector signals — what we see (1-2 pages)
5. Sprint recommendation — named sprint, price, timeline (1 page)
6. Scope summary — bullets (1 page)
7. Exclusions — bullets (0.5 page)
8. Engagement gates — table (0.5 page)
9. Next step — Founder signature line (0.5 page)
10. Appendix — references, definitions, disclosure

The Diagnostic Report is a decision document. The customer reads it and either signs the linked proposal or declines. It is not a sales brochure.

## Sprint Outcome Report — structure

1. Cover
2. Executive summary — what was committed, what shipped (180 words)
3. Sprint scope recap (0.5 page)
4. What we did — week-by-week summary (2-3 pages)
5. What we found — sector-specific patterns surfaced (1-2 pages)
6. What we delivered — named assets with file references (1 page)
7. Proof Pack reference — link or appendix (1 page)
8. Recommendations — what to do next (1-2 pages, sorted Continue / Expand / Pause)
9. Approval gates fired during sprint — log (0.5 page)
10. Appendix — disclosure, definitions, version stamps

Every number cited in the Outcome Report must be sourced to a dated record. Estimated numbers are explicitly labeled "Estimated". Verified numbers are explicitly labeled "Verified" with the source.

## Sector Scorecard — structure

1. Cover
2. Methodology (1-2 pages) — mandatory, first content section
3. Sector definition and scope (1 page)
4. Sector ranking summary — table of the 11 dimensions (1 page) — see `docs/intelligence/SECTOR_RANKING_SYSTEM.md`
5. Buyer persona summary — anonymized (1-2 pages) — see `docs/intelligence/BUYER_PERSONA_SYSTEM.md`
6. Trigger events observed (1 page) — see `docs/intelligence/TRIGGER_EVENT_SYSTEM.md`
7. Competitive landscape (1-2 pages) — see `docs/intelligence/COMPETITIVE_INTELLIGENCE_SYSTEM.md`
8. Recommended Dealix engagement pattern (1 page)
9. Limitations and what we cannot claim (1 page) — mandatory
10. Appendix — references, disclosure

The Sector Scorecard contains aggregated patterns only. No customer names, no PII, no confidential metrics that could deanonymize a customer.

## Methodology section (Sector Scorecard mandatory)

Must answer:

- What data sources fed the scorecard?
- What date range does the data cover?
- What sample size?
- What was excluded and why?
- Who approved publication?

## Limitations section (Sector Scorecard mandatory)

Must answer:

- What can a reader NOT conclude from this report?
- What sample-size or recency caveats apply?
- What outcomes are estimated vs verified?

## Approval (trust gates)

| Report | Trust gate |
|---|---|
| Diagnostic Report | A2 — Founder + Brand Lead |
| Sprint Outcome Report | A2 — Founder + Brand Lead |
| Sector Scorecard | A3 — Founder only (public-facing) |

## Failure mode

- Sector Scorecard published without methodology section.
- Sprint Outcome Report cites a number without a source.
- Diagnostic Report sent without the exclusions section.

## Recovery path

1. Pull the report.
2. Restore the missing section.
3. Re-issue with a new report number.
