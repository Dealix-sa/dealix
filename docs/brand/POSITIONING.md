# Positioning

## Purpose

Define how Dealix is positioned in the market: a Saudi B2B AI Operating Systems company that builds operational AI systems for Saudi enterprises — not a chatbot, CRM, or marketing agency. Positioning is Saudi-first, PDPL-compliant, and approval-first.

## Owner

Founder / CEO with content lead.

## Daily Usage

- Reference this file before writing any external message.
- Check that every pitch, email, and page reflects this positioning.
- Use the canonical one-liner when describing Dealix.

## Inputs

- `docs/company/DEALIX_SOURCE_OF_TRUTH.md`
- Customer evidence and pain radar
- Market and competitor signals

## Outputs

- Canonical positioning statement
- One-liner and elevator pitch
- Category definition: Saudi B2B AI Operating Systems

## Workflow

1. Start from the canonical statement.
2. Adapt to audience (sector, role) without changing the core.
3. Check against CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md.
4. Approve before publish.
5. Review quarterly for drift.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Every external message reflects the canonical positioning.
- Dealix is never described as a chatbot, CRM, or marketing agency.
- No guaranteed revenue or fake claims.
- Positioning is consistent across AR and EN.

## Risks

- Drift toward agency/CRM/chatbot language.
- Over-promising outcomes.
- Inconsistency between AR and EN positioning.

## What Not To Do

- Do not claim guaranteed revenue or assured returns.
- Do not use fabricated testimonials or results.
- Do not describe Dealix as a chatbot, CRM, or marketing agency.
- Do not bypass PDPL or approval-first rules.
