# DEALIX LinkedIn Profile Guide

> Wordmark: **DEALIX**
> Tagline: **INTELLIGENT DEALS. REAL GROWTH.**

This document defines how Dealix presents on LinkedIn — the company page,
the founder profile, and the team profiles. LinkedIn is the platform
where Saudi B2B buyers, partners, and operators evaluate Dealix between
meetings. The profile is part of the sales process whether we like it
or not.

---

## 1. Stance

The Dealix LinkedIn presence is built to be **scannable and
defensible**. A buyer who lands on the company page or the founder
profile should be able to confirm three things in under 60 seconds:

1. We are a real Saudi B2B operator, not a marketing front.
2. We are sober — no guaranteed-revenue rhetoric, no logo wallpaper.
3. We are reachable — there is a clear path to a conversation.

If any of those three signals is missing, the profile is broken.

## 4. Founder profile — structure

The founder profile is one of the most-clicked pages in Dealix's first
year. It must read like a senior operator's profile — not a recruiter's,
not an influencer's, not an investor's.

### 4.1 Headline templates

The headline is 220 characters max. Pick the variant that fits the
current phase.

- **Building phase:** "Building Dealix · Saudi B2B Revenue OS ·
  Trust-gated execution"
- **Sales phase:** "Founder, Dealix · Saudi B2B Revenue OS · Closing
  deals with evidence, not promises"
- **Hiring phase:** "Founder, Dealix · Hiring senior operators in
  Riyadh"

Do **not** use:
- "AI entrepreneur" (vague).
- "Helping companies 10x revenue" (banned vocabulary).
- "Founder | CEO | Investor | Speaker | Author" (status laundry list).
- "Ex-[BigCorp]" as the primary identity if it is no longer relevant.

### 4.2 About section

A short, first-person bio. 4–6 paragraphs. Structure:

1. Who I am and where I am operating (Saudi B2B, [city]).
2. What I am building (Dealix in one paragraph).
3. What I have done before (the relevant 1–2 prior chapters).
4. What I am skeptical of (briefly — earns trust).
5. How to reach me (a single line; phone optional).

Avoid "I help X achieve Y" formulations. Operators do not write those.

### 4.3 Experience

- Dealix at the top, with the founder role and a one-paragraph
  description that matches the company About.
- Prior roles listed honestly, with their actual scope. Do not
  inflate titles.
- Dates accurate. Gaps allowed and not glossed over.

### 4.4 Featured

Pin 3 items:
- The company manifesto (LinkedIn post or article).
- The latest sector brief (PDF document post).
- A founder essay that reflects current thinking.

### 4.5 Avatar and banner

- Avatar: founder headshot, neutral background, sober. **Not** the
  Dealix logo.
- Banner: a Dealix-styled banner is acceptable. Either:
  - Deep Navy banner with small "DEALIX" wordmark and a sober line of
    copy: "Saudi B2B Revenue OS"; or
  - A neutral photograph (e.g. Riyadh skyline) with a Deep Navy
    overlay and the wordmark.

## 5. Founder posting cadence

The founder is the loudest brand voice on LinkedIn. Cadence guidance:

- 2 operator essays per week.
- 1 sector signal or stat per week.
- 1 customer announcement per quarter (only with approval logged).
- Replies to comments within 24 hours when possible.

The founder does not:
- Repost generic motivational quotes.
- Tag other people for visibility without a real reason.
- Run paid "personal brand" ads.
- Use comment-pod patterns or engagement bait.

## 6. Team profiles

Team members keep their own profiles. Brand guidance for team profiles
is light but firm:

- Headline includes "Dealix" and a clear role (e.g. "Customer Success
  at Dealix · Saudi B2B Revenue OS").
- Banner: optional Dealix-styled banner; not required.
- About: in their own voice. They reference Dealix but the page is
  about them.
- Posts: their own posts. Dealix does not script team posts. Brand
  rules still apply: no guaranteed-revenue claims, no unapproved
  customer references.

Team members do **not** copy-paste the company About into their own
profile. That looks scripted and reduces trust.

## 7. Recruiter outreach posture

LinkedIn is also where recruiting happens. Dealix posture:

- Hiring posts use the role template (`DEALIX_SOCIAL_MEDIA_KIT.md`
  section 5.4).
- DMs are answered, not ignored. We do not ghost candidates.
- We do not list "great culture" or "fast-paced startup" — we describe
  the work.

For inbound recruiter DMs (recruiters trying to hire from Dealix), the
founder reply is polite and brief: "Thanks. Not currently exploring.
Happy to keep in touch."

## 8. LinkedIn-specific copy patterns

### 8.1 Featured article structure

A long-form LinkedIn article (1000–1800 words):

- Title in Inter Bold equivalent (LinkedIn uses its own type).
- Sub-title with the central claim.
- Body: 5–8 sections, each with a sub-heading.
- A "What this means for you" closing section.
- A short About paragraph at the end.

### 8.2 Document post structure

A 5–10 page PDF document post (carousel-equivalent):

- Slide 1: the question.
- Slide 2: the pattern.
- Slides 3–N: the data.
- Penultimate slide: the implication.
- Final slide: the CTA (one line).

### 8.3 Comment etiquette

- Reply to substantive comments within 24 hours.
- Disagree on substance, not on person.
- Tag a person only when their voice is genuinely needed.

## 9. What we do not put on LinkedIn

- "Guaranteed [revenue / sales / meetings]" — any phrasing, any
  language.
- "Trusted by leading Saudi enterprises" without named, approved
  customers.
- Screenshots of internal dashboards with real customer data
  unblurred.
- Quotes from customers we have not gotten written approval from.
- Politically inflammatory content. We are a Saudi B2B operator
  building software — we stay focused.

---

## 10. Crisis and correction

If a published LinkedIn post is incorrect:

1. Edit the post with a visible correction note ("Correction: an
   earlier version of this post said X; corrected to Y on [date].").
2. Or delete and republish if the error is structural.
3. Log the correction in the social log.

We do not silently delete or silently edit. Saudi B2B buyers notice.
