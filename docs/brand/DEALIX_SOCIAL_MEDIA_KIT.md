# DEALIX Social Media Kit

> Wordmark: **DEALIX**
> Tagline: **INTELLIGENT DEALS. REAL GROWTH.**

This document defines how Dealix appears on social platforms — LinkedIn, X,
and any open-graph share. It covers template sizes, copy patterns, posting
rhythm, and the rules that keep Dealix's tone on social sober and
defensible.

Critical: Dealix does not auto-post. Every social asset is reviewed and
published by a human. We do not run automation that posts on behalf of
customers, partners, or founders.

---

## 1. Platform stance

| Platform | Primary use | Cadence | Voice |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| LinkedIn | Operator essays, sector insight, hiring | 2–3 / week | Calm, specific, operator-to-operator |
| X | Short signal, link-out to long-form | 0–3 / week | Sharper, brief |
| YouTube | Recorded sessions, walkthroughs | Episodic | Conversational, sober |
| Open Graph | Share previews | Per page | Same as the page |

Other platforms (Instagram, TikTok, Threads) are not currently in the
Dealix social mix. Adding one requires brand director approval and an
explicit plan.

## 2. Profile assets

### 2.1 Company page (LinkedIn)
- Avatar: monogram, white on Deep Navy, 400×400 px.
- Banner: wordmark + tagline on Deep Navy, 1584×396 px, optional teal
  swoosh in lower right.
- Bio: 1–2 sentences. Example: "Dealix is the Saudi B2B Revenue
  Operating System. Trust-gated execution. Approval-first by default."
- About: short paragraph describing what Dealix does, in EN and AR.
  No customer logos in the About section unless logos are approved.

### 2.2 Company page (X)
- Avatar: monogram, white on Deep Navy, 400×400 px.
- Banner: wordmark + tagline on Deep Navy, 1500×500 px.
- Bio: 1 sentence. Example: "Saudi B2B Revenue Operating System.
  Trust-gated. Operator-built."

### 2.3 Founder profile (LinkedIn)
- Avatar: founder's headshot (not the Dealix logo).
- Banner: a Dealix-styled banner (Deep Navy, monogram + small wordmark)
  is acceptable but optional.
- Headline template: see `DEALIX_LINKEDIN_PROFILE_GUIDE.md`.

## 4. Visual template patterns

Dealix social posts follow a small number of visual patterns. Picking one
and holding it within a post-series creates recognition.

### 4.1 The thesis post
- Deep Navy background, full bleed.
- One sentence in Inter Bold `4xl`, max 14 words, centered.
- Small "DEALIX" wordmark in the bottom-left, tagline beneath in `xs`.
- No image, no chart.

### 4.2 The stat card
- Deep Navy background.
- Headline number in Emerald Teal, Inter Bold `5xl`.
- Topic label in Soft Silver, Inter Medium `sm`.
- Denominator or disclosure in Soft Silver, Inter Regular `xs`.
- Monogram in corner.
- A stat card without a denominator does not ship.

### 4.3 The quote card
- Slate background.
- Quote in Inter Medium `xl`, white.
- Attribution in Inter Regular `sm`, Soft Silver.
- Customer logo at 40% opacity in corner (only with logged approval).
- Monogram opposite corner.
- Quotes never published without proof-ledger approval.

### 4.4 The diagram post
- Single horizontal diagram.
- Nodes on Slate, edges Soft Silver / Emerald Teal.
- Title above the diagram in Inter Semibold `lg`.
- Read in the post's locale direction (LTR or RTL).

### 4.5 The carousel
- 5–10 slides, square (1080×1350 on LinkedIn).
- Slide 1: hook (one sentence).
- Slides 2–N: one idea per slide.
- Final slide: CTA — read more, share, or "DM to discuss".
- Each slide carries the monogram and slide number.

## 5. Copy patterns

### 5.1 The operator essay (LinkedIn)
A short essay — 5–10 short paragraphs, 600–1200 characters total — that:

1. Opens with a specific observation from the Saudi B2B market.
2. Names the pattern most people miss.
3. Gives a concrete example (anonymized unless approved).
4. Closes with a question or a one-line takeaway.

Do not end with "Thoughts?" or "DM me to learn more". End with a real
question that you actually want answered.

### 5.2 The signal tweet (X)
Under 240 characters. One observation. One link. No hashtag spam (one
hashtag max, only if it carries meaning).

### 5.3 The sector micro-brief (LinkedIn document)
A 3–5 page PDF carousel that:
- Names the sector and the time window.
- Gives 3–5 data points with denominators.
- States the limitation ("n=12 deals, Q1 2026, Riyadh + Jeddah").
- Ends with a sober conclusion, not a sales pitch.

### 5.4 The hiring post
- Name the role and the team.
- Name the compensation band or refer to a public range.
- Describe the work, not the perks.
- Single CTA — apply via the careers page link.

### 5.5 The customer announcement
Only published with the customer's written approval logged in the proof
ledger. The post:
- Names what the customer accomplished, not what Dealix did.
- Includes a quote when the customer has approved the quote.
- Avoids "Dealix transformed their business". Says "in 14 days, X
  shipped Y, Z, and W with us."

## 6. What we do not post

- Customer names, logos, or quotes without written approval logged.
- "Guaranteed revenue / sales / meetings" copy, in any phrasing.
- Sub-tweets at competitors. We name our category, not theirs.
- Memes that depend on hype vocabulary or industry slang.
- Auto-generated AI content presented as human-written.
- Generic motivational quotes attributed to "anonymous".

## 7. Posting rhythm

| Day | LinkedIn | X |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mon | Operator essay | 0–1 signal |
| Tue | Stat / diagram | 0–1 signal |
| Wed | Sector micro-brief or quiet | 0 |
| Thu | Operator essay | 0–1 link-out |
| Fri | Customer announcement (when available) | 0 |

The rhythm is a default, not a quota. We do not post just to post. If a
draft does not pass the on-brand checklist, it stays in draft.

---

## 8. Approval workflow (manual, not automated)

1. Author drafts the post in the team doc.
2. Author runs the on-brand checklist:
   - No banned vocabulary.
   - No unauthorized customer reference.
   - Denominator on any metric.
   - Bilingual version if customer-facing.
   - Asset attached and sized correctly.
3. Brand director reviews.
4. Founder reviews if the post references a customer, a metric, or a
   product claim.
5. Author publishes manually at the planned time. **We do not use a
   third-party scheduler that posts without a human click.**
6. Author records the publication in the social log with a link.

---

## 9. Hashtags and tagging

- One hashtag maximum per post.
- Hashtags must carry meaning (e.g. `#SaudiB2B`) and not be filler
  (`#Innovation`, `#Hustle`).
- Tag a person only if you have permission or if they are a public
  account with a clear reason to be tagged.
- Tag a customer only with logged approval.

---

## 10. Image alt text

Every social image has alt text. The alt text:
- Is written in the post's locale (Arabic for Arabic post, English for
  English post).
- Describes the image in plain language.
- Is not a marketing tagline.

Example, good: "Bar chart titled 'Pipeline lift by sector, n=12, Q1
2026'. Logistics leads with the largest lift; healthcare second; SaaS
third."

Example, bad: "Dealix grows your pipeline!"

---

## 11. Crisis and escalation

If a published post turns out to be incorrect:

1. Update the post (or delete and republish) with a correction note.
2. Log the correction in the social log.
3. If the error involves a customer or a regulator, notify founder and
   legal immediately.

We do not silently delete posts. Corrections are public.
