# Visual Direction

## Purpose

Define the visual direction for Dealix: confident, precise, Saudi-first, enterprise-grade. Visuals support the positioning as a Saudi B2B AI Operating Systems company — not a consumer chatbot or marketing agency.

## Owner

Founder / CEO with design lead.

## Daily Usage

- Reference before creating any public visual asset.
- Check that visuals match the positioning and voice.
- Approve all visuals manually before publish.

## Inputs

- `docs/brand/POSITIONING.md`
- `docs/brand/VOICE_AND_TONE.md`
- Existing visual identity files in `docs/brand/`

## Outputs

- Visual direction principles
- Guidance for website, decks, and social
- Approval checklist for visuals

## Workflow

1. Define asset purpose and audience.
2. Apply visual direction principles.
3. Check against positioning and voice.
4. Approve before publish.
5. Log approved visuals.

## Visual Principles

- Confident, enterprise-grade, not playful.
- Saudi-first sensibility; Arabic typography respected.
- Clear data visualization over decoration.
- Evidence-led: show real dashboards and proof, not stock hype.
- Consistent color, type, and spacing across channels.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Visuals match positioning and voice.
- No visuals imply guaranteed revenue or fake results.
- All public visuals are approved and dated.
- Visuals are consistent across AR and EN.

## Risks

- Decorative or hype-driven visuals dilute positioning.
- Inconsistency between AR and EN visuals.
- Using stock imagery that implies fake results.

## What Not To Do

- Do not imply guaranteed revenue or assured returns.
- Do not use fabricated testimonials or results in visuals.
- Do not describe Dealix as a chatbot, CRM, or marketing agency in visuals.
- Do not publish visuals without approval.
