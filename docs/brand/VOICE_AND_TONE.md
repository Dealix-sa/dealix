# Voice and Tone

## Purpose

Define how Dealix sounds across every channel and language. The voice is confident, precise, Saudi-first, and approval-first. It speaks to B2B operators and executives — not consumers. It never over-promises.

## Owner

Founder / CEO with content lead.

## Daily Usage

- Apply this voice to every draft before approval.
- Check tone consistency across AR and EN.
- Reject any copy that sounds like hype, guarantees, or agency-speak.

## Inputs

- `docs/brand/POSITIONING.md`
- `docs/brand/CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md`
- Customer evidence

## Outputs

- Voice principles
- Tone guidance per channel (email, website, sales, support)
- Examples of correct and incorrect copy

## Workflow

1. Identify channel and audience.
2. Draft using the voice principles below.
3. Check against CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md.
4. Approve before publish.

## Voice Principles

- Confident, not boastful.
- Precise, not vague.
- Saudi-first, Arabic-native where appropriate.
- Approval-first: never publish without human approval.
- B2B operator tone: clear, direct, evidence-based.
- No guarantees, no hype, no agency-speak.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Copy is confident and precise.
- No guaranteed revenue or fake claims.
- Tone is consistent across AR and EN.
- Copy is approved before publish.

## Risks

- Hype creeping into copy.
- Inconsistency between AR and EN.
- Agency-speak diluting positioning.

## What Not To Do

- Do not claim guaranteed revenue or assured returns.
- Do not use fabricated testimonials or results.
- Do not describe Dealix as a chatbot, CRM, or marketing agency.
- Do not publish without approval.
