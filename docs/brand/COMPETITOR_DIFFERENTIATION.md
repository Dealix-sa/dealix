# Competitor Differentiation

## Purpose

Define how Dealix is different from competitors in the Saudi B2B market. Dealix is a Saudi B2B AI Operating Systems company, not a chatbot, CRM, or marketing agency. Differentiation is Saudi-first, PDPL-compliant, and approval-first.

## Owner

Founder / CEO with content lead.

## Daily Usage

- Reference before making any competitive claim.
- Check competitive claims against CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md.
- Update this file as competitors and market evolve.

## Inputs

- Market & Competitor Watch OS signals
- Customer evidence
- `docs/brand/POSITIONING.md`

## Outputs

- Differentiation matrix
- Approved competitive talking points
- Counter-positioning guidance

## Workflow

1. Identify the competitor category (CRM, chatbot, agency, generic AI tool).
2. Map Dealix differentiators against that category.
3. Draft talking points in the Dealix voice.
4. Check against CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md.
5. Approve before use.

## Differentiation (summary)

- Dealix builds operational AI systems, not chatbots or CRMs.
- Saudi-first, Arabic-native, PDPL-compliant by design.
- Approval-first: every send is human-approved.
- Evidence-led: Executive Proof Packs, not marketing fluff.
- B2B enterprise focus, not consumer.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Competitive claims are factual and verifiable.
- No guaranteed revenue or fake claims.
- Dealix is never described as a chatbot, CRM, or agency.
- All competitive claims are approved before use.

## Risks

- Overstating competitor weaknesses.
- Drifting into agency/CRM/chatbot comparisons that dilute positioning.
- Using unverified competitor claims.

## What Not To Do

- Do not claim guaranteed revenue or assured returns.
- Do not use fabricated testimonials or results.
- Do not describe Dealix as a chatbot, CRM, or marketing agency.
- Do not make unverified competitor claims.
- Do not bypass PDPL or approval-first rules.
