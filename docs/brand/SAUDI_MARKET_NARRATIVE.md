# Saudi Market Narrative

## Purpose

Define the narrative Dealix tells in the Saudi market: a local AI Operating Systems company built for Saudi B2B enterprises, PDPL-compliant, approval-first, Arabic-native. The narrative is evidence-led and never over-promises.

## Owner

Founder / CEO with content lead.

## Daily Usage

- Reference before any Saudi-market communication.
- Ensure the narrative is consistent across AR and EN.
- Check against CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md.

## Inputs

- `docs/brand/POSITIONING.md`
- `docs/company/SAUDI_B2B_STRATEGY_AR.md`
- Customer evidence in Saudi sectors
- PDPL requirements

## Outputs

- Saudi market narrative (AR and EN)
- Sector talking points
- Compliance messaging (PDPL, approval-first)

## Workflow

1. Identify the Saudi sector and audience.
2. Apply the narrative below.
3. Add sector-specific evidence from real proof packs.
4. Check against CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md.
5. Approve before publish.

## Narrative

- Dealix is a Saudi B2B AI Operating Systems company.
- Built for Saudi enterprises, Arabic-first, PDPL-compliant.
- Every action is approval-first — human approval before any send.
- Evidence-led: we show real results, not marketing fluff.
- We build operating systems, not chatbots, CRMs, or marketing campaigns.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Narrative is consistent across AR and EN.
- No guaranteed revenue or fake claims.
- PDPL and approval-first are always mentioned.
- Dealix is never described as a chatbot, CRM, or agency.
- All sector claims are real and verifiable.

## Risks

- Narrative drift toward agency/CRM language.
- Over-promising outcomes in sector messaging.
- Inconsistency between AR and EN narrative.

## What Not To Do

- Do not claim guaranteed revenue or assured returns.
- Do not use fabricated testimonials or results.
- Do not describe Dealix as a chatbot, CRM, or marketing agency.
- Do not bypass PDPL or approval-first rules.

Review sector talking points this week and align them with this narrative.
