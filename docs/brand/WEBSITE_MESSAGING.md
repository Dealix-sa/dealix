# Website Messaging

## Purpose

Define the messaging architecture for the Dealix website: hero, value props, proof, and calls to action. The website must position Dealix as a Saudi B2B AI Operating Systems company — Saudi-first, PDPL-compliant, approval-first.

## Owner

Founder / CEO with content lead.

## Daily Usage

- Reference before editing any website page.
- Check every page against CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md.
- Approve all copy changes before publish.

## Inputs

- `docs/brand/POSITIONING.md`
- `docs/brand/VOICE_AND_TONE.md`
- `docs/brand/CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md`
- Customer evidence and proof packs

## Outputs

- Hero messaging
- Value prop blocks
- Proof section guidance
- CTA hierarchy

## Workflow

1. Map the page to its audience and intent.
2. Draft hero and value props in the Dealix voice.
3. Check against CLAIMS_GUARDRAILS.md.
4. Add proof from real Executive Proof Packs only.
5. Approve before publish.
6. Review monthly for accuracy.

## Messaging Architecture

- Hero: canonical one-liner (Saudi B2B AI Operating Systems).
- Value props: the operating systems Dealix builds.
- Proof: real, verifiable evidence — no fabricated results.
- CTA: book a diagnostic, request a proof pack.
- Compliance: PDPL and approval-first noted clearly.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Every page reflects canonical positioning.
- No guaranteed revenue or fake claims.
- All proof is real and verifiable.
- All copy is approved before publish.
- Messaging is consistent across AR and EN.

## Risks

- Hero drift toward chatbot/CRM/agency language.
- Proof section using fabricated results.
- Inconsistency between AR and EN pages.

## What Not To Do

- Do not claim guaranteed revenue or assured returns.
- Do not use fabricated testimonials or results.
- Do not describe Dealix as a chatbot, CRM, or marketing agency.
- Do not publish without approval.
