# DEALIX Brand Voice

**Owner:** Brand Lead + Founder
**Source of truth:** This doc + `docs/00_constitution/`

## Voice in one sentence

Confident, founder-led, evidence-first. No hype. No magic. No guarantees.

## Voice attributes

| Attribute | What it sounds like | What it does not sound like |
|---|---|---|
| Confident | "Dealix runs the deal flow loop." | "Dealix might possibly help with..." |
| Founder-led | "I sign off on every proposal." | "Our AI handles everything for you." |
| Evidence-first | "Sprint 14 produced 23 qualified accounts." | "We've helped hundreds of businesses." |
| Decisive | "Sprint scope is fixed. Out-of-scope is a separate sprint." | "We can be flexible with scope." |
| Direct | "Cost is SAR 15,000. Approval gate is the Founder." | "Pricing varies based on your needs." |

## Banned phrases

Never use any of these in customer-facing copy:

- "AI-powered" / "powered by AI"
- "Transform your business"
- "Supercharge", "turbocharge", "next-level"
- "Guaranteed sales", "guaranteed results", "نضمن مبيعات"
- "Game-changer", "revolutionary", "disrupt"
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Cutting-edge", "state-of-the-art", "best-in-class"
- "Synergy", "leverage" (as a verb)
- "Hassle-free", "effortless"
- "Trusted by thousands" (unless verifiable)
- Brand or model names of the underlying language model

## Approved replacements

| Banned | Approved |
|---|---|
| "AI-powered outreach" | "Drafted outreach, queued for founder approval" |
| "Guaranteed leads" | "Evidenced opportunities" / "فرص مُثبتة بأدلة" |
| "Transform your sales" | "Restructure your deal flow" |
| "Effortless" | "Operator-light" |
| "Best-in-class" | "Built for Saudi B2B" |
| "Unlock growth" | "Open the next sprint" |

## Sentence-level rules

1. Lead with the noun. "Sprint output is X." not "What you get is..."
2. Use active voice. "Founder approves" not "Approval is given by the Founder".
3. State the number. "Three sectors, 90 days, SAR 45,000." not "A flexible engagement model."
4. State the limit. "Up to 40 accounts per sprint."
5. State the gate. "Approval class A2 — Founder signs off before send."

## Bilingual rule

When the audience includes Saudi business readers, AR and EN sections must be parallel:

- Same structure
- Same length (within 15 percent)
- Same numbers
- Same disclosures

Never write an AR section that is shorter, vaguer, or softer than its EN counterpart.

## Disclosure rule

Every customer-facing markdown that mentions value, ROI, savings, revenue uplift, or pipeline impact must end with:

> Estimated value is not Verified value / القيمة التقديرية ليست قيمة مُتحقَّقة

No exceptions.

## Tone variations by surface

| Surface | Tone |
|---|---|
| Landing page H1 | Decisive, one-line, no qualifier |
| Proposal body | Founder-readable, evidence-led |
| Deck speaker notes | Conversational, founder-first-person |
| Sector report | Methodology-led, aggregated, no PII |
| LinkedIn post | Founder voice, one idea, one number, one CTA |
| Internal SOP | Operator-readable, imperative verbs |

## Founder voice

When the founder writes in first person:

- "I" is fine. "We" implies a team we may not have at the named stage.
- Name the trust gate explicitly. "I review every send."
- Name the limit explicitly. "I cap concurrent sprints at three."

## Voice checklist (run before any external publish)

1. Are any banned phrases present? Remove.
2. Is there a guarantee claim? Replace with evidenced language.
3. Are numbers cited and dated?
4. Is the bilingual section parallel?
5. Is the disclosure present?
6. Is the trust gate named?

## Failure mode

- Marketing copy reads like a SaaS template.
- AR section is half the length of the EN section.
- A guarantee phrase slips into a proposal.

## Recovery path

1. Re-run the voice checklist.
2. Swap to approved replacements.
3. If the asset has already been sent, send a corrected version with a one-line note: "Replaces prior version dated [DATE]."

## Disclaimer

Dealix does not guarantee revenue, meetings, or conversion outcomes. Brand voice exists to make that fact unmistakable.
