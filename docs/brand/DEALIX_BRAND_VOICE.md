# Dealix Brand Voice | صوت العلامة

> Dealix sounds like the calmest, most prepared operator in the room. Bilingual native. Evidence-led. Never hyped.

---

## 1. Voice in One Line | الصوت في سطر

**English:** Precise, calm, growth-honest, locally fluent.
**Arabic:** دقيق، هادئ، صادق في النمو، فصيح محلياً.

If a sentence reads like a generic AI startup, it is not Dealix.

---

## 2. Voice Attributes | السمات

| Attribute | We sound like… | We do not sound like… |
|---|---|---|
| Precise | "10 qualified meetings in 30 days, gated by your approval." | "Massive pipeline acceleration." |
| Calm | "Here is the next step." | "Don't miss out — limited time!" |
| Growth-honest | "Pilots typically produce 6–12 qualified meetings; your range will depend on ICP density." | "Guaranteed 5x ROI." |
| Locally fluent | "Aligned to the Saudi B2B buying cycle and Hijri/Gregorian planning." | "World-class global solution for any market." |
| Operator-first | "Approve, decline, or edit each draft before it sends." | "Set it and forget it." |

## 3. Tone by Surface | النبرة حسب السطح

| Surface | Tone | Example opener |
|---|---|---|
| Landing hero | Direct, confident | "Turn warm relationships into intelligent deals." |
| Product UI | Functional, brief | "12 drafts ready for your approval." |
| Email outreach | Personal, specific | "Saw your team is hiring 3 BDRs — quick thought:" |
| Sales deck | Evidence-led | "What pilots ship: 10 qualified meetings, 1 approved proof pack." |
| Customer support | Empathetic, exact | "Got it — here's what we'll do, and what we need from you." |
| Investor / partner | Strategic, restrained | "Dealix is the approval-gated Revenue OS for Saudi B2B." |

---

## 4. Bilingual Principles | مبادئ ثنائية اللغة

- Arabic copy is **written natively**, not translated. We hire and brief Arabic writers; we do not run English through a translator.
- The Arabic version is **equal in length, scannability, and rhythm** to the English — no afterthought paragraphs.
- Brand terms ("Dealix," "Revenue OS," "Founder Console") stay in Latin script even inside Arabic copy, with bidi isolation.
- Numerals: Western Arabic numerals (0–9) by default; Eastern only in editorial.

## 6. Don't-Say Phrases | عبارات نتجنبها

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "Guaranteed revenue / guaranteed ROI" | Non-negotiable: no guaranteed revenue claims. |
| "Fully autonomous" / "AI does it for you" | Violates approval-gating posture. |
| "We'll send for you" | Violates no-external-sending rule. |
| "Revolutionary" / "Magical" / "Game-changing" | Hype words; not operator voice. |
| "10x your sales" / specific multiplier promises | Unsubstantiated. |
| "Best in class" / "World's leading" | Unprovable. |
| "Cheapest" / "Lowest price" | Off-brand; we compete on outcome and trust. |
| "Trust us" | Trust is shown, not claimed. |

## 7. Voice Patterns | الأنماط

**The Dealix Sentence Pattern**

1. **What** the customer gets.
2. **How** approval is preserved.
3. **By when** or with what evidence.

Example: "Ten qualified meetings, drafted for your approval, within 30 days — with a full proof pack you sign off before publication."

**The Dealix Headline Pattern**

`<Outcome verb> + <noun the customer cares about> + <trust qualifier>`

- "Turn warm relationships into intelligent deals — with your approval on every send."
- "Run a 30-day Revenue Sprint — without a single auto-sent email."

---

## 8. Microcopy Conventions | الكتابة المصغرة

- Buttons: verbs first. "Approve draft," "Queue for review," "Open deal."
- Empty states: explain + offer one next step. Never just "No data."
- Error states: state what happened + the next step + who to contact.
- Confirmations: name the action and the resulting state. "Draft queued for your approval."
- Approval prompts: always show what will happen if approved **and** if declined.

---

## 9. Numbers & Claims | الأرقام والادعاءات

- Use **ranges**, not point estimates: "6–12 qualified meetings," not "10 meetings."
- Anchor numbers to **conditions**: "in 30 days, with ICP density ≥ X."
- Cite **source or pilot N**: "across 7 Saudi B2B pilots."
- Never round up. Never extrapolate from a single customer to a category claim.

---

## 10. Editing Checklist | قائمة التحرير

Before any copy ships:

- [ ] Does it pass the five non-negotiables? (No external sending, no proof publish without approval, no guaranteed revenue, no pricing/contract commitments without escalation, approval-gated only.)
- [ ] Is the Arabic native, not translated?
- [ ] Are numbers ranges with conditions and sources?
- [ ] Is there at least one verb that names the customer outcome?
- [ ] Are there zero hype words?
- [ ] Does the CTA name the action and its trust gate?

---

## 11. Governance | الحوكمة

- **Owner:** Brand Lead + Content Lead.
- **Approval gate:** Customer-facing copy reviewed against the editing checklist before publish.
- **Voice audit:** Monthly sample of 20 shipped pieces; flagged violations re-edited within the same week.
