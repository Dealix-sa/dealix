# Dealix Brand Voice

## 1. Voice attributes

| Attribute | Means | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Calm | We don't shout. | "Here is what we found. Here is what to approve." |
| Specific | Numbers, names, mechanisms. | "8 accounts scored ≥ 80. Top 3 below." |
| Founder-grade | Speaks operator to operator. | "You can ship this in one approval." |
| Trust-first | We always say what is and isn't automated. | "Drafted for your review — not sent." |
| Local + global | Saudi context, enterprise rigor. | "PDPL-aware. Approval-first." |

## 2. Voice anti-patterns

- ❌ "Revolutionary AI that 10x your sales overnight."
- ❌ "Guaranteed 30% pipeline lift in 90 days."
- ❌ "Set it and forget it."
- ❌ "Fully autonomous outbound at scale."
- ❌ Emoji-led marketing copy.

These violate the **no guaranteed claims** and **no uncontrolled
external automation** non-negotiables.

## 3. Banned claim patterns (regex-checked)

The brand verifier rejects any artifact containing:

- `guarantee[d]? (revenue|sales|results|leads|pipeline)`
- `\d+x (your|in) (sales|revenue|pipeline)`
- `fully autonomous (outbound|sales|sending)`
- `unlimited (sends|outreach|automation)`
- `set (it|and) forget (it)?`

## 4. Tone by surface

| Surface | Tone | Length |
|---|---|---|
| Landing hero | Direct, value-first. | 1 sentence + 1 sub. |
| Founder console | Operator shorthand. | Labels, not paragraphs. |
| Proposal cover | Confident, specific. | 3 bullets + scope table. |
| Email draft | Personal, low-pressure. | 70–120 words. |
| Social post | Insight first, link last. | 60–150 words. |
| Audit log | Neutral, machine-readable. | Field-style. |

## 5. Bilingual voice

Arabic copy mirrors English — same pillars, same restraint. It is
never a translation; it's a re-write that lands the same operator
message in idiomatic Saudi business Arabic.

| EN | AR |
|---|---|
| Intelligent Deals. Real Growth. | صفقات ذكية. نمو حقيقي. |
| Drafted, not sent. Founder-approved. | مسودة بانتظار اعتمادك — لم تُرسل. |
| Score ≥ 80 → priority list | نتيجة ≥ 80 → قائمة أولوية |
