# Dealix Brand Voice

How Dealix sounds when it writes, speaks, or replies. Apply to every customer-touching surface: landing copy, proposals, decks, emails, console microcopy, console error messages.

## 1. Voice attributes

| Attribute       | Means                                                                          |
|-----------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Direct          | Short sentences. State the result, then the why. Avoid throat-clearing.        |
| Trust-first     | Always say what we will NOT do. Always say where the human approval is.        |
| Numerate        | Lead with numbers. Before/after metrics. Concrete deltas, not adjectives.      |
| Bilingual       | AR and EN side-by-side, not auto-translated. Each language stands on its own.  |
| Operator-grade  | Talk like a CRO/CFO/Head-of-Sales would. Not like a SaaS marketer.             |
| Calm            | No exclamation marks. No urgency tricks. No countdown timers in copy.          |

## 2. Tone by surface

| Surface                    | Tone                                          |
|----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Landing hero               | Confident, one promise, one verifiable claim  |
| Pricing                    | Transparent. State what's included, in numbers|
| Proposal                   | Operator-to-operator. Bilingual side-by-side  |
| Email outreach (draft)     | Polite, specific, short. < 110 words English. |
| LinkedIn outreach (draft)  | Polite, specific, no auto-pitch in first msg  |
| Console microcopy          | Imperative ("Approve", "Hold", "Kill")        |
| Console errors             | Plain: what happened, what to do next         |

## 3. Words to use

- **Trust-gated** — when describing automation that requires human approval.
- **Approval queue** — what the founder reviews before any external action.
- **Revenue intelligence** — the category we play in.
- **Sample / Proof Pack** — the artefact a prospect gets to verify our work.
- **Sprint** — a time-boxed engagement (typically 7 days).
- **Operating system** — what Dealix is. Not "platform", not "tool".
- **AI-assisted** — what our agents are. Never "fully autonomous".
- **Founder-approved** — what every outbound action is, before it goes out.

## 4. Words to avoid

| Avoid                            | Reason                                              | Use instead                  |
|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Guaranteed revenue / sales       | We do not guarantee outcomes; doctrine violation    | "measurable revenue lift"    |
| Fully autonomous                 | We do not run uncontrolled outbound                 | "AI-assisted, trust-gated"   |
| AI that sells for you            | Implies no human control                            | "AI that drafts, you approve"|
| Game-changing                    | Empty SaaS adjective                                | State the metric             |
| Disruptive                       | Empty SaaS adjective                                | State the metric             |
| Revolutionise / 10x / unlock     | Hype words; refused by verifier                     | Concrete number or delta     |
| Synergy / leverage (as verb)     | Corporate filler                                    | Use plain English            |
| Cutting-edge / best-in-class     | Unverifiable superlatives                           | State the source of evidence |
| World's first / only             | Unprovable                                          | Drop or attribute            |

## 5. Sentence patterns

### Hero / claim

> Intelligent Deals. Real Growth.
> Saudi B2B revenue intelligence with AI assistance and human approval on every outbound action.

### Pricing line

> Revenue Sprint — 499 SAR. 7 days. Diagnostic + Proof Pack + founder-reviewed outreach drafts. No external sending without approval.

### Refusal copy (when a request violates doctrine)

> We don't do that. Here's why: [reason]. Here's what we *can* do for the same outcome: [alternative].

### Approval prompt (console)

> 12 outbound drafts ready for review. Approve, edit, or hold. Nothing leaves the system until you decide.

## 6. Bilingual rules

- **Side-by-side, not stacked translation.** Proposals show AR on one column, EN on the other.
- **Localise the metric.** Prices in SAR, not USD. Timestamps in Asia/Riyadh.
- **Don't auto-translate idioms.** Re-write for the local register.
- **Numbers stay LTR** even inside RTL text.

## 7. Failure modes the verifier catches

- Banned phrases (guaranteed, fully autonomous, 10x, revolutionise…).
- Exclamation marks in brand documents.
- Emoji in brand documents.
- English-only proposal templates.
- Tagline typeset incorrectly.
