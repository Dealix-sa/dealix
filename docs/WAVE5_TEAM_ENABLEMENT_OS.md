## Executive Summary

Wave 5 establishes the **Team Enablement OS** — the organizational and operational framework that scales Dealix from founder-led to sustainable team-based company. This OS codifies roles, responsibilities, hiring specs, onboarding processes, decision-making authority, and daily/weekly/monthly operations.

**Key deliverables**:
- Complete organizational chart + role descriptions (now through Year 3)
- Hiring roadmap + recruitment specs
- Comprehensive onboarding manual (30/60/90-day plans)
- Decision-making authority matrix (who decides what?)
- Weekly/monthly/quarterly operations schedule

- Compensation framework

**Organizational impact**: Scale from 1 person to 8–12 person team; maintain startup culture

## 1. Dealix Organizational Philosophy

### 1.1 Core Operating Principles

**Ownership & Accountability**:
- Every role has clear decision authority
- Failure is learning, not blame
- Success is celebrated publicly
- Accountability is individual, not committee

**Transparency & Communication**:
- All decisions documented (why, not just what)
- Weekly updates shared with full team
- Customer feedback shared unfiltered
- Financial performance visible to team

**Speed & Bias to Action**:
- "Done" beats perfect (iterate, don't analyze forever)
- Decisions made with 80% information (not waiting for 100%)
- Small experiments over big planning
- Founder empowers team to move fast

**Customer-First**:
- Every decision evaluated against customer impact
- Team visits customers regularly (every person)
- NPS + churn are team OKRs (shared responsibility)
- Customer feedback shapes priorities

**Lean & Sustainable**:
- No unnecessary bureaucracy
- Hiring only when clearly needed (not for growth's sake)
- Work-life balance (no 80-hour weeks)
- Profitability from Day 1 (not burning VC cash)

### 1.2 Remote-First Operations

**Current state**: Founder in Riyadh; future team likely distributed

**Policies**:
- All meetings recorded (absent team members catch up)
- Async-first communication (email > Slack > meetings)
- Timezone overlap required (core hours: 9am–2pm Riyadh time)
- Monthly in-person gathering (all hands in Riyadh or customer site)
- No meeting before 9am Saudi time

## 4. Comprehensive Onboarding Program

### 4.1 Pre-Hire Preparation (Before Day 1)

**4 weeks before start**:
- [ ] Offer letter signed + start date confirmed
- [ ] Background check completed
- [ ] IT equipment ordered (laptop, phone, monitors if onsite)
- [ ] Onboarding buddy assigned (usually tech lead or founder)

**1 week before start**:
- [ ] Email sent with welcome message + first-day agenda
- [ ] Send reading list (CLAUDE.md, DEALIX_BUSINESS_MODEL.md, role-specific docs)
- [ ] Set up email + GitHub + Slack + CRM access
- [ ] Prepare workspace (desk, equipment, parking if onsite)
- [ ] Calendar invites sent for first week meetings

**Day 0 (afternoon before/morning of)**:
- [ ] IT setup (laptop, phone, accounts)
- [ ] Give tour (office, facilities if onsite; or Zoom walkaround if remote)
- [ ] Welcome from founder (30 min, casual chat + excitement)
- [ ] Meet team (informal lunch or coffee chat)

### 4.2 30-Day Onboarding Plan (Month 1)

**Goal**: Person understands role, context, systems; can execute with guidance

#### **Week 1: Context & Basics**

**Daily schedule**:
- 9am: Team standup (observe, don't speak unless asked)
- 10am–12pm: Onboarding activities
- 12pm–1pm: Lunch (with team)
- 1pm–3pm: Independent learning
- 3pm–5pm: Meetings + pair activities

**Week 1 Checklist**:
- [ ] Monday: Office/remote setup + meet team
- [ ] Read CLAUDE.md + DEALIX_BUSINESS_MODEL.md
- [ ] Read all Wave 1–5 documents + role-specific docs
- [ ] Coffee chat with founder (understanding vision + expectations)
- [ ] Coffee chat with manager (role details + first projects)
- [ ] Coffee chat with 2–3 team members (context + culture)
- [ ] Join customer call (observe, don't participate)
- [ ] Understand systems (Zoho, GitHub, Slack, tools)
- [ ] Set up development environment (if engineering)
- [ ] Friday: Week 1 checkpoint (with manager, how are you feeling?)

**Learning outcomes**:
- Understand Dealix mission, market, offer ladder
- Understand their role + first 90 days
- Know who to ask for what
- Feel welcomed + supported

#### **Week 2–3: Deep Context & First Projects**

**Week 2 Checklist**:
- [ ] Assigned 2–3 small tasks (defined scope, clear success criteria)
- [ ] Co-lead call with manager (customer call or internal meeting)
- [ ] Understand customer context (read 3 customer profiles)
- [ ] Present to team (what did you learn this week?, 15 min)
- [ ] Friday: Checkpoint (progress on tasks, blockers?)

**Week 3 Checklist**:
- [ ] Complete first assigned tasks independently
- [ ] Present completed work to team (demo or review, 30 min)
- [ ] Debrief with manager (what went well? what's confusing?)
- [ ] Clarify expectations for month 2
- [ ] Peer feedback (ask manager for 3 people to get feedback from)

**Learning outcomes**:

#### **Week 4: Ramp & Mid-Point Review**

**Week 4 Checklist**:

- [ ] Receive feedback (strengths + development areas)

## 7. Core Values & Culture

### 7.1 Dealix Core Values

**Customer Obsession**:
- Every decision evaluated against customer impact
- NPS and churn are team OKRs
- Regular customer exposure (all team members visit customers 1x/quarter)
- Feedback loops short (customer feedback → product change in <2 weeks)

**Bias for Action**:
- "Done is better than perfect"
- Decisions made with 80% information (not waiting for 100%)
- Small experiments over big planning committees
- Founder empowers team to move fast (within guardrails)

**Ownership & Accountability**:
- Every person owns their outcomes (not blaming others)
- Failure is learning, not blame (we debrief, we improve)
- Success is celebrated publicly (recognizing achievers)
- Authority distributed (not every decision goes to founder)

**Transparency & Trust**:
- Decisions explained (why, not just what)
- Financial performance visible (team sees revenue, growth, profit)
- Customer feedback shared unfiltered (good + bad)
- Honest conversations (feedback given directly, not behind backs)

**Lean & Sustainable**:
- No unnecessary bureaucracy (processes optimized for speed)

## 10. Feedback & Performance Management

### 10.1 Feedback Culture

**Regular feedback** (not annual surprise):
- Weekly: Manager observes + provides immediate feedback (positive + constructive)
- Monthly: 1:1 deep dive (career, development, satisfaction)
- Quarterly: Formal review + bonus (if applicable)
- Annual: Strategic career conversation + compensation review

**Feedback guidelines**:
- **Specific**: "You wrote great documentation" not "Good work"
- **Timely**: Given within 24–48 hours (while fresh)
- **Balanced**: Both strengths + development areas
- **Actionable**: Clear what to do differently next time
- **Private**: 1:1 feedback, not in team settings

**Feedback examples**:

*What NOT to do*:
"Your code quality isn't good."

*What TO do*:
"In your PR yesterday, I noticed 3 functions without unit tests. Our target is >80% coverage. Can we add tests before merging? I'm happy to pair on it if helpful."

### 10.2 Performance Rating System

**Quarterly performance rating** (1–5 scale):

- **5 = Exceeds expectations**: Beyond role responsibilities, driving team forward
- **4 = Meets expectations**: Consistently delivering on objectives
- **3 = Partially meets expectations**: On track, some gaps in execution
- **2 = Below expectations**: Not meeting key objectives
- **1 = Doesn't meet expectations**: Serious performance issue, improvement plan needed

**Rating implications**:

| Rating | Bonus | Salary Adjustment | Action |
|--------|-------|-------------------|--------|
| 5 | 200% of target | +10% | Promotion discussion |
| 4 | 100% of target | +5% | Continue current path |
| 3 | 50% of target | 0–2% | Development plan created |
| 2 | 0% | 0% | 30–60-day improvement plan |
| 1 | 0% | 0% | Immediate improvement plan or separation |

## 11. Conflict Resolution & Tough Conversations

### 11.1 When Someone Isn't Working Out

**Clear process** (no surprises):

**Early warning** (at first sign):
- [ ] Manager has 1:1 conversation (within 48 hours of noticing)
- [ ] Specific feedback (what's not working, what needs to change)
- [ ] Support offered (extra help, training, mentoring, resources)
- [ ] Timeline: "Let's check in next Friday to see progress"

**If issue persists** (after 1–2 weeks):
- [ ] Second conversation (more serious tone)
- [ ] Explicit expectations: "These are non-negotiable"
- [ ] Support remains offered (we want this to work)
- [ ] Clear timeline: "Let's check in again in 2 weeks"
- [ ] Document conversation (email summary)

**If issue still not resolved** (after 4 weeks):
- [ ] Improvement plan created (specific goals, timeline, success criteria)
- [ ] Founder + manager meet with person (30-day improvement plan)
- [ ] Clear: "If we don't see improvement in 30 days, we'll need to part ways"
- [ ] Extra support offered (manager check-ins every 3–5 days)

**If improvement doesn't happen**:
- [ ] Separation discussion (no surprises at this point)
- [ ] Final paycheck calculated (including any unused leave)
- [ ] Severance offered (if applicable, usually 1–2 weeks)
- [ ] Exit interview (what did we not do well as a company?)
- [ ] Transition plan (knowledge transfer, customer handoff)
