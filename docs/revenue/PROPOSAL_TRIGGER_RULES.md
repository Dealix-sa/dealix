# Proposal Trigger Rules

## Purpose
Decide, mechanically, when a lead becomes a proposal — and when it does not.
Removes founder ambiguity at the "should I send the doc?" moment.

## Proposal Ready When
- Prospect replied positively to outreach or sample.
- A clear problem statement is on the table.
- A decision maker (founder, CEO, sales lead, COO) is in the thread.
- Sample pack was requested or acknowledged as useful.
- Scope can be stated in one paragraph.
- Price band fits one of the published tiers (`docs/revenue/PRICING_AND_PACKAGING.md`).
- A start condition can be named (e.g., "starts when invoice paid").

## Not Ready When
- Vague interest only ("looks cool, let's talk later").
- No decision maker in the thread.
- No real fit (wrong sector, wrong stage, wrong geography).
- Prospect is asking for guaranteed revenue / guaranteed meetings.
- Wants extensive custom work that breaks the published packages.
- Trust signal is unclear (anonymous email, no website, no LinkedIn).

## Proposal Output Must Include
- Objective (one line).
- Scope (in / out).
- Deliverables (numbered list).
- Price (SAR, matches a tier).
- Timeline (start, end, milestones).
- Start condition (typically: invoice paid).
- Follow-up date (concrete).

## Anti-Patterns (Reject)
- Adding revenue or reply guarantees to close.
- Promising features Dealix does not ship today.
- Discounting more than the published annual discount.
- Skipping the start condition to "be flexible".
