# Revenue Operations Playbook

## Purpose
The end-to-end commercial machine for Dealix: from ICP to cash.

## Stages
1. **ICP filter** — only sectors that match the operating system.
2. **Lead sourcing** — founder-led + warm intros + partner referrals.
3. **Qualification** — score against `docs/acquisition/LEAD_QUALIFICATION_SCORE.md`.
4. **Outbound cadence** — controlled by `docs/acquisition/OUTBOUND_CADENCE_SYSTEM.md`.
5. **Sample** — small, focused, anonymized; tracked in `delivery/sample_quality_log.csv`.
6. **Proposal** — uses the 5-rung ladder; tracked in `sales/proposal_tracker.csv`.
7. **Payment path** — `docs/finance/PAYMENT_PATH_SYSTEM.md`.
8. **Delivery** — Sprint 5 takes over.
9. **Win/loss** — every closed deal is reviewed.

## The 5-rung product ladder
| Rung | Offer | Price |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Free Diagnostic | 0 SAR |
| 2 | Revenue Intelligence Sprint | 499 SAR |
| 3 | Sector Data Pack | 1,500 SAR |
| 4 | Managed Ops Retainer | 2,999–4,999 SAR/mo |
| 5 | Custom AI Build | 5K–25K SAR |

## Non-negotiables (revenue edition)
- Founder-led: no automated cold outreach.
- No mass spam: each DM is hand-written.
- No discount > 30% without founder approval.
- Bad revenue (mismatched sectors, hostile buyers, exploitative pricing pressure) is rejected.

## Operating cadence
- Daily: log every action.
- Weekly: review win/loss, refresh ICP scorecard.
- Monthly: refresh source performance and message performance.

## Forbidden patterns
- LinkedIn scraping.
- Cold WhatsApp blasting.
- Buying lead lists.
- Misrepresenting capacity ("we have a team of 12").

## Metrics
- Leads added per week.
- DM reply rate.
- Sample → proposal conversion.
- Proposal → cash conversion.
- Cash collected this month.

## Owners
- Founder owns every external touch until the first 10 paying customers.
- Sales sub-agent assists with drafting only; never sends.
