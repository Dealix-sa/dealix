# Revenue Quality

Not all revenue is good revenue. Some customers drain the company.

## Good Revenue
- clear ICP
- paid upfront or approved PO
- clear scope
- repeatable delivery
- potential retainer
- low trust risk
- useful case study potential

## Bad Revenue
- unclear scope
- asks for guaranteed sales
- refuses approval process
- high legal / compliance ambiguity
- low price and high customization
- no repeatability
- no proof potential

## Rule
Do not accept revenue that damages trust, focus, or repeatability.

## Operating Mechanism

Revenue quality plugs into:

- **Action Router** (`docs/control_plane/ACTION_ROUTER.md`): "send
  proposal" is APPROVE-path, so the founder must consciously accept
  each piece of revenue.
- **Client Tiering** (`docs/client_success/CLIENT_TIERING.md`):
  bad-revenue traits push a client toward Tier C/D and limit founder
  time invested.
- **Kill List** (`docs/founder/KILL_LIST.md`): "offers with unclear
  scope" and "clients asking for guaranteed results" are explicit
  kill-immediately patterns.
