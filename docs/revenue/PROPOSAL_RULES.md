# Proposal Rules

A proposal is a contract draft. Treat it that way.

## Required sections
1. Client context (one paragraph).
2. Outcome (one sentence).
3. Scope — what is included.
4. Out of scope — what is not included.
5. Deliverables — concrete artifacts.
6. Timeline — start, milestones, end.
7. Price and payment terms.
8. Assumptions and dependencies.
9. Approval and acceptance criteria.
10. Validity period.

## Prohibited language
- Guaranteed revenue, guaranteed leads, guaranteed compliance.
- "Up to" claims without a floor.
- Open-ended scope ("as required", "and more").
- Outcomes that depend on the client's actions without stating them.

## Approval before sending
- Internal review against `docs/trust/NO_OVERCLAIM_POLICY.md`.
- Founder approval (A2) for any new pricing band or scope template.
- Suppression-list and sanctions screen on the client entity.

## Rule
A proposal that cannot be delivered cannot be sent. If the scope is not deliverable in the stated time at the stated price, the proposal does not leave the company.
