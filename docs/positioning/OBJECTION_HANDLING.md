# Objection Handling

**Owner:** Founder
**Source of truth:** `docs/positioning/DEALIX_POSITIONING.md` + `docs/00_constitution/NON_NEGOTIABLES.md`

## Principle

Every objection answer is honest, evidence-backed, and free of guarantees. The goal is not to overcome the buyer — the goal is to confirm or disconfirm fit.

A buyer who walks away after a clean objection answer is a buyer Dealix would have failed. That is a win, not a loss.

## Top 12 objections + safe answers

### 1. "We already have a CRM."

Answer: "Dealix is not a CRM. It sits above your CRM and feeds it scored accounts, gated drafts, and dated proof. If your CRM is your record-keeper, Dealix is the layer that fills it with qualified work."

What we don't say: "We replace your CRM."

### 2. "Can you guarantee leads / meetings / revenue?"

Answer: "No. We guarantee scope: a named sprint, named deliverables, a Proof Pack, and a credit guardrail if a milestone slips. We do not guarantee the customer's market response. That is the buyer's market, not ours."

See `docs/00_constitution/NON_NEGOTIABLES.md`.

### 3. "Why not just use HubSpot/Salesforce/ChatGPT?"

Answer: "If those tools are solving your deal flow and gating problems, you don't need Dealix. We work with customers whose pattern is: AI drafts pile up unreviewed, sends ship without approval, and wins do not compound into reusable assets. If that's not your pattern, we're not the fit."

### 4. "Your prices are higher than an agency."

Answer: "Per sprint, sometimes. Per outcome — proof produced, asset captured, gate enforced — sprints compound. An agency delivers a campaign; we deliver a system the customer keeps. If short-term campaign work is what you need, an agency is a better fit."

### 5. "Do you scrape data?"

Answer: "No. Scraping is a category rule we refuse. Account maps come from sanctioned sources and customer-provided inputs. If a buyer's plan depends on scraped contacts, we're not a fit."

### 6. "Can you send WhatsApp/cold outreach for us at scale?"

Answer: "No. We draft and queue; the customer or the founder approves each batch; we send only with explicit approval. WhatsApp automation specifically is outside what we offer. See our exclusions list."

### 7. "How is this different from an AI agency that builds custom GPTs?"

Answer: "Custom GPTs without gates produce drafts. We produce the entire loop: deal flow input, drafts, gates, approved send, logged outcome, Proof Pack. The gates are the architecture, not the LLM."

### 8. "We tried sales automation before and it burned our reputation."

Answer: "That's why our default is queue-only. Every send carries a named approval class and an audit log. If you want zero sends, we run scoring and drafting only and you keep the inbox to yourself."

### 9. "Our sales process is too specific; nothing standard works."

Answer: "The Sprint catalog has named sprints, but the inside of each sprint is configured to your sector and your buyer. The Diagnostic surfaces the configuration before the Sprint commits."

### 10. "Why a Saudi-specific Revenue OS? Why not a regional or global tool?"

Answer: "Saudi B2B has specific data norms (PDPL), language requirements (Arabic-first or bilingual), and a buyer profile (founder-led) that global tools do not optimize for. A regional tool that adds Saudi support is not the same as a tool built for Saudi from day one."

### 11. "Who else have you worked with? Can you share names?"

Answer: "We publish anonymized sprint outcomes by default and named case studies only with explicit written customer approval. If you need a named reference, we can request one from a past customer, but they reserve the right to decline."

Never invent a customer name. Never imply customers exist if they do not.

### 12. "What happens if we want to stop?"

Answer: "Sprints are fixed-scope and fixed-price; you stop by not signing the next sprint. Retainers are month-to-month with a 30-day notice clause. There is no annual lock-in. The Capital Loop assets produced during your engagement remain yours."

## How to use this doc

- Discovery calls: review the top 3 objections before the call based on the lead's profile.
- Decks: keep this doc open during pitch rehearsal.
- Proposals: pre-empt the top 2 likely objections in the exec summary.
- Onboarding new operators: this doc is required reading.

## What never to do in an objection answer

1. Never claim a result that has not been verified.
2. Never disparage a competitor by name beyond the structured comparison in `COMPETITIVE_NARRATIVE.md`.
3. Never invent a customer story.
4. Never promise a feature that is not on the roadmap.
5. Never agree to send external messages without a named approval gate.
6. Never offer a discount to close the objection. Discounts close trust gates instead.

## When an objection means "not a fit"

If a buyer requires any of the following, document the no-fit and move on:

- Guaranteed leads or meetings.
- Scraping or bulk cold sending.
- Auto-send without approval gates.
- White-label resale without partner-program participation.
- A custom CRM build.

Each is a category-rule violation. The right answer is "we're not the right fit."

## Failure mode

- An operator promises a result to close an objection.
- An operator agrees to a scope outside the category rules.
- An objection answer drifts into competitor disparagement.

## Recovery path

1. Reset the conversation to the named scope.
2. Document the misstatement and notify the Founder.
3. Update training material with the missed handle.
