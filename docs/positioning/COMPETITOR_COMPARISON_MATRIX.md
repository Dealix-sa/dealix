# Competitor Comparison Matrix

> Wordmark: **DEALIX**
> Tagline: **INTELLIGENT DEALS. REAL GROWTH.**
> Positioning: **Saudi B2B Revenue Operating System**

This document is the head-to-head Dealix comparison grid. It compares
Dealix against the four alternative patterns Saudi B2B buyers know:
**scraping tools**, **spam / blast tools**, **generic CRM**, and
**generic agency**. The grid is the structured complement to
`COMPETITIVE_NARRATIVE.md`, which carries the story.

The grid is honest. Where an alternative beats Dealix, we say so.
Where Dealix beats an alternative, we say why with a defensible
proof point. The grid is meant to be sent to a buyer who is doing
their own evaluation — it must survive their scrutiny.

---

## 1. How to read the grid

- Each row is a capability or constraint that matters in Saudi B2B
  revenue work.
- Each column is one of the four alternatives, plus Dealix.
- "Yes" / "No" answers carry an explanatory note where useful.
- A "Partial" answer is honest about a partial fit.
- Where the comparison is not applicable (the alternative simply
  does not address the capability), the cell reads "n/a".

We do not name specific brands inside the grid. The grid is by
**category**. Brand specifics live in sales conversations, not in
written collateral.

## 3. Notes per row

The grid is shorthand. The notes below carry the substance.

### 3.1 Contact sourcing
Scraping tools are purpose-built for this; they win the row. Dealix
uses customer data, integrations, and approved enrichment partners —
we do not scrape, and we do not buy unconsented lists.

### 3.2 Mass external sending
Spam tools are purpose-built for this; they win the row. Dealix
explicitly does not send without per-action approval. This is the
**most important defensible "no"** in the grid.

### 3.3 Trust-gated approval per action
The structural Dealix advantage. No alternative has this as a
first-class feature. CRMs can be configured to require approval for
certain actions, but the gate is configurable, not enforced.

### 3.4 Approval ledger
The audit-ready ledger of every approved action, with timestamp,
owner, and outcome. CRMs have activity logs, but they are activity
records, not approval records.

### 3.5 Arabic-native drafting
Most CRMs are English-first with Arabic translations of UI. Dealix
drafts in Arabic at native quality because it is the design center,
not a localization layer.

### 3.6 Saudi-first data residency
Most global tools store in Europe or the US. Some have Saudi options
now. Dealix is in-kingdom by default with the right data classes.

### 3.7 PDPL-aligned posture
PDPL alignment requires more than data residency; it requires lawful
basis tracking, data subject rights, breach response, and processor
agreements. Dealix is built around this; most alternatives bolt it on.

### 3.8 ZATCA-compliant invoicing
For Saudi B2B sales workflows that include invoicing, ZATCA Phase 2
integration is required. Dealix supports this; most alternatives do
not.

### 3.9 Sector templates for Saudi B2B
Templates tuned to Saudi B2B sub-sectors (logistics, healthcare,
industrial, professional services, B2B SaaS). Generic CRMs and
generic agencies bring generic templates.

### 3.10 Deal map artifact
A structured view of live accounts and their state, exportable. CRMs
have pipeline views that approximate this but are not artifact-
producing.

### 3.11 Drafted outbound (held for approval)
The drafted outbound queue, with each draft held until the human
approves it. Generic outbound tools allow scheduling and pause
features but treat approval as friction, not as the feature.

### 3.12 Evidence ledger (signed)
A signed, append-only record of approved actions and their
outcomes. No alternative offers this in a way that satisfies Saudi
internal audit.

### 3.13 Pilot Sprint (14-day)
The bounded engagement that produces concrete artifacts. Agencies
offer engagements but with looser deliverable definitions. Tools do
not offer engagements.

### 3.14 Capability stays after engagement
Tools stay with the buyer. Agency capabilities leave when the
agency does. Dealix is the layer that stays.

### 3.15 Operator-built
Founder-led, operator-built tools differ structurally from those
built by marketing or design departments. Dealix is operator-built.
This is not a feature; it is a signal of where the product's
opinions come from.

### 3.16 Guaranteed outcome claims
Many alternatives advertise guaranteed meetings, guaranteed sales,
or guaranteed pipeline. Dealix does not. This is policy, not
preference.

### 3.17 Customer publication without approval
Many alternatives publish customer logos and quotes without
explicit logged approval. Dealix does not. This is policy.

### 3.18 Operator-time required per week
A real cost. The pilot requires roughly 2 hours of operator time per
week. Tools require lower setup but ongoing self-driven effort.
Agencies require less hands-on time but no transfer of capability.

### 3.19 Time to first signed artifact
14 days for Dealix. Tools depend on the buyer's self-driven pace.
Agencies typically take 30–60 days to produce a signed artifact.

## 6. Honest acknowledgments

Where alternatives do something well that Dealix does not:

- **Sourcing volume.** A scraping tool will find more contacts
  faster than Dealix does. If sourcing volume is the only need,
  use a scraping tool.
- **Hands-off external sending.** A spam tool will send more
  external messages per hour than Dealix will (because we send
  zero without approval). If hands-off sending is the only need,
  Dealix is not the right tool — and we recommend reconsidering
  the need.
- **Database depth.** A mature CRM has years of integration
  surface area Dealix does not duplicate. We sit above it for
  exactly this reason.
- **Delegated execution.** An agency can take the operator's time
  to zero (until the engagement ends). Dealix requires ~2 hours
  per week during the pilot.

These honest acknowledgments are part of the credibility of the
grid.

## 7. Forbidden comparison phrases

- "Dealix is the only [...]" — most "only" claims are wrong.
- "Dealix replaces your CRM." — we do not.
- "Better than [named brand]" — we compare by category.
- "Faster ROI than [...]" — ROI is not our axis.
- "Guaranteed more meetings than [...]" — banned.
- "Cheaper than [...]" — price is not our axis.

When a buyer pushes for any of these, we redirect to the structural
differences (trust gate, approval ledger, Saudi posture, evidence).

## 9. When the grid changes

The grid changes when:

- A real new alternative category emerges (e.g. a "Saudi-built CRM"
  that genuinely fits Saudi B2B).
- A capability shifts in the market (e.g. PDPL alignment becomes
  table stakes for everyone).
- A Dealix capability changes (added or removed).

The grid is versioned in this document. Customer-facing deliveries
should reference the current version date.
