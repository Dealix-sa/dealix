# Dealix Positioning

## 1. One-line positioning

> **Dealix is the Saudi B2B Revenue Operating System for intelligent
> deal flow, founder-approved growth, and trust-gated AI execution.**

## 2. Positioning canvas

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **For whom** | Saudi B2B founders running 5–500 person revenue orgs. |
| **Who need** | A defensible way to compound deal flow without losing control or trust. |
| **Dealix is a** | Revenue Operating System (not a CRM, not an agency, not a chatbot). |
| **That** | Generates scored accounts, drafts outbound, runs the proposal factory, captures payment, triggers delivery, and ships proof — all behind founder approval. |
| **Unlike** | US sales-automation tools that send blindly or local agencies that work without an audit trail. |
| **Our product** | Combines a founder console, an approval queue, an AI agent registry, and an audit ledger — Saudi-native (PDPL / ZATCA aware). |

## 3. Top three buying triggers

1. The founder is the bottleneck for revenue execution.
2. Compliance (PDPL / ZATCA / sector rules) makes US tools risky.
3. A previous outbound experiment burnt trust with the market.

## 4. Top three objections (and answers)

| Objection | Answer |
|---|---|
| "We already have a CRM." | Dealix is the operating layer on top of it. It writes to the CRM via contracts, not replaces it. |
| "Will it spam our market?" | No external send happens without an explicit founder approval — every draft sits in a queue. |
| "Why not an agency?" | An agency doesn't leave you a system. Dealix is the system; the founder owns it for life. |

## 5. Sales kit anchors

Every commercial conversation lands on these three anchors:

1. **Founder console** — one screen, one decision per row.
2. **Approval queue** — drafts in, founder-approved actions out.
3. **Audit ledger** — every approval becomes searchable evidence.

If any of those three is not visible in a demo, the prospect saw the
wrong demo.
