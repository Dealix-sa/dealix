# DEALIX Positioning

**Owner:** Founder
**Source of truth:** This doc + `docs/00_foundation/DEALIX_POSITIONING.md`

## One-line positioning

Dealix is a Saudi B2B Revenue Operating System for intelligent deal flow, founder-approved growth, and trust-gated AI execution.

## For whom

| Audience | Description |
|---|---|
| Primary | Saudi B2B service-company founders with teams of 5-50, running deal flow themselves, with 1-3 commercial people |
| Secondary | Heads of Sales at Saudi B2B firms (50-200 employees) accountable for pipeline and revenue |
| Tertiary | Heads of GTM at regional B2B SaaS companies expanding into KSA |

Not for: enterprise IT buyers procuring a Salesforce replacement; B2C marketers; agencies looking to white-label a tool.

## Against what

Dealix is positioned against three failure patterns, not against individual products.

| Failure pattern | What Dealix replaces |
|---|---|
| Leaky deal flow run on spreadsheets and memory | Intelligent deal flow loop with scored accounts and named triggers |
| Ungoverned AI drafting that ships externally without review | Trust-gated AI execution with named approval classes |
| Disconnected proof and capital, where wins do not compound | Capital Loop where every closed deal produces reusable assets |

Adjacent named competitors and how Dealix differs:

| Competitor | Their claim | Dealix difference |
|---|---|---|
| HubSpot | Marketing and sales hub | Not a CRM; sits above record-keeping with deal flow and gating |
| Salesloft / Outreach | Sales engagement at scale | No bulk cold sending; founder-approved queue only |
| Gong | Conversation intelligence | No call-recording dependency; gates and approvals are the primary instrument |
| Generic agency | Bespoke campaigns | Reusable Sprint catalog; founder-led; trust-gated |
| Scraping or "leads" vendor | Bulk contact lists | Refused outright; not the business |

## Value (what the buyer gets)

Concrete deliverables, not abstractions:

- **Scored account map** in the buyer's named sector, refreshed on a defined cadence.
- **Drafted outreach pack** — emails, contact-form notes, partner-referral asks — queued for founder review.
- **Approval log** — every gated decision recorded with date, approver, and outcome.
- **Proof Pack** — dated, anonymized record of sprint outcomes, ready for case-study reuse.
- **Capital register** — reusable assets (scripts, playbooks, templates) produced by each sprint.

## Value the buyer does NOT get

To prevent expectation drift, Dealix names what it does not deliver:

- Guaranteed leads, meetings, or revenue.
- External sends without explicit per-batch approval.
- A CRM platform license.
- A paid-media spend allocation.
- A scraped database of Saudi B2B contacts.

## Why now

See `docs/positioning/WHY_DEALIX_NOW.md` for the full argument. Summary:

1. Vision 2030 accelerates Saudi B2B services formation; founders need revenue infrastructure faster than agency-led delivery can supply.
2. AI drafting cost has collapsed; approval cost has not. The bottleneck moved from production to governance.
3. The Saudi B2B buyer is a hands-on founder who will not delegate revenue to a black-box agency.

|---|---|

## Failure mode

- Positioning collapses to "CRM with AI" in someone else's copy.
- The founder-buyer cannot self-identify from the landing page H1.
- The exclusions list disappears from customer surfaces.

## Recovery path

1. Re-publish the category language.
2. Re-state exclusions on the hero surface.
3. Audit competitor mentions for accuracy quarterly.

## Disclaimer

Dealix does not guarantee revenue, meetings, or conversion outcomes. Positioning claims are categorical, not promissory.
