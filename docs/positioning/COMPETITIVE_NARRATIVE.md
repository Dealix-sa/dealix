# Competitive Narrative

**Owner:** Founder
**Source of truth:** `docs/positioning/DEALIX_POSITIONING.md`

## Principle

Dealix does not compete with products. Dealix competes with patterns. Each named competitor is shorthand for a pattern Saudi B2B operators recognize.

## How to use this doc

Use these comparisons in discovery calls, decks (slide 6), and proposals (appendix). Do not put them on a public landing page; they read as defensive. Use them when a buyer asks "How are you different from X?" — and only then.

## Versus HubSpot (and Salesforce, Zoho, etc.)

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What they do | CRM — record-keeping, contact management, pipeline stages |
| What they don't do | Generate deal flow; gate AI execution; produce reusable proof |
| What Dealix does instead | Sit above the CRM, feed it scored accounts, gate every AI-drafted send, produce Proof Packs |
| Who needs both | A buyer with an active CRM uses Dealix as the input and gate layer |
| Who needs only Dealix | An early-stage Saudi B2B founder without a CRM still benefits — the CRM can come later |

Honest phrasing: "Dealix is not a CRM. It sits on top of your CRM, or it works without one until you need one."

## Versus Salesloft and Outreach

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What they do | Sales engagement at scale — high-volume sequences across email, phone, social |
| What they don't do | Founder-approve every batch; refuse scraping inputs; honor Saudi data norms |
| What Dealix does instead | Queue every batch for explicit founder approval; refuse scraped inputs; PDPL/CITC alignment |
| Tradeoff | Lower throughput than Salesloft. Higher trust per send. Better fit for founder-led teams. |

Honest phrasing: "Dealix produces fewer sends than Salesloft on purpose. Each send carries a named approval."

## Versus Gong (conversation intelligence)

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What they do | Record calls, transcribe, score, surface coaching moments |
| What they don't do | Generate deal flow; run pre-call account scoring; manage post-call gated actions |
| What Dealix does instead | Provide deal flow input and post-call action gating; does not depend on call recording |
| Co-exist? | Yes. Dealix and Gong solve adjacent problems for different audiences. |

Honest phrasing: "If your team needs conversation intelligence, Gong is a better fit. Dealix solves the layer before and after the call."

## Versus generic B2B agencies

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What they do | Bespoke campaigns, account-management, content production, sometimes outbound |
| What they don't do | Build a reusable system; expose gates and logs; let the customer audit AI output |
| What Dealix does instead | Sprint catalog with named deliverables; Capital Loop produces reusable assets; founder-approved gates |
| Pricing pattern | Agencies often retainer-only. Dealix runs Sprint → Pilot → Retainer with a fixed-scope upfront. |

Honest phrasing: "Agencies make work. Dealix makes systems. The first sprint produces a system the customer keeps."

## Versus scraping tools (Apollo, ZoomInfo data layers, scrapers)

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What they do | Sell bulk contact data, often scraped from public profiles |
| What they don't do | Respect platform terms, PDPL, CITC; produce qualified opportunities |
| What Dealix does instead | Refuse scraped inputs as a category rule; build account maps from sanctioned sources |
| Position | Not a competitor. A category Dealix refuses to operate in. |

Honest phrasing: "Dealix is not a scraping tool and does not buy scraped data. If that's what you need, we're not a fit."

## Versus consultants and "AI transformation" firms

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| What they do | Strategy decks, roadmaps, change-management |
| What they don't do | Ship a running revenue loop; operate the gates; produce dated proof |
| What Dealix does instead | Ship the loop in a Sprint; operate the gates; produce a Proof Pack per Sprint |
| Tradeoff | Less abstract. More opinionated. Founders who want a strategy deck should hire consultants. |

Honest phrasing: "We don't write transformation decks. We run sprints."

## Cross-category claim

Dealix's claim is not "we are better at outreach than Salesloft" or "we are better at CRM than HubSpot." The claim is: "We are the only Revenue Operating System built for Saudi B2B founders with trust gates as first-class architecture." See `docs/positioning/CATEGORY_CREATION_OS.md`.

## What to do when a buyer says "we already use [X]"

1. Acknowledge the tool. Do not attack it.
2. Ask what the tool does NOT solve for them.
3. Map their unmet need to the Dealix layer (deal flow, gate, proof, capital).
4. Offer a Diagnostic, not a switch.

## Failure mode

- Dealix is positioned in a customer's mind as "another CRM" or "another agency."
- Discovery calls drift into feature comparisons with HubSpot.

## Recovery path

1. Re-anchor the conversation to category language (Revenue OS, gates, Proof Pack).
2. Offer a Diagnostic instead of a feature debate.
