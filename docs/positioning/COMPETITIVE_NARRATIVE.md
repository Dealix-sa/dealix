# Competitive Narrative

> How Dealix talks about other tools in the buyer conversation.
> Rule one: we never disparage. Rule two: we always reframe.

## 1. The frame

Every comparison maps to a single axis:

> **Tool ↔ Operating System.**

The buyer is choosing between collecting more tools and adopting an operating system.

## 2. Adjacencies

| Adjacency | What they do | What Dealix does | Reframe |
|---|---|---|---|
| CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho) | Store the customer record | Operate revenue motions on top of any CRM | "We don't replace your CRM. We make it productive." |
| Sales engagement (Outreach, Salesloft) | Multi-touch outbound | Draft + queue + approve, never auto-send | "We are signal-first, not volume-first." |
| Marketing automation (Marketo, HubSpot) | Trigger-based nurture | Trust-gated, founder-approved campaigns | "Automation, but founder-controlled." |
| AI SDR (Artisan, 11x.ai) | Autonomous SDR agents | Semi-autonomous distribution under approval | "We respect customers and the founder enough to keep humans on the line." |
| Lead generation (Apollo, Cognism) | Sell B2B lists | Score, prioritise and operate accounts | "We don't sell lists. We operate accounts." |
| Consultancies | Bespoke human service | Productised, repeatable operating system | "A consultancy hands you a deck. We hand you a system." |
| Custom builds | Glue between point tools | Pre-integrated operating layer | "You can build it in 12 months. Or you can operate it on Monday." |

## 3. Decision criteria we re-set

When a buyer says they care about:

| Criterion | Reframe |
|---|---|
| "Most features" | "Most outcomes per riyal" |
| "Send volume" | "Reply-quality per send" |
| "Number of leads" | "Cash collected" |
| "AI automation" | "Trust-gated AI execution under founder control" |
| "Reporting" | "Operating cadence with audit-grade visibility" |

## 4. Pricing posture

We do not race to the bottom. We price the OS — not the tools beneath it. Pricing guardrails: `docs/product/PRICING_GUARDRAILS.md`.

## 5. Battle cards (sketch)

Each battle card sits in `docs/marketing/` once a competitor is named in two or more buyer conversations. Template:

```
Competitor: <name>
Their best frame: <quote>
Why a buyer would prefer them: <reasons>
Our reframe: <one-line>

Pricing posture: <link to guardrails>

```

## 6. Tone

Always respectful. Always reframing. Never disparaging. Never naming a competitor in a hero or campaign without Brand Guardian + founder approval.
