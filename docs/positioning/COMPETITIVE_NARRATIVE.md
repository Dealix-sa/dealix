# Competitive Narrative | السرد التنافسي

> Dealix does not "beat" tools on tool terms. We **reframe the buyer's problem** from "which tool sends faster" to "which operating company ships trustworthy deals." The narrative below is how we do that consistently.

---

## 1. Purpose | الغرض

This document arms the founder, sales, and partner teams with a single, consistent way to handle competitive conversations — without naming competitors disrespectfully, without making unprovable claims, and without violating any non-negotiable.

---

## 2. The Reframe | إعادة التأطير

When a prospect compares us to anything else, we reframe in this order:

1. **Restate the real problem** ("You don't need more sends — you need pipeline you can audit.")
2. **Name the category gap** ("No tool is a Revenue Operating Company. Tools record, automate, or send. We operate.")
3. **Show the trust gate** ("Dealix is approval-gated by design — every send, every proof, every commitment.")
4. **Show the local fit** ("Bilingual-native. Saudi compliance-aware. KSA buying-cycle aligned.")
5. **Offer the ladder** ("Start with a Free Diagnostic. Decide after evidence, not pitch.")

---

## 3. Comparison Lens | عدسة المقارنة

We never compare feature-by-feature. We compare on **operating posture**.

| Posture | Tool category | Dealix |
|---|---|---|
| Record-keeping | CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive) | Dealix sits on top; CRM remains the record. |
| Volume sending | Sales engagement (Outreach, Salesloft, Apollo) | Dealix drafts; customer approves. No external sending. |
| Autonomous outreach | AI SDR (point tools) | Dealix is approval-gated, bilingual-native, evidence-led. |
| Hour-based service | Agency / consultancy | Dealix is productized rungs + software + proof. |
| Generic global SaaS | Western horizontal tools | Dealix is Saudi B2B-native. |

---

## 4. Common Objections & Reframes | الاعتراضات الشائعة

**"We already have HubSpot / Salesforce."**
- Reframe: "Great. We sit on top of it. CRM is the record; Dealix is the operator. We make HubSpot earn its keep by producing drafts, approvals, and evidenced deals."

**"Why not just use Outreach / Apollo?"**
- Reframe: "Those tools send at scale. In Saudi B2B, scale-send erodes trust. We draft and queue; you approve. Your CRM and your relationships stay intact."

**"Isn't this just an AI SDR?"**
- Reframe: "AI SDRs auto-send. We do not auto-send. Approval-gated by design. That's the difference between activity and accountable pipeline."

**"We can hire an agency for less."**
- Reframe: "Agencies bill hours. We package outcomes — a Revenue Sprint produces a defined, evidenced result. And we leave you with an operating system, not a stack of decks."

**"How do I know it will work for us?"**
- Reframe: "Start with the Free Diagnostic. We show you the ICP density, the warm graph, and a sample draft set — before you commit a riyal."

**"Can you guarantee revenue?"**
- Reframe: "No one credible can. We share ranges from comparable pilots, conditions for those ranges, and the pilot N behind them. We commit to evidence, not guarantees."

**"Do you have local case studies?"**
- Reframe: "Yes — published only with customer approval. Here are the ones we can share; here are the anonymized patterns from the ones we can't."

## 5. Battle Card Snapshots | بطاقات المقارنة

> Use these as conversation aids, never as published assets without legal review.

### vs. CRM (HubSpot / Salesforce)

- **They:** record the deal.
- **We:** ship the deal.
- **Trust gate:** approval-gated drafts, evidenced approvals on top of their CRM.
- **Posture:** complementary, not replacement.

### vs. Sales Engagement (Outreach / Salesloft / Apollo)

- **They:** auto-send at scale.
- **We:** draft + queue + approve. No external sending.
- **Trust gate:** zero auto-sends; full audit trail.
- **Posture:** reframe the problem; we are a different category.

### vs. AI SDR point tools

- **They:** autonomous outreach.
- **We:** approval-gated, bilingual-native, evidence-led.
- **Trust gate:** every action gated by human approval and signed.
- **Posture:** category reframing; ROC > AI SDR.

### vs. Agencies / Consultancies

- **They:** hours, decks, hand-offs.
- **We:** productized rungs, software, proof pack.
- **Trust gate:** evidence control plane; co-owned outcomes.
- **Posture:** we leave behind an operating system.

## 6. Words We Use | الكلمات التي نستخدمها

- "Operating company," "Revenue OS," "approval-gated," "evidence control plane," "bilingual-native," "Saudi B2B reality," "productized rungs."

## 7. Words We Avoid | الكلمات التي نتجنبها

- "Better than," "kill," "destroy," "replace," "world-class," "the best," "fully autonomous," "guaranteed."

---

## 8. Public-Channel Etiquette | آداب القنوات العامة

- We do not name competitors negatively on LinkedIn, in essays, or in decks.
- We do not screenshot competitor UIs.
- We do not make side-by-side feature claims publicly.
- Competitive content is **internal** sales enablement; public content is **category-led**.

---

## 9. Non-Negotiables in Competitive Work | الخطوط الحمراء

- No guaranteed revenue claims when arguing against competitors.
- No customer logos referenced for competitive proof without written approval.
- No "we'll send for you" framing to win against sales engagement tools.
- No pricing matches or contract concessions without escalation to founder/commercial lead.
- No autonomous-agent framing to compete with AI SDR tools.

---

## 10. Owner & KPI | الملكية والمؤشرات

- **Owner:** Founder + Sales Lead.
- **KPI:** Win rate on competitive deals reframed to ROC category (target ≥ 50%).
- **KPI:** % of deals where prospect repeats Dealix vocabulary by call 2 (target ≥ 60%).
- **KPI:** Zero non-negotiable violations in competitive comms (audited monthly).
