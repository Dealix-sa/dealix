# Category Creation — Revenue Operating System

**Owner:** Founder
**Source of truth:** `docs/positioning/` + `docs/00_constitution/`

## The category

Dealix creates and occupies a category named **Revenue Operating System (Revenue OS)**.

A Revenue Operating System is not a CRM, not an agency, not a sales-engagement platform, and not a generic AI assistant. It is the layer that turns scattered deal flow, fragmented proof, and ungoverned AI output into a single, founder-approved revenue loop.

## Why a new category is needed

Existing categories each solve one slice:

| Category | What it solves | What it leaves unsolved |
|---|---|---|
| CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce) | Record-keeping | Deal flow generation, proof creation, gated AI execution |
| Sales engagement (Outreach, Salesloft) | Outbound cadence | Trust gating, founder approval, Saudi context |
| Conversation intelligence (Gong) | Call analytics | Deal flow input, execution control |
| Agency / consultancy | Bespoke project work | Reusable system, founder control, repeatability |
| Generic AI assistants | Drafting at the prompt | Governance, trust gates, audit trail |

A Saudi founder running B2B revenue today must compose four to six of these to approximate what one Revenue OS should provide. The composition is fragile, expensive, and ungoverned.

## What a Revenue OS does

A Revenue OS sits on top of existing data and tools and provides four loops:

1. **Deal Flow Loop** — Continuous identification, scoring, and routing of opportunities.
2. **Trust Gate Loop** — Every AI-drafted action passes a named approval class before any external execution.
3. **Approved Execution Loop** — Once gated, the action is queued, dispatched, and logged.
4. **Capital Loop** — Every closed loop produces a reusable asset (script, playbook, model) that lowers the cost of the next loop.

## Why now

Three forces converge:

1. **Saudi B2B macro.** Vision 2030 is accelerating B2B services formation. Founders need revenue infrastructure faster than agency-led delivery can supply.
2. **AI inflection.** Drafting cost is near zero. Approval cost is unchanged. The bottleneck has moved from production to governance.
3. **Founder-buyer.** The Saudi B2B buyer is increasingly a hands-on founder who will not delegate revenue to an external agency.

The combination demands a category where AI prepares and the founder approves — at the speed of AI, with the discipline of an audit trail.

## Category rules Dealix sets

For the Revenue OS category to hold, Dealix establishes these rules:

1. No external execution without an explicit, named, recorded approval class.
2. No scraping. No bulk cold outreach. No platform-terms violations.
3. Every output ties back to a Proof Pack, a Capital asset, or both.
4. Estimated value and Verified value are separate fields, never collapsed.
5. PDPL and CITC frameworks are first-class governance, not footnotes.

A competitor that violates any of these rules is operating outside the category, regardless of marketing claims.

## Category enemies

Dealix's positioning explicitly names the patterns it refuses:

- **Spray-and-pray outbound** — high-volume cold messaging with no approval gates.
- **Black-box AI agencies** — opaque execution that the customer cannot audit.
- **CRM as religion** — workflows that mistake record-keeping for revenue work.
- **Bespoke-everything consulting** — projects that produce decks but no reusable system.

## Category language

Dealix introduces and defends these terms:

- Revenue Operating System
- Trust Gate
- Approval Class (A0, A1, A2, A3)
- Proof Pack
- Estimated value vs Verified value
- Capital Loop

See `docs/01_category/CATEGORY_LANGUAGE.md` for the full glossary.

## Why this matters commercially

Categories that are named, defended, and operated produce three commercial effects:

- **Founder buyers self-identify.** A buyer who reads "Revenue OS" knows whether they want one. CRM buyers do not.
- **Price floors hold.** A Revenue OS is not benchmarked against a SaaS seat license; it is benchmarked against the founder's own revenue cost.
- **Partner ecosystems form.** Agencies, integrators, and trainers can build on top of a Revenue OS without competing with it.

## Failure mode

- Dealix gets described as "a CRM with AI." Category collapses to commodity.
- Competitors adopt "Revenue OS" without the rules. Category dilutes.

## Recovery path

1. Publish the category rules publicly and enforce them in every customer surface.
2. Audit Dealix's own copy quarterly for category drift.
3. Reject partnerships and integrations that violate category rules.
