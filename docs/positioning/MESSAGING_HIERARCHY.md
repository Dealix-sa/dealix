# Messaging Hierarchy

## L1 — One sentence

> Dealix is the Saudi B2B Revenue Operating System for intelligent
> deal flow, founder-approved growth, and trust-gated AI execution.

## L2 — Three pillars

1. **Intelligent deals.** Sector-ranked accounts, scored against your ICP.
2. **Real growth.** Drafts, proposals, follow-ups — built for approval.
3. **Founder-controlled.** Every external action sits behind your sign-off.

## L3 — Five proof anchors

1. Founder console with approval queue.
2. AI agent registry with kill switches.
3. PDPL / ZATCA-aware data layer.
4. Audit ledger on every approval.
5. Proof factory turning closed deals into next month's outbound.

## L4 — Twelve operational lines

Used on landing, in deck, in proposals.

1. "Drafts in. Approvals out."
2. "Score every account before you touch it."
3. "Saudi B2B by default."
4. "Trust-gated, not autonomous."
5. "Every action audit-ready."
6. "Founder owns the system; the system runs the motion."
7. "No guaranteed claims — we sell the machinery."
8. "Built for one decision per row."
9. "From scored account to signed proposal in one console."
10. "Proof compounds. Outbound improves."
11. "ZATCA-compliant invoicing baked in."
12. "Kill switch on every agent."

## L6 — Forbidden message patterns

- ❌ Revenue / leads / pipeline guarantees.
- ❌ "10x in 90 days" / multiplier claims.
- ❌ "Fully autonomous outbound."
- ❌ "Set it and forget it."
- ❌ Anything that implies external action without approval.
