# Revenue Sprint Scope

The Revenue Sprint is a two-week, single-outcome engagement. This document is the canonical scope contract. It is referenced by every proposal, every kick-off, and every QA pass.

## In scope

- One named bottleneck in the buyer's revenue motion (e.g., lead qualification, pipeline hygiene, proposal turn-around).
- One artefact produced and handed off: a working spreadsheet, a documented workflow, or a configured agent run.
- Up to four 30-minute syncs across two calendar weeks.
- One written wrap-up brief at completion, with explicit "what worked / what did not" sections.
- One AI Run Ledger entry per agent-touched action, tagged to the sprint ID.

## Out of scope

- Building net-new software.
- Sending outbound on the buyer's behalf (the buyer always presses send; see [docs/trust/AUTONOMY_POLICY.md](../../trust/AUTONOMY_POLICY.md)).
- PDPL or regulatory advice (A3; never executed under a sprint).
- Closing or signing contracts on the buyer's behalf.
- Public claims of revenue lift; only estimated, observed, and verified figures, properly labelled.

## Boundary rules

- If the buyer requests work that falls outside the in-scope list, the engagement does not silently extend. The Revenue Lead opens a Change Request and proposes either a sprint extension at the published rate or rolls the work into a follow-on engagement.
- If the work cannot be completed in two weeks, this is logged in the AI Run Ledger and the buyer is given a no-blame refund option per [REFUND_POLICY.md](./REFUND_POLICY.md).

## Inputs the buyer provides

- One named point of contact with decision authority.
- Read access to one data source (or a sanitized export).
- Two hours of buyer time across the sprint.

## Outputs Dealix produces

- A scope kick-off note (within 24h of payment).
- The agreed artefact (by day 12).
- A bilingual wrap-up brief (by day 14).
- A Proof Pack snippet attached to the AI Run Ledger.

## Owner

Revenue Lead owns this scope. Trust Lead counter-signs any change.
