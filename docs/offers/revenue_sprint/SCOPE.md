# Dealix Revenue Sprint — Scope

## In Scope
- Client intake and ICP confirmation.
- Qualified lead research against agreed sectors and geography.
- Deduplication and bad-fit removal.
- A/B/C priority scoring with evidence per account.
- Pain hypothesis per top account.
- Arabic and English outreach messages.
- Follow-up cadence (first message, follow-up 1, follow-up 2).
- 14-day action plan.
- Executive memo.
- QA pass against the public QA checklist.
- Handoff session with the client's owner.

## Out Of Scope
- Guaranteed sales or revenue.
- Guaranteed replies or meetings.
- Sending outreach on behalf of the client unless separately approved.
- Cold-list scraping or scraping that violates Terms of Service of any platform.
- CRM implementation, configuration, or migration.
- Ad campaign management.
- Legal, compliance, or regulatory certification.
- Translation of legal contracts.

## Assumptions
- The client provides ICP signals, exclusions, and approval contact.
- The client has the authority to approve the start condition.
- Sensitive client data stays in the private operating environment.
- Public claims (case studies, logos) require written approval.

## Change Requests
- Any change to scope, sectors, or volume is logged in writing.
- A new start condition is required if the change increases effort.

## Acceptance
The sprint is considered delivered when:
- Delivery report is handed over.
- QA checklist is recorded as PASS.
- Handoff session is held or formally waived in writing.

## Trust Note
Dealix does not guarantee sales, revenue, replies, or meetings.
Dealix delivers opportunity intelligence and execution assets.
