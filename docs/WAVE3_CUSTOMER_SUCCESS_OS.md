## 2. Customer Journey Mapping

### 2.1 Three-Phase Customer Lifecycle

```
PHASE 1: ONBOARDING (Days 0–7)
  Goal: Deliver sprint, get immediate wins
  Owner: Founder + Onboarding Specialist
  Duration: 5 business days (Diagnostic Sprint execution)
  
PHASE 2: IMPLEMENTATION (Days 8–42)
  Goal: Deliver pilot system, confirm ROI, build momentum
  Owner: CSM + Onboarding Specialist
  Duration: 4–6 weeks (Pilot → Production)
  
PHASE 3: EXPANSION & RETENTION (Month 3+)
  Goal: Maximize LTV through retainer + upsell
  Owner: CSM + Founder (strategic deals)
  Duration: 12+ months (ongoing)
```

### 2.2 Phase 1: Onboarding (Diagnostic Sprint)

**Duration**: 5 business days  
**Goal**: Deliver diagnostic outputs, get client buy-in for Phase 2

#### Pre-Sprint (Day 0)

**Customer actions**:
- [ ] Assign sprint sponsor (C-suite stakeholder)
- [ ] Provide 2 hours daily for team interviews
- [ ] Give access to data (CRM, sales records, email, etc.)
- [ ] Confirm success metrics they care about

**Dealix actions**:
- [ ] Kickoff call (30 min, all leaders present)
- [ ] Send sprint agenda + daily schedule
- [ ] Confirm data access + security protocols
- [ ] Set up collaboration tools (Slack, shared doc, etc.)

#### Daily Standup Rhythm (Days 1–5)

**Morning (9am Saudi)**:
- 15-min standup
- Founder: "Yesterday's findings. Today's agenda."
- Customer: "Any blockers?"
- Confirm afternoon interview time

**Afternoon (2pm Saudi)**:
- 60-min stakeholder interview
- Deep-dive on specific process (revenue, operations, customer, etc.)
- Capture pain points, workarounds, root causes
- Identify quick wins

**Evening (5pm Saudi)**:
- Founder documents findings
- Updates sprint brief for tomorrow
- Sends brief update email to customer stakeholder

#### Day 5 Delivery & Sign-off

**Morning deliverable review (9am–12pm)**:
- Present Workflow Map (current vs. ideal state)
- Showcase Leakage Report (quantified pain + impact)
- Walk through KPI Model (success metrics & targets)
- Discuss First OS Recommendation (ROI + timeline)

**Client validation (12pm–1pm)**:
- Finance/ops confirms numbers are realistic
- Leadership agrees on recommended OS
- Commitment to proceed with Phase 2 pilot or retainer

**Deliverables signed off**:
- [ ] Workflow Map approved
- [ ] Leakage Report approved
- [ ] KPI Model baseline established
- [ ] Next-step decision confirmed (pilot, retainer, or revisit)

#### Phase 1 Success Criteria

- [ ] All 5 deliverables completed + signed by customer
- [ ] Customer stakeholder NPS >7 (post-sprint survey)
- [ ] Decision made on Phase 2 (proceed or archive for later)
- [ ] No scope creep (sprint stayed focused on diagnosis, not custom development)
- [ ] Payment received for Phase 1 (50% deposit + 50% completion)

## 3. NPS & Customer Satisfaction Strategy

### 3.1 NPS Measurement Framework

**Definition**: "How likely are you to recommend Dealix to a peer?" (0–10 scale)

**Scoring**:
- Promoter (9–10): Active advocate, likely to refer
- Passive (7–8): Satisfied but not enthusiastic
- Detractor (0–6): Dissatisfied, may speak negatively

**Target**: NPS >50 (meaning 50%+ promoters, <10% detractors)

### 3.2 NPS Survey Cadence

| Survey | Timing | Audience | Key Question | Action Threshold |
|--------|--------|----------|---------------|------------------|
| **Post-Sprint** | Day 5 (end of diagnostic) | All clients | "How likely to recommend?" | <7 → escalate to founder |
| **Post-Pilot** | Day 42 (end of implementation) | All clients | "How satisfied with results?" | <7 → improve, then re-survey |
| **Monthly** | 1st of each month | Retainer clients | "How's the OS performing?" | <6 → emergency CSM review |
| **Quarterly** | End of Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4 | All active clients | "What's your overall experience?" | Analyze trends + plan improvements |
| **Annual** | 12-month mark | All clients | "Would you expand/renew?" | <7 → renewal at risk, intervene |

### 3.3 NPS Detractor Response Protocol

**If NPS <7 at any touchpoint**:

**Immediate (within 24 hours)**:
- [ ] CSM calls customer to understand dissatisfaction
- [ ] Root cause diagnosis (system issue? expectation mismatch? external factor?)
- [ ] Action plan created (fix, refund, pivot, or escalate)

**Short-term (next 1–2 weeks)**:
- [ ] Execute action plan (quick fix or improvement)
- [ ] Follow-up call to confirm resolution
- [ ] Re-survey NPS (if improvement significant enough)

**Escalation (if NPS stays <5)**:
- [ ] Founder involved (strategic discussion with customer leadership)
- [ ] Discount/credit offered (if system failed)
- [ ] Renewal/retainer at risk → pause pending improvement

### 3.4 NPS Improvement Playbook

**Common detractor reasons & fixes**:

| Reason | Fix | Timeline |
|--------|-----|----------|
| "OS didn't deliver promised ROI" | Audit metrics + rebaseline expectations | 2 weeks |
| "System is slow/buggy" | Technical debugging + performance optimization | 1 week |
| "Team didn't get trained properly" | Retraining session + documentation improvement | 1 week |
| "We don't have time to use it" | Process simplification or automation adjustment | 2 weeks |
| "Expectations set too high" | Reset goals + show early wins | Immediate |
| "Competitor solution seems better" | Feature comparison + pricing discussion | Immediate |

## 4. Retention Playbook & Intervention Framework

### 4.1 Retention Triggers (Early Warning System)

**Monitor these indicators monthly**:

| Indicator | Yellow Flag | Red Flag | Action |
|-----------|-----------|---------|--------|
| **KPI Tracking** | <70% of target | <50% of target | CSM review + adjust strategy |
| **OS Usage** | <50% of team using system | System unused for 1 week | Emergency check-in + retraining |
| **NPS** | 7–8 (passive) | <6 (detractor) | Founder call + recovery plan |
| **Communication** | 1–2 week gaps in response | No response to emails | Escalation + outreach |
| **Engagement** | Skipping monthly calls | Declining meeting requests | Strategic CSM intervention |
| **Renewal Timeline** | <90 days to renewal | Renewal not discussed | Renewal negotiation + offer adjustment |

### 4.2 Customer Health Score (Predictive Model)

**Calculate quarterly**:

```
Health Score = (KPI % × 30) + (NPS score × 3) + (Usage % × 20) + (Engagement × 50)
              ÷ 100

Interpretation:
  >75 = Healthy (on track for renewal + upsell)
  60–75 = At risk (needs CSM intervention)
  <60 = Critical (founder intervention required)
```

**Example**:
- Client A: KPI 85%, NPS 9, Usage 90%, Engagement strong = Score 8.2/10 (Green)
- Client B: KPI 50%, NPS 6, Usage 40%, Engagement weak = Score 5.1/10 (Red)

### 4.3 Retention Interventions

#### Yellow Flag Intervention (Health Score 60–75)

**Trigger**: Client is passive or at-risk

**CSM Actions**:
- [ ] Schedule 1:1 call with customer stakeholder (30 min)
- [ ] Review KPI performance (celebrate wins, diagnose gaps)
- [ ] Identify pain points (what's not working? What could be better?)
- [ ] Create 30-day improvement plan (specific actions + timeline)
- [ ] Offer hands-on support (additional training, custom reporting, etc.)

**Deliverables**:
- 30-day improvement plan (email + shared document)
- Weekly check-ins (confirm progress)
- Renewed engagement + confidence

#### Red Flag Intervention (Health Score <60)

**Trigger**: Client is dissatisfied, at high churn risk

**Founder Actions**:
- [ ] Executive call with customer C-suite (60 min)
- [ ] Deep dive on current situation (what happened? why?), without blame
- [ ] Offer significant remediation (additional system, price reduction, extended pilot, etc.)
- [ ] Create recovery roadmap (specific milestones + owner assignments)
- [ ] Weekly monitoring for 60 days (until back to healthy)

**Options**:
1. **Deepen engagement**: Upsell second OS (shift focus from problems to opportunities)
2. **Extend implementation**: 30-day pilot extension (give more time to see ROI)
3. **Price adjustment**: Reduce monthly retainer (acknowledge gap, build trust)
4. **Strategic reset**: New success plan with different metrics (reset expectations)

**Success metric**: Get Health Score back >65 within 90 days or accept churn.

### 4.4 Churn Prevention Tactics

**Proactive retention actions (before red flags)**:

| Tactic | Frequency | Owner | Expected Impact |
|--------|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| **Business Review** | Monthly | CSM | Celebrate progress, identify issues early |
| **Surprise & Delight** | Quarterly | Founder | Extra feature, training, or discount |
| **Referral Program** | Monthly | CSM | Create ambassador mindset |
| **Strategic Planning** | Quarterly | Founder + CSM | Show expansion roadmap, grow commitment |
| **Advisory Board** | Bi-annually | Founder | Build deep relationship with leadership |
| **Customer Community** | Quarterly | CSM | Connect customers, build loyalty |

## 6. Customer Advisory Board (CAB) Framework

### 6.1 CAB Purpose & Structure

**Purpose**: Strategic guidance + trust building with high-value customers

**When to invite**: After 6+ months of successful retainer, strong NPS (9+), and expansion interest

**Frequency**: 2x per year (strategic planning sessions)

**Size**: 3–5 customers (not all customer base, only strategic ones)

### 6.2 CAB Membership Criteria

Customer qualifies for CAB if they meet ALL:
- [ ] Active retainer for 6+ months
- [ ] NPS score 9–10 (Promoter)
- [ ] KPI targets achieved + exceeded
- [ ] Proceeding to 2+ OS implementations (multi-product commitment)
- [ ] Executive (C-suite) participation + commitment
- [ ] Located in or willing to travel to Saudi Arabia (for occasional in-person meetings)

### 6.3 CAB Meeting Agenda (Half-day, In-person or Zoom)

**Goal**: Strategic planning + product feedback + relationship deepening

**Duration**: 3–4 hours

**Attendees**:
- Dealix: Founder + Product Lead (future) + CSM
- Customer: 2–3 executives (CEO, COO, or VP-level)

**Agenda**:

1. **Welcome & Context** (15 min)
   - Dealix mission update + year-to-date progress
   - Agenda overview + confidentiality agreement

2. **Customer Wins Review** (30 min)
   - Each CAB member shares their Dealix impact story
   - Key metrics achieved (revenue, efficiency, team capability)
   - Breakthrough moments

3. **Market Trends & Opportunities** (30 min)
   - Dealix vision for next 12 months
   - Emerging OS opportunities (new systems in development)
   - Customer perspective on market needs

4. **Product Feedback Session** (45 min)
   - Demo of upcoming features/systems
   - Direct feedback on product roadmap
   - "What should we build next?" brainstorm

5. **Strategic Planning** (30 min)
   - Customer expansion roadmap (next 3–5 systems)
   - ROI projections for expansion
   - Partnership opportunities (reseller, co-marketing, etc.)

6. **Networking & Closing** (30 min)
   - Informal connection time between customers
   - Dealix founder 1:1 with each member
   - Next CAB meeting confirmed + action items

### 6.4 CAB Post-Meeting Deliverables

- Personalized expansion proposal for each member (3–5 systems over 12 months)
- Quarterly strategy calls (between full CAB meetings)
- Early access to new OS features (beta testing)
- Discounted pricing on expansion services (5–10% discount)
- Co-marketing opportunity (case study, LinkedIn post, customer reference)

### 6.5 CAB Value Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **CAB Member Retention** | 100% (no churn) | Retention rate at 12 months |
| **CAB Expansion Rate** | 3x revenue per member | Average LTV within 12 months |
| **Referral Generation** | 2+ qualified leads per member | Warm intros + referrals generated |
| **Case Study Participation** | 100% willing | Anonymized case study publication |
