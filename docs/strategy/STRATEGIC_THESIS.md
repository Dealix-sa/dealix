# الفرضية الاستراتيجية — Strategic Thesis

> Who Dealix serves, what we sell, why now, why us, what we won't do.

## Purpose
Anchor every decision to a written thesis. When in doubt, the thesis decides.

## Owner
Founder/CEO.

## Inputs
- Market observation (Saudi B2B, AI operations adoption).
- Customer conversations (`docs/customer/` notes).
- Existing strategy docs (`docs/strategy/CATEGORY_DESIGN.md`, `MARKET_MAP_SAUDI.md`, `CEO_STRATEGY.md`).
- Founder's distinctive capability inventory.

## Outputs
- This file (kept stable; changes require Monthly Strategy Review approval).
- Referenced by `ICP_STRATEGY.md`, `GTM_STRATEGY.md`, `MOAT_SYSTEM.md`, `COMPETITIVE_STRATEGY.md`.

## Rules
1. The thesis changes at most once per quarter, in the Monthly Strategy Review.
2. Every active bet must trace to a thesis pillar.
3. Bets that contradict the thesis are killed, regardless of revenue.
4. The "What we won't do" list is enforced — items there cannot be added without a thesis amendment.

## Metrics
- % of active bets traceable to thesis: 100%.
- Thesis amendments per year: target ≤ 2.
- Bets killed for thesis violation: tracked.

## Cadence
Reviewed quarterly. Stable otherwise.

## Evidence
This file + the Monthly Strategy Review record of amendments.

## Verifier
`make thesis-verify` — checks every bet in `STRATEGIC_BETS.md` references a thesis pillar number.

## Runtime Command
`make thesis-review` (manual prompt, no automation).

---

## Who we serve

**Beachhead**: mid-market Saudi B2B operators (annual revenue SAR 10M–500M) who need evidenced operational improvement, not "AI" theater.

**Primary buyer**: founder, COO, or commercial director who personally owns a P&L line and can decide within 30 days.

## What we sell

A laddered set of operational engagements, each producing reusable evidence:

1. Signal Sample (paid diagnostic).
2. Revenue Sprint (focused 4–6 week engagement).
3. Managed Pilot (productized variant of Sprint).
4. Revenue Desk (retainer for ongoing operational discipline).
5. Dealix OS (eventual productization — license + light services).

See `docs/revenue/OFFER_LADDER.md`.

## Why now

- Saudi mid-market is investing in operational rigor, not just digitalization.
- Buyers are skeptical of "AI" marketing; they reward evidence and disclosure.
- Vision 2030 mandates measurable operational outcomes; consultants who can show them win.
- The market has more pilots than proof; Dealix's discipline of "evidence per sprint" is rare.

## Why us

- Founder operating background in commercial discipline, not academic AI.
- Bilingual AR/EN from day one — not localized, native.
- An operating system (this repo) as a moat, not a slide deck.
- Bias to "case-safe templates" instead of unverifiable claims.

## Thesis pillars (numbered for bet traceability)

1. **Evidence-led commerce**: every paid engagement produces a case-safe artifact.
2. **Sprint factory**: delivery is templated; founder is not the bottleneck after 10 successful runs.
3. **Sector data compounding**: each sprint adds to anonymized sector benchmarks (`docs/sector-reports/`).
4. **Trust as product**: governance and disclosure are surfaces, not afterthoughts.
5. **Bilingual native**: AR and EN parity at proposal, delivery, and evidence level.

## What we won't do

- Sell "AI transformation" branding.
- Guarantee revenue, ROI, or conversion outcomes.
- Run cold outreach, scraping, or automated WhatsApp/LinkedIn sends.
- Take engagements where the buyer cannot decide within 30 days.
- Take engagements that forbid all forms of anonymized evidence (no artifact = no engagement).
- Build SaaS speculatively; productization requires 10 successful sprint runs first.
- Pursue markets outside Saudi until the beachhead rule in `MARKET_ENTRY_DECISION.md` is met.

## When the thesis would change

- Beachhead saturated (≥ 50 PSDE per year).
- Buyer behavior shift (decision cycles > 90 days dominate).
- Regulatory change altering disclosure economics.
- A new pillar earns its place via three successful bets traceable to it.

## القواعد العربية
1. الفرضية تتغير مرة في الربع كحدّ أقصى، في المراجعة الشهرية.
2. كل رهان نشط يربط بركيزة من ركائز الفرضية.
3. قائمة "ما لن نفعله" مُلزِمة، تعديلها يحتاج تعديل الفرضية.
