# Strategic Thesis

## One-Line Thesis

Saudi mid-market companies will pay for revenue intelligence that is delivered
as a managed service before they will pay for software, and Dealix wins by
turning that managed service into evidence that compounds into recurring
revenue.

## Why Now

1. Saudi Vision 2030 has multiplied the number of companies under pressure
   to grow revenue with limited sales headcount.
2. AI is now usable by founder-led companies to score, draft, and route
   opportunities — but the trust gate is the bottleneck.
3. International tools do not respect Arabic, local regulations, or local
   buying behavior.

## Why Us

1. Saudi-native operator with first-hand sales context.
2. A doctrine that puts evidence and trust ahead of feature count.
3. A modular OS that can sell as a service today and as software when
   proven repeatable.

## What We Believe (and Are Willing to Test)

- Buyers want outcomes, not dashboards.
- Trust is the moat — moves slowly, but compounds.
- Managed delivery generates the data needed to build SaaS later.
- One Saudi case study is worth ten international ones.

## What We Reject

- Spray-and-pray cold outreach.
- Selling SaaS before delivering the outcome manually.
- Importing pricing or positioning from US playbooks without translation.
- "Move fast and break things" applied to client-facing claims.
