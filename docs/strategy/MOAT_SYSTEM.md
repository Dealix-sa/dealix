# Moat System

> A moat is what stops a smart, well-funded competitor from copying you.
> Most "moats" are wishes. We measure ours.

## The Five Candidate Moats For Dealix

1. **Arabic + Saudi-context data and prompts.**
   Every outreach hook, sector report, and intelligence pack is bilingual
   and Saudi-specific. Compounds with every sprint delivered.
   Measure: number of Saudi-named entities in our research graph;
   number of sector-specific hook templates.

2. **Evidence-bound delivery.**
   Every claim links to verifiable evidence. Buyers trust because they
   can check.
   Measure: % of A-priority leads with linked evidence in the Proof Pack;
   number of overclaim incidents (target 0).

3. **Founder trust and brand.**
   The founder signs the work. Founder reputation compounds over time.
   Measure: named referrals; LinkedIn engagement on proof posts;
   inbound asks naming the founder.

4. **Productized templates and playbooks.**
   Each repeatable workflow we capture is a moat against future
   competitors who must rebuild from scratch.
   Measure: workflows at 5+ successes (template-grade);
   workflows at 10+ successes (automation-grade).

5. **Capital asset registry.**
   Every public proof artifact, signed contract, completed sprint, and
   approved case study is a permanent moat asset.
   Measure: capital asset count + value contribution per category.

## Moat Maturity Levels

For each candidate moat, score 0–4:

| Level | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 0 | Asserted, not measured |
| 1 | Measured once |
| 2 | Tracked monthly, improving |
| 3 | Visible to customers (they cite it) |
| 4 | Visible to competitors (they avoid the segment) |

Target by month 6: at least one moat at level 2.
Target by month 12: at least one moat at level 3.

## What is **not** a moat

- "AI" — table stakes.
- "Network effects" — we are not a marketplace.
- "Brand" without measurement.
- "Speed" — copyable.
- "Hard work" — copyable.

## Moat Investment Rules

- Every Sprint must add to **at least one** moat (data, templates,
  proof, or referrals).
- A Sprint that adds to zero moats is a Sprint we under-priced.
- The Capital Allocation review weights moat-building above feature work.
