# Go-To-Market Strategy

> Founder-led outbound, sample-first, sprint-priced, retainer-targeted.

## The Sequence

1. **Founder-led outbound first.** Until repeatability is proven, the
   founder does the work. No outsourced SDRs.
2. **Samples before calls.** Never sell on promise; always sell on a
   concrete artifact the prospect can read in 5 minutes.
3. **Sprint as the wedge.** A small, fixed-price engagement that proves
   capability without bidding for a long sales cycle.
4. **Retainer after delivery.** Every completed Sprint asks the retainer
   question with evidence.
5. **SaaS only after repeatability.** SaaS is the *last* product to
   build, not the first.

## Channels — In Order

1. **Direct DM to decision-maker** (LinkedIn / email).
2. **Warm referral** from existing customers and partners.
3. **Founder content** (LinkedIn, X) as proof, not as a lead engine.
4. **Approved case studies** — the highest-trust artifact we can publish.
5. **Partners** — only after we have proof to share with them.

## Channel Discipline

- We do **not** run paid ads before PMF.
- We do **not** run cold email blasts.
- We do **not** scrape lists; we research the right 100, not the random
  10,000.
- We do **not** automate outreach in a way that removes the founder
  signature.

## The 25-DM Week

- Each week: ≥ 25 named, researched DMs to ICP decision-makers.
- Each DM: one personalised hook, one offer (sample, Sprint, or call).
- Cadence: see `docs/sales/FOLLOW_UP_SEQUENCE.md`.

## Sample-First Selling

A **sample** is a 1-page artifact tailored to the prospect:

- 3 named target accounts with Arabic + English evidence
- 1 outreach hook per account
- 1 paragraph of strategic context
- A clear call-to-action: "Want 30 like these? That is the Sprint."

Samples cost the founder 30–45 minutes. They convert at 5–10x cold DMs.

## Sprint as Wedge

See `docs/revenue/OFFER_LADDER.md`.
The Sprint is the lowest-risk way for a prospect to test our quality.

## Retainer Conversion

Every Sprint handoff includes the Retainer Ask:

> "Based on this Sprint, we recommend a monthly Revenue Desk at
> [price]. Should I send the scope?"

If the customer says no, ask: "What would have made this a yes?"
Record the answer in the Friction Log.

## What we will **not** do this year

- Outsourced SDR teams
- Paid acquisition
- Marketplace
- Self-serve SaaS
- RFP-driven enterprise sales

## Review

Quarterly, in the Monthly Strategy Review.
A channel can be added only after the prior channels have hit their
repeatability targets.
