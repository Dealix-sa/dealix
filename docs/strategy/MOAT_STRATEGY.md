# Moat Strategy

> What makes Dealix hard to copy after the first 6, 12, 24 months.

## The Moat Stack (in order of strength)

1. **Trust + Evidence Operating Model**
   - Every external claim has an audited evidence pack
   - Every automated action has an approval trail
   - Every incident has a logged post-mortem
   - Why hard to copy: requires discipline before customers, not after

2. **Sector Playbooks With Proof**
   - Each Tier 1 sector gets a playbook from real, won engagements
   - Includes: scoring weights, pain hypotheses, message patterns, objections, sample artifacts
   - Why hard to copy: requires shipped sprints, not desk research

3. **Productized Pricing + Productized Delivery**
   - Fixed scope at every rung of the ladder
   - Delivery Playbook = how to actually do the work
   - Why hard to copy: requires productization discipline (most agencies can't)

4. **Bilingual Arabic-First Operating Layer**
   - Saudi-native messaging, objections, sample artifacts
   - Why hard to copy: requires founder in-market

5. **Founder + AI System As The Eat-Your-Cooking Proof**
   - Dealix runs the Dealix business on Dealix
   - Every Daily Brief, Weekly Review, decision log = public-safe proof
   - Why hard to copy: requires using the product on the product (and surviving)

6. **Compounding Customer Knowledge Base**
   - Win/loss data tagged by sector × message × price
   - Sector benchmarks built from real engagements
   - Why hard to copy: requires longitudinal data we'll have and they won't

## Moat Investments This Quarter

- **Trust gates** → ship `dealix/trust/` modules (approval matrix, claim guard, policy engine)
- **Sector playbooks** → produce 1 playbook per Tier 1 sector with first sprint
- **Evidence packs** → every paid sprint exits with a public-safe case study draft
- **Public learning** → weekly LinkedIn post on a learning, not a hype

## Moat Anti-Patterns (what would weaken the moat)

- Shipping a feature without trust gates
- Publishing a case study without evidence pack approval
- Discounting to win deals (signals: not productized)
- Hiring before the playbook exists (hire dilutes, then dies)
- Building a "platform" before having an operating motion

## Moat Test (apply quarterly)

For each moat layer, ask:
- **Time to copy:** How long would a well-funded competitor take to replicate this? (Target: > 6 months for each layer)
- **Defensibility under attack:** If a competitor priced 50% below us tomorrow, would we still win? (Target: yes, on trust + outcomes)
- **Compounding rate:** Is this moat getting stronger each month? (Target: yes — more evidence, more sectors, more playbooks)

If any layer fails any test → escalate to Weekly CEO Review.

## What This Moat Is Not

- Not the AI model (commoditized — any vendor can swap)
- Not the codebase (open-source-grade tooling exists)
- Not the brand (we'll earn brand later, on the back of trust + proof)
- Not the founder personally (the moat must outlast the founder's bandwidth)

## What Strengthens The Moat Most Per Hour

Top 3 highest-leverage moat work, ranked:
1. Shipping a paid sprint → produces evidence + sector data + case study
2. Writing the playbook from a shipped sprint → makes the next sprint repeatable
3. Public learning post tied to evidence → compounds reputation + draws ICP

Everything else is lower-leverage. Use this list when you're tempted to "build something cool".
