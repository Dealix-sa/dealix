# Moat Strategy

## Layers of Defensibility

Dealix builds five layers. None alone is a moat; together they compound.

### 1. Evidence Moat
Every delivery generates a proof pack, a case study, and a measured outcome.
After 12 retainers, no new entrant can match our library of Saudi-specific evidence.

### 2. Trust Moat
The Trust OS, approval matrix, and No-Overclaim Policy are difficult to copy
because they require restraint, not engineering. Buyers prefer the slower-moving,
trust-heavy vendor when revenue is on the line.

### 3. Workflow Moat
The Dealix workflow library (offer ladder, proposal templates, delivery
playbooks) is tuned to Saudi mid-market behavior. Each new client refines it.

### 4. Network Moat
Founders introduce other founders. Every retainer is asked for one
introduction at month three. Over 18 months this becomes a referral engine.

### 5. Data Moat
With consent and proper governance, anonymized pipeline and proposal
performance data feeds the Learning OS, which improves scoring and drafting
quality for the next cohort.

## What Is NOT Our Moat

- Model access (commodity).
- Generic SaaS features (replicable).
- One-time integrations (decay).
- Founder presence on stage (does not scale).

## Test for "Is It a Moat?"

A capability is a moat only if:
1. It would take a new entrant at least 12 months to replicate.
2. It improves every quarter under our operating cadence.
3. Removing it would visibly hurt the close rate or retention.
