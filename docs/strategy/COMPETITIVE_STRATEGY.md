# Competitive Strategy

## How We Win

We do not compete on feature count, headcount, or marketing spend. We win
on three axes: Saudi context, trust posture, and outcome ownership.

## Axes

### 1. Saudi Context

- Arabic-first artifacts.
- Local data residency stance documented and verifiable.
- Pricing and proposals built for SAR cashflow rhythms.
- Direct, founder-to-founder selling motion.

### 2. Trust Posture

- Public No-Overclaim Policy.
- Approval matrix gates every external claim.
- Trust tests run on every PR (`tests/trust/`).
- Risk-classified workflows (see `docs/trust/WORKFLOW_RISK_CLASSIFICATION.md`).

### 3. Outcome Ownership

- We sell delivered outcomes, not seats.
- Sprints have a defined deliverable, not a list of tasks.
- Retainers carry a written scorecard.

## Competitor Categories

| Category | Examples | Where We Win | Where We Don't |
|---|---|---|---|
| International RevOps tools | HubSpot, Salesforce add-ons | Saudi context, trust, managed delivery | Feature breadth |
| Local sales agencies | Various boutique agencies | AI leverage, repeatability, evidence trail | Brand recognition |
| AI demo shops | Solo builders, freelancers | Trust posture, productized delivery | Hourly cost |
| In-house attempts | Client builds their own | Speed, focus, ongoing iteration | Internal political capital |

## Defensive Moves

- Lock in retainer clients with quarterly reviews before competitors can re-pitch.
- Publish at least one case study every 60 days.
- Convert delivered work into reusable templates inside the company OS.

## Plays We Do Not Run

- Discount wars.
- "Land and expand" without a paying landing.
- Public attacks on competitors.
