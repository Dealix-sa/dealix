# الاستراتيجية التنافسية — Competitive Strategy

> How Dealix differs from agencies, SaaS, freelancers, in-house teams. No comparisons by feature list.

## Purpose
State the competitive position with discipline. Avoid agency-style feature comparisons. Anchor on operating posture.

## Owner
Founder/CEO.

## Inputs
- Strategic thesis (`STRATEGIC_THESIS.md`).
- Existing benchmark docs (`docs/strategy/COMPETITOR_BENCHMARK.md`, `COMPETITIVE_POSITIONING.md`).
- Customer feedback (`docs/customer/`).

## Outputs
- Positioning statement (below).
- Comparison frame used in proposals (not bashing competitors — clarifying choice).

## Rules
1. Never name a competitor pejoratively in any customer-facing doc.
2. Never claim a feature parity Dealix has not delivered for 3 paid customers.
3. Differentiation is stated in terms of operating posture, not technology.
4. If a competitor offers something Dealix should, log it as a candidate bet, not as defensiveness.
5. "We are better at X" requires evidence; otherwise say "We make a different choice on X."

## Metrics
- Customer-facing competitive mentions matching rule 1: 100%.
- Bet candidates sourced from competitive scan: tracked quarterly.

## Cadence
Quarterly review.

## Evidence
This file + the Monthly Strategy Review competitive section.

## Verifier
`make competitive-verify` — scans customer-facing docs for banned phrases.

## Runtime Command
`make competitive-scan`

## The four alternative categories

| Category | What they offer | Where Dealix is different |
|---|---|---|
| Consulting agencies | Strategy decks, time-and-materials, named teams | Dealix delivers operating artifacts, not slides; templated sprints, not bespoke engagements |
| Horizontal SaaS | Generic CRM / ops tools with a free trial | Dealix delivers the operating discipline first; tools are downstream of the operating model |
| Freelancers / boutique studios | Hands-on execution, project-by-project | Dealix is a system with documentation, evidence, and governance, not a person-dependent shop |
| In-house build | Hire ops manager + tools | Dealix is a sprint factory — faster to first evidence; transferrable artifacts on completion |

## Positioning statement

For Saudi mid-market operators who have run pilots without measurable operational change, Dealix runs evidenced operational sprints with case-safe artifacts and transferrable templates — so the customer keeps the playbook, not just the result.

## What Dealix does NOT compete on
- Cheapest hourly rate (we are not the lowest cost).
- Largest team headcount.
- Number of "AI" features (we do not lead with AI naming).
- Famous logos (we lead with evidence, not name-drops).

## What Dealix DOES compete on
- Time to first evidence (Signal Sample to artifact).
- Evidence quality (case-safe, anonymized, replicable).
- Bilingual native delivery (AR + EN parity).
- Governance posture (disclosure, trust pack, refund policy in writing).
- Transfer of artifacts at sprint close (the customer keeps the playbook).

## Comparison frame used in proposals
```
What you might also consider:

- A consulting agency: better for board-deck moments; usually no transferrable
  artifact at close.
- A SaaS tool: better if your operating model is already crisp; less useful when
  the operating model itself is the gap.
- A freelancer: better for one-off execution; risk concentrated on one person.
- An in-house hire: better if you are committing 3+ years to this capability.

Dealix is a 4–6 week sprint factory producing a transferrable playbook plus the
operational outcome. Estimated value is not Verified value / القيمة التقديرية
ليست قيمة مُتحقَّقة.
```

## Avoiding the trap
The founder is tempted to imitate competitors that look successful. Imitation costs the moat. Imitate only after a Go/No-Go gate.

## القواعد العربية
1. لا نذكر منافسًا بسوء في وثيقة موجهة للعميل.
2. لا ندّعي تكافؤ ميزة لم نطبّقها لثلاثة عملاء مدفوعين.
3. التميز يُعبَّر عنه بموقف تشغيلي، لا بقائمة ميزات.
