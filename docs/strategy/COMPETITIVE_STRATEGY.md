# Competitive Strategy

> We do not compete on "AI". Everyone has AI.
> We compete on **trust, evidence, and Saudi-native execution**.

## The Competitive Landscape

| Category | Examples | Where they win | Where they lose |
|----------|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Scraped-list vendors | Apollo, Lusha, ZoomInfo | Volume + price | No personalisation, no Saudi context, no trust |
| Outsourced SDR agencies | International + regional | Capacity | Generic, English-only, transient |
| In-house sales teams | The buyer's own team | Domain knowledge | Capacity, time-to-pipeline |
| AI copywriting tools | Generic LLM tools | Speed | No evidence, no human signature |
| Marketplaces | LinkedIn, vendor directories | Reach | No depth, no qualification |

## Where Dealix Wins

1. **Arabic + Saudi context** that international tools structurally
   cannot match.
2. **Evidence-bound output** that buyers can verify in minutes.
3. **Founder signature** that signals trust.
4. **Sprint as wedge** — low friction to try us.
5. **Retainer continuity** that scraped-list vendors cannot offer.

## Where We Do **Not** Try To Win

- Price below scraped-list vendors. We are 5–10x more expensive.
- Volume of leads per dollar. They win that.
- Speed of self-serve onboarding. We are not self-serve yet.
- Mass-market English-only motion. Not our customer.

## Positioning Statement

> Dealix is the **Arabic-native, evidence-bound, founder-led** revenue
> intelligence operator for Saudi B2B companies who refuse to send their
> Saudi customers a generic English cold email.

## Competitive Anti-Patterns

- Listing features in a sales call (table stakes).
- Comparing on price (we lose).
- Comparing on volume (we lose).
- Comparing on speed-of-tool (we lose).
- We compare on **outcomes**: replies received, opportunities surfaced,
  retainer extensions earned.

## Battle-Card Discipline

Maintain in `docs/sales/BATTLECARDS.md`:

- 3 most common alternatives the buyer mentions.
- One sentence on how we are different.
- One sentence on the evidence we cite.

## Review

Quarterly. Refresh battle-cards after every loss.
Loss reasons go into the Friction Log.
