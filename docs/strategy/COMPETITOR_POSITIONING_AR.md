# Competitor Positioning (Honest)

> **Status:** Default contrast table. Update after every real win or loss.
> **Companion:** `DEALIX_POSITIONING_BIBLE_AR.md`.

## The principle

Dealix is **a layer above** existing tools, not a replacement for them. The contrast we draw is "we add the missing layer" — not "we are better than X". The second framing is overclaim; the first is accurate.

## Direct vs indirect competitors

| Type | Examples | What they sell | Where Dealix fits |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CRM | HubSpot, Zoho, Salesforce | contact + deal + pipeline | Dealix reads from CRM, layers ops + decisions on top |
| Marketing automation | Mailchimp, Marketo, ActiveCampaign | email + nurture | Dealix is not an email tool; we generate drafts that go into your tool |
| AI chat | ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini | general LLM | Dealix is Saudi-specific, governance-bounded, evidence-bound |
| Local B2B agencies | various | execution, content, ads | we don't execute; we give the agency + founder visibility |
| WhatsApp bots | WATI, Twilio, Yellow.ai | automated replies | bots are outbound; Dealix is read-only + decision layer |
| Lead intelligence | Apollo, ZoomInfo, Lusha | contact data | we don't scrape; we work with the leads you already have |
| Analytics dashboards | Looker, Metabase | visualization | we ship a daily digest, not a dashboard; dashboards are inputs, decisions are outputs |

## What we don't compete on

- **Cheapest price.** We are not the cheapest. We are the most operationally honest.
- **Feature count.** We have fewer features than HubSpot. We have more opinion.
- **"All-in-one."** We are not all-in-one. We are the layer that makes all-in-one work.

## What we do compete on

| Dimension | Dealix | Typical competitor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Saudi-first | Yes, by design | No, retrofitted |
| Governance | Built-in, evidence-bound | Optional, bolt-on |
| Trust posture | Default to human approval | Default to automation |

| First-touch | Audit, not demo | Demo, not audit |

## How to handle "what about X?" in a sales call

If the prospect says:

> **"We already use HubSpot."**

You say:

> "ممتاز. Dealix يقرأ من HubSpot ويعطيك الطبقة اللي فوقه — اليومية، المتابعة، الفرص الضايعة. ما نطلب منك تطلع من HubSpot."

> **"We have a VA doing follow-up."**

You say:

> "حل ممتاز للمرحلة الأولى. الـ audit يكشف لك حجم الفرص اللي الـ VA ما يقدر يلحقها. لو في فجوة، Dealix يسدها بدون ما تستأجر ثالث."

> **"We tried a chatbot, it didn't work."**

You say:

> "غالباً لأن الـ chatbot كان outbound. Dealix بالعكس — ما يرسل شيء بدون إذنك. يقرأ ويرتب ويطلع توصيات، وأنت تقرر."

> **"Your competitor X is cheaper."**

You say:

> "معك حق. الحل الأرخص مناسب إذا كنت تحتاج تنفيذ. الحل مالتنا مناسب إذا كنت تحتاج رؤية قرارات يومية. الأولى أرخص، الثانية أوفر على المدى الطويل."

> **"We'll just build it ourselves."**

You say:

> "تقدرون. السؤال هو: كم شهر وكم شخص حتى توصلون لنفس النتيجة؟ الـ audit يجاوبكم على هذا السؤال في 5 أيام — أرخص من البناء."

## Red lines (never say these in a competitor contrast)

- "We are better than HubSpot." (overclaim; integration, not replacement)
- "We replaced their VA." (we don't replace humans; we add a layer)
- "We do everything they do, but cheaper." (we don't)
- "AI is smarter than your team." (we are AI-with-humans, not AI-instead-of-humans)

## What the founder should track

Every 30 days, log:

- How many prospects mentioned a specific competitor?
- Which objection opened the door vs closed it?
- Did the prospect end up choosing Dealix or the competitor? Why?
- Did we lose on price, features, or trust?

Update this doc every quarter.
