# Dealix North Star

> The one sentence that overrides all other sentences.

## The North Star

**Qualified B2B revenue opportunities, converted into paid Revenue Sprints
and retainers.**

That is what Dealix does. Not "AI". Not "platform". Not "operating system".
Those are how we deliver. The North Star is **paid revenue opportunities
moving through our funnel**.

## Why this and not a vaguer mission

A North Star must be:

1. **Falsifiable** — we can measure whether it is moving (paid sprints +
   retainers, monthly).
2. **Actionable today** — every team member can ask "did my last hour
   move the North Star?" and get a clear yes/no.
3. **Defensible against scope creep** — anything that does not move the
   North Star is by default deferred.

"Become the AI category leader of Saudi Arabia" is a vision.
A vision is not a North Star. A vision can take 10 years.
A North Star tells you what to do **today**.

## The North Star Metric

Single metric: **Monthly cash collected from Revenue Sprints + active retainer MRR.**

That is one number. Everything else is a leading indicator of that number.

## Leading Indicators

In priority order:

1. DMs sent to qualified ICP per week
2. Reply rate
3. Samples sent
4. Calls booked
5. Proposals sent
6. Proposal-to-payment conversion rate
7. Sprint delivery on time
8. Retainer asks made
9. Retainer asks accepted

## What is **not** the North Star

- LinkedIn followers
- GitHub commits
- Documentation pages
- Internal tool sophistication
- Number of agents built

These are means. They are not the end.
A founder who optimises for means without proving the end is doing
craft, not business.

## North Star Review

Once a quarter, in the Monthly Strategy Review of month 3, 6, 9, 12,
ask: **Should the North Star change?**

The bar to change it is **evidence**:

- 6 months of customer pull pointing to a different category, or
- A material shift in market conditions, or
- A failure of the current North Star to predict revenue.

Until evidence shows up: the North Star does not change.
