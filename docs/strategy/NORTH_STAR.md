# Dealix North Star

> The one sentence the company is organized around.
> If a decision can't be tied back to this, it's the wrong decision.

## The Statement

**Dealix turns Saudi mid-market commercial chaos into trustworthy, AI-operated revenue, one founder at a time — starting with the founder who builds it.**

## Decomposed

- **Saudi mid-market** — companies between SAR 5M and SAR 500M annual revenue, founder-run or family-owned, with manual sales/ops processes
- **commercial chaos** — disconnected spreadsheets, unmeasured outreach, unscored leads, untracked promises, opaque delivery
- **trustworthy** — every claim has evidence, every action has an approval trail, every risk has an owner
- **AI-operated** — AI prepares, humans approve, workflows execute, policies govern, metrics improve the system
- **revenue** — cash collected, not vanity metrics
- **one founder at a time** — single-founder Revenue Sprint is the atomic unit before any team play
- **starting with the founder who builds it** — Dealix runs itself on Dealix; eat the cooking

## North-Star Metric

**Cash collected (SAR) per month.**

Not MRR alone. Not ARR. Not pipeline. Cash that the bank can see.

We track MRR as a leading indicator. We track pipeline as a planning input. We track delivery as a quality gate. But cash collected is the one we report up.

## Counter Metric

**Trust incidents per month.** Target = 0.

If cash goes up and trust incidents go up, we are degrading the moat. Trust beats speed.

## Time Horizon

- **30 days:** First paid Revenue Sprint with evidence pack
- **60 days:** Three paid sprints + first retainer in pilot
- **90 days:** First retainer signed + first public case study
- **6 months:** SAR 25K/month cash collected for two consecutive months
- **12 months:** SAR 100K/month cash collected, 5+ active retainers, 3+ sector playbooks proven

## What This Is Not

- Not a platform-first strategy (platform is a consequence, not the entry)
- Not an enterprise-only strategy (mid-market first, then up)
- Not a US-first strategy (Saudi first, then GCC, then MENA)
- Not a multi-product strategy (one offer ladder, productized rungs only)

## What Has To Be True For This To Work

1. Saudi mid-market founders **will pay** for productized revenue-ops help (validated by first 3 paid sprints)
2. AI-assisted + founder-approved delivery is **faster + cheaper** than agency consulting (validated by delivery time + margin)
3. Trust as a wedge **wins** against speed-first competitors (validated by retention + referral)
4. The founder can stay focused on this for 12 months without pivoting (validated by Focus Policy adherence)

If any of these become false, we revisit North Star — not the day-to-day execution.

## Review Cadence

- Quarterly review by founder
- Annual review with one outside advisor
- Re-written only if a "what has to be true" assumption breaks
