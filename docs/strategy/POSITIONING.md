# Positioning

> What we are. What we're not. The sentence we want the buyer to say back.

## The Positioning Sentence

> "Dealix is the Saudi mid-market's productized revenue operations team — AI-assisted, founder-approved, evidence-backed."

## Category

**Category we play in:** Productized revenue operations (RevOps-as-a-service)

**Category we don't play in:**
- General AI consulting
- Enterprise software / SaaS subscription only
- Marketing agencies (we do not do creative/brand)
- Pure data engineering (we use data, we don't sell pipelines)

## Frame

We are not a SaaS. We are not a consultancy. We are a **productized operating service**:
- **Productized** like SaaS — fixed scope, fixed price, fixed delivery time
- **Operating** like a team — outcomes, not features
- **Service** like a consultancy — humans in the loop on every external action

## Who It's For

- **ICP:** Saudi mid-market companies, SAR 5M – 500M revenue, founder-run, 10–200 employees
- **Sectors first:** Logistics, B2B services, light manufacturing, professional services
- **Buyer:** Founder / CEO / Head of Sales
- **Job to be done:** "Show me where my next 5 customers are, what to send them, and what they're worth — without me hiring 3 people."

## Who It's NOT For

- Pre-revenue startups (we need a real pipeline to work with)
- Enterprises with formal procurement > 90 days (timeline doesn't fit Sprint)
- Founders who want "AI to take over" with no human oversight (we refuse)
- Marketing-budget buyers (we are not an agency)

## Promise

- A productized Revenue Sprint in 7 days
- Every claim backed by evidence
- Every external action approved by you
- A monthly retainer that compounds, or a clean exit with everything documented

## Anti-Promise

- We will not pretend to be fully autonomous AI
- We will not send messages on your behalf without approval
- We will not handle your contracts or compliance work
- We will not promise revenue figures (we promise process + visibility)

## Tone

- Operator > guru
- Evidence > opinion
- Specific > clever
- Arabic-first in market, English-first in code

## Comparison Frame

| Alternative | What they do | Why Dealix is different |
|---|---|---|
| Hire an SDR | Outsource calling | We give you a system, not a person to manage |
| Hire an agency | Pay for activity | We charge for productized outcomes |
| Buy a CRM | Buy a tool | We come with the work, not just the surface |
| DIY with ChatGPT | Free, unreliable | We come with trust gates + evidence packs |
| Hire a CFO/RevOps | SAR 30K+/month | We start at SAR 499 |

## What We Want The Buyer To Say

Three signals that positioning landed:

1. "Oh — so you're not really an agency."
2. "So I don't have to manage another vendor."
3. "What sectors have you done this in?"

If we hear those three questions, positioning is working.

## What Breaks Positioning

- Selling a feature instead of an outcome
- Discounting below the productized price (signals "we're a consultancy")
- Adding scope without changing price (signals "we'll do anything for the money")
- Publishing screenshots instead of evidence packs
- Promising automation without showing the approval gate
