# Data Retention Policy

> **Status:** CANONICAL · **Owner:** Founder · **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-05

---

## 1. Principle

```
Collect minimum.
Store intentionally.
Delete predictably.
Prove responsibly.
```

## 2. Data categories and retention

| Category | Example | Retention | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Engagement intake | Sprint intake data | Duration of engagement + 12 months | Deletable on request |
| Customer artifacts | Command Pack outputs | Duration of engagement + 12 months | Customer owns; deletable on request |
| Consent records | Consent capture at intake | 24 months min (audit) | Retained to prove lawful basis |
| Operational logs | Approval/audit logs | 12 months min | Required for audit trail |
| Marketing contact (warm) | Name, company, role | Until objection / 24 months inactivity | Manual, consented only |

These are baseline defaults; tighten per customer contract. Not legal advice —
confirm with counsel before enterprise commitments.

## 3. Deletion and export

- Customers may request deletion or a copy of their data (DSAR-style).
- Every deletion is **logged** (doctrine rule #9): who requested, what, when, by whom executed.
- Exports are an A4 action (approval + log).

## 4. Minimum-data rule (Command Sprint)

We collect only: company name, industry, contact role, current sales/delivery
flow, top 5 opportunities, current offer, follow-up examples, pain points,
approval contact, consent. Nothing more. Forbidden at intake: passwords, full CRM
dumps, API keys, large personal files, scraped data.

## 5. PDPL alignment

Retention, right-to-deletion, right-to-copy, and a complaints mechanism are all
documented (Data OS noted only 31% of surveyed Saudi e-commerce sites disclosed
all four — Dealix discloses all four). See `PRIVACY_AND_PDPL_READINESS.md`.

## 6. Review

Re-review retention windows on every release and before any enterprise contract.
