# AI Risk Register — سجل مخاطر الذكاء الاصطناعي

## Purpose
Maintain a NIST AI RMF-style register of identified AI risks at Dealix, with likelihood, impact, current mitigation, and residual risk per item.

## Owner
Founder.

## Inputs
- Agent inventory.
- Threat model from `docs/ai_management/AI_THREAT_MODEL.md`.
- Incident history.
- Audit findings.

## Outputs
- Risk register table (below).
- Quarterly review with risk-level changes documented.

## Rules (numbered)
1. Every identified risk has a row with likelihood, impact, mitigation, residual rating, owner.
2. Likelihood and impact use a 1 to 5 scale; risk score is the product.
3. Any risk with residual score above 12 requires escalation to the founder and a remediation plan within 14 days.
4. Risks are not closed; they are downgraded or retired with rationale.
5. New AI capabilities trigger a register review before they go live.
6. Register is reviewed quarterly.

## Metrics
- Risks with residual score above 12 (target 0).
- Mitigations implemented vs proposed.
- Mean time from risk identification to mitigation in place.

## Cadence
Quarterly review. Continuous additions.

## Evidence (paths)
- `docs/ai_management/AI_RISK_REGISTER.md` (this file).
- `docs/trust/registers/audits/` for the audit findings that update this register.

## Verifier
Founder.

## Runtime Command
`make ai.risk.review` opens the register and the recent incident log for the quarterly review session.

## Operating substance
The register is shaped after NIST AI RMF Map-Measure-Manage. The Map step is identifying every risk relevant to Dealix's AI surface. The Measure step is the likelihood-impact scoring. The Manage step is the mitigation and residual rating.

The single most important risk in the table is AIR-008 (overclaim by drafter agent), because it is high-likelihood and high-impact. Mitigation runs on three layers: scanner before output, safe-language library lookup, human review before ship. Residual rating is still 6 — non-zero — because no triple-layer defense is perfect. Continuous attention required.

The register is intentionally short. A long register loses signal. New risks are added when they are real, not when they are theoretical. We do not pad the register to look thorough.

Quarterly review re-rates every row. Likelihood and impact may have changed; mitigations may have weakened or strengthened. Re-rating in writing is the audit trail.
