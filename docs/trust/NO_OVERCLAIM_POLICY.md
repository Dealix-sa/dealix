# Dealix No-Overclaim Policy

## Purpose
Protect customers, partners, and Dealix itself from any external
communication that promises outcomes Dealix cannot honestly stand behind.

## Owner
Sami / Founder CEO.

## Scope
Applies to:
- proposals
- DMs, emails, and any outreach
- landing pages and marketing copy
- case studies
- decks and PDFs
- AI- or agent-generated content
- any artifact produced by the Revenue Sprint Kit

## Forbidden Claims
The following terms and any equivalent phrasing are forbidden in any
external Dealix communication:

- "guaranteed revenue"
- "guaranteed sales"
- "guaranteed meetings"
- "guaranteed replies"
- "fully compliant"
- "100% automated sales"
- "no-risk"
- "risk-free"
- "we will close deals for you"
- "we will replace your sales team"

## Allowed Framing
- "qualified opportunities"
- "opportunity intelligence"
- "outreach assets"
- "action plan"
- "support for the client's sales motion"
- explicit caveat: "Dealix does not guarantee sales, revenue, replies, or meetings."

## Evidence Rule
- No public claim about a client, sector, or result is published without
  written approval from the client and supporting evidence held in the
  private operating environment.
- Case studies require explicit written approval and a redaction pass
  against this policy and the QA checklist.

## Review Before Publication
Before any external use, every artifact produced by the Revenue Sprint
Kit (DM, sample pack, proposal, delivery report, case study) is reviewed
against this policy. AI- or agent-generated content is treated as
external content and reviewed before sending.

## Why
LLM outputs and outreach content shaped by external signals can produce
unsupported or sensitive claims. Reviewing every artifact before
publication is the simplest control against that risk and is consistent
with widely-published LLM risk guidance (e.g. OWASP LLM Top 10).
