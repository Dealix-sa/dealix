# No-Overclaim Policy

## Statement

Dealix does not claim — publicly or to a prospect — any outcome, capability,
certification, or guarantee that is not backed by evidence on file.

## Scope

Applies to:
- Website and landing pages.
- Proposals and SOWs.
- Sales DMs and email copy.
- Public talks and interviews.
- Social media (including the founder's personal accounts when speaking about Dealix).

## What We Avoid

- Absolute revenue guarantees ("we will add SAR X").
- Compliance certifications we do not hold.
- Model performance numbers not measured on our data.
- Generic AI marketing claims ("fully autonomous", "no human in the loop").
- Client names without written consent.

## What We Allow

- Outcomes measured on our own delivered work, with the client named only with consent.
- Ranges and expectations clearly labeled "based on our experience".
- Capability statements that map to a verifiable workflow in the OS.
- Comparisons grounded in public information.

## Enforcement

- Every public claim is reviewed before publication (A2 approval).
- Each claim references an evidence file in the proof library.
- Violations are logged as Trust incidents and reviewed in the Weekly CEO Review.

## Rule of Thumb

If a buyer cannot ask "show me the proof" and receive an artifact in 24 hours,
the claim is not allowed.
