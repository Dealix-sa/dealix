# Market Thesis

Why Dealix exists, in the language of a strategic outsider.

## The market
Saudi B2B is large, diversifying fast, and underserved by trusted revenue systems. Most operators run on improvised pipelines, manual outreach, and over-claiming vendors.

## The shift
Two shifts make Dealix possible now:
1. Saudi enterprises and mid-market increasingly value Saudi-native, locally trusted vendors.
2. AI-assisted preparation can compress research, scoring, and message drafting without crossing trust lines — if it is governed.

## The wedge
Dealix wins by combining:
- Saudi-native voice and sector depth.
- A trust-first posture (Claim Guard, suppression, approval matrix).
- A productized delivery system that compounds case studies.
- An offer ladder that converts proof to recurring revenue.

## The moat
- Recurring revenue.
- Case study library and sector reports.
- Operating system codified in `docs/`.
- Founder reputation and partner network.

## The risk
- Over-claim incidents.
- Sector misallocation.
- Founder bandwidth before the right hires.
- Macro changes in Saudi B2B spending.

## Rule
The thesis is short, honest, and reviewed quarterly. A thesis you cannot defend with evidence is a story.
