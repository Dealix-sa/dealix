# Market Thesis — أُطروحة السوق

## Purpose
Dealix's market thesis for investors and board. Concrete, locally grounded, evidence-backed. No "trillion-dollar TAM". No copy-paste analyst slides. The thesis must hold whether or not generative AI continues to attract capital.

## Owner
Founder. Reviewed quarterly.

## Inputs
- Sector reports from `docs/sector-reports/`.
- ICP interviews and observations.
- Saudi public data (GASTAT, MCIT, Tadawul disclosures, regulator publications).
- Competitive scan.

## Outputs
- This thesis.
- Public-safe summary referenced in `docs/investor/COMPANY_OVERVIEW.md`.

## The Thesis (Five Claims)

### Claim 1 — Saudi B2B is operationally young and trust-sensitive
- Most Saudi B2B operators do not have mature commercial operations.
- They cannot trust external vendors with their data without explicit governance.
- Implication: a trust-first, evidence-anchored operating partner is differentiated by default.

### Claim 2 — AI-supported services beat AI-as-a-product (in this market, today)
- Buyers are not paying for raw model access; they pay for outcomes.
- Service margins on AI-supported delivery can exceed 60% per engagement.
- Implication: building a product before earning service repeatability is premature.

### Claim 3 — Sector-specific authority compounds faster than generalist authority
- One published sector report a quarter, methodology-disclosed, beats five generic LinkedIn posts.
- Buyers in Saudi B2B reward category-specific operators.
- Implication: Dealix concentrates content and capability in named sectors.

### Claim 4 — Productization beats platform-first thinking
- The 3 → 5 → 10 ladder produces SaaS candidates that have already earned their revenue.
- Implication: Dealix's SaaS path is incremental and evidence-led, not capital-led.

### Claim 5 — PDPL + responsible AI are tailwinds, not headwinds
- Saudi PDPL and SDAIA AI ethics provide a compliance moat against careless competitors.
- Implication: trust-os investments compound, not cost.

## Rules
1. No market size claim without a sourced public figure and a methodology note.
2. No statement implying Dealix is the only operator in a category.
3. Every projection labelled "Estimated value is not Verified value / القيمة التقديرية ليست قيمة مُتحقَّقة".
4. Sources cited inline; no orphan numbers.
5. Quarterly review; claims that no longer hold are revised, not buried.

## Metrics
- Claim-by-claim evidence count.
- Sources freshness (≤ 12 months for public data).
- Revisions per claim per year (target small but non-zero).

## Cadence
