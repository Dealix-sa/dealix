> customer-facing templates — generating filler content would itself be the

## Why these are surfaced
"Exists" ≠ "done". A delivery checklist that says only `# TODO` is worse than
no file — it gives a false sense of completion that the master verifier must
break. `make production-certification` lists these as a warning; once the
count drops to zero, promote the warning to a blocking failure in
`.github/workflows/dealix-production-certification.yml` and in the
`production-certification:` Makefile target.

## How to clear an entry
1. Fill the file with REAL content (owner, cadence, inputs, outputs, KPI where it makes sense — see the template in `readiness/production_readiness_gates.md`).
2. Re-run `python scripts/verify_non_empty_files.py`.
3. When the count reaches zero, promote the audit from warning to blocking:
   ```yaml
   # in .github/workflows/dealix-production-certification.yml
   - name: Placeholder-files audit (BLOCKING)
     run: python scripts/verify_non_empty_files.py
   ```
   and in the Makefile:
   ```make
   production-certification: everything
       $(PYTHON) scripts/verify_non_empty_files.py   # remove the `|| echo WARN`
   ```

## What the audit refuses to do
- Auto-fill `policy_template.md` files. Policy templates that lie to customers are worse than missing.
- Generate ROI estimates without inputs.
- Fabricate certification-program content the founder hasn't decided on.
- Write delivery checklists for sprints whose actual scope isn't yet committed.

Honesty here is what makes the gate trustworthy.
