# Content Strategy

> The founder's content is a proof artifact, not a content factory.

## Content Pillars

1. **Saudi B2B revenue systems** — what works locally, with evidence.
2. **Founder building in public** — what we learned this week.
3. **Trust-first AI operations** — governance over hype.
4. **Revenue Sprint lessons** — patterns across multiple sprints (with consent).
5. **Approved case studies** — named customers with named outcomes.
6. **B2B sales intelligence** — the craft, not the tooling.

## What we will not post

- Hot takes on others' work.
- Unverified "industry trends".
- "AI eats X" prophecy posts.
- Vague aspirational quotes.
- Posts about ourselves with no proof.
- Cross-posts of others' content without commentary.

## Cadence

- LinkedIn: 3–5 posts / week (text-led).
- X: 7–14 posts / week (short, observation-led).
- Long-form (newsletter / blog): 1 per fortnight.

## The Pre-Post Check (every post)

- [ ] Is there an evidence link or a verifiable claim?
- [ ] Does it pass `NO_OVERCLAIM_POLICY.md`?
- [ ] Does it name a customer? If yes — is written consent on file?
- [ ] Does it contradict the autonomy or trust policy?
- [ ] If asked for the source by a stranger, can I deliver it in 30 seconds?

If any "no": don't post.

## Content → Pipeline (not the other way)

Content is **downstream** of work, not upstream:

- We do the work.
- We capture what we learned.
- We post what we can defend.
- Replies from ICP enter the pipeline through the normal qualification
  flow.

We do **not** invent content to "build an audience" first.

## Anti-Patterns

- "Influencer" posting style (we are not influencers).
- Engagement-bait questions ("agree?").
- Numbered list posts with no specific examples.
- Recycling other founders' frameworks without attribution.

## Review

Quarterly. Cull pillars that have not produced inbound, refine those
that have.
