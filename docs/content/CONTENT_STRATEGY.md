# Content Strategy

> What we publish, why, and what we refuse.

## Goal

Build **founder authority** through evidence-backed operator insight, drawing inbound from Tier 1 ICP.

## Posture

- Operator > guru
- Specific > clever
- Evidence > opinion
- Bilingual (Arabic primary in market; English for shareability)
- Anti-hype

## Cadence

| Channel | Volume | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn (founder) | 1 post/week + 3 considered comments/week | Founder |
| X (Twitter) | Optional repost of LinkedIn (English) | Founder |
| Sector reports | 1/quarter | Founder + advisor |
| Case studies | 1/quarter (post first closed Sprint) | Founder + Trust |

## Content Pillars

1. **Process** — how the productized Sprint actually runs (transparency = trust)
2. **Trust** — approval matrix, claim guard, public safety (the moat publicly)
3. **Sector** — what we learn from each shipped sprint (per `SECTOR_PLAYBOOKS.md`)
4. **Founder OS** — eating the cooking (Daily Brief, Weekly Review, decisions)
5. **Anti-pattern** — what we refuse and why

## What We Don't Publish

- Vendor / model comparisons (we don't fight commodity battles)
- Hot takes on industry news (low-signal)
- AI hype / fear cycles
- Anything that makes us look smarter than the customer
- Pre-release feature teases (we announce when shipped + paying customer)
- Anything that doesn't pass `claim_guard.py`

## Approval Tier (per `APPROVAL_MATRIX.md`)

- LinkedIn post: A3 (founder + evidence pack if making a claim)
- X repost: A2 (founder)
- Sector report: A3 (founder + advisor)
- Case study: A3 (founder + client consent + advisor for first case study)

## Distribution

- LinkedIn: founder personal account only (no company page push this quarter)
- X: cross-post English version
- Newsletter (when launched): max monthly, never weekly (no spam)
- Direct: include relevant post link in proposal follow-ups

## Engagement Discipline

- Reply to substantive comments within 24 hr
- Don't reply to flame / troll comments
- Don't argue with competitors publicly
- Don't engage with prompts to compare ourselves to others
- Don't reply to "you should X" outside our focus (warm thanks, move on)

## Content → Pipeline Tracking

- Every content piece tagged with content_id in `learning/`
- Inbound leads tagged with content_id of first interaction
- Monthly: which content brings which leads?
- Quarterly: kill low-signal content formats

## Saudi-Specific Notes

- Arabic posts perform better in Saudi market (use AR-first, EN-second)
- Avoid Friday posting (low engagement, work week constraint)
- Public holiday calendar matters
- Religious / cultural sensitivity (avoid commercializing)

## What This Strategy Refuses

- Content for SEO sake
- Content that requires "going viral" to succeed
- Lifestyle / personal-brand content unrelated to the work
- Stock images / generic AI graphics
- Recycled content from training data
