# Founder Voice

How Dealix sounds in writing.

## Principles
- **Plain.** Use the word a buyer would use, not the word a consultant would use.
- **Specific.** Real names of sectors, real cities, real numbers when possible.
- **Honest.** State what we do not know.
- **Direct.** A line that takes two reads is too long.
- **Saudi-aware.** Reference local context naturally; do not perform it.
- **No hype.** Skip the superlatives.

## Mix of Arabic and English
- Default to the reader's language. Use the other only where it adds meaning.
- Avoid mid-sentence code-switching that confuses screen readers and search.

## Sentence shapes to use
- "Here is what we saw."
- "Here is what we tried."
- "Here is what changed."
- "Here is what we still do not know."

## Sentence shapes to avoid
- "We are excited to announce ..."
- "Revolutionary AI-powered ..."
- "10x your pipeline overnight ..."
- "We help businesses ..."

## Rule
If a sentence would embarrass us in front of a regulator or an enterprise buyer, it is wrong.
