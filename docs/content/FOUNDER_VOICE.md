# Founder Voice

> How the founder writes and speaks publicly.
> Tone consistency = brand trust.

## Default Tone

- **Operator > guru.** "I run this on us; here's what happened" not "the future of AI is..."
- **Specific > clever.** "We delivered the Sprint in 7 days" not "we transformed the customer's journey."
- **Honest > polished.** Admit what we don't know. Mark guesses as guesses.
- **Short > long.** Make the point. Stop.
- **Local > global.** Saudi context first; international framing only when relevant.
- **Calm > urgent.** No hype, no "limited time", no exclamation points.

## Forbidden Voice Patterns

- Guru-speak ("here's the secret nobody is talking about")
- Generic optimism ("AI is going to change everything")
- Industry buzzwords ("synergy", "transformation", "10x")
- Fake humility ("just a small founder here")
- Fake confidence ("I knew all along")
- Manufactured contrarianism ("everyone is wrong about X")
- Performative vulnerability ("here's why I cried last week")
- Pop psychology framing ("the 5 traits of...")

## Allowed Patterns

- "Here's what happened this week."
- "We tried X and it didn't work. Here's why we think."
- "I was wrong about Y. Here's what we changed."
- "Three things our Sprint customers asked for. We built one."
- "The trust gate caught X this month. Process saved us."
- "Refusing this kind of work matters because..."

## Language Posture

- **Arabic primary** in Saudi-market posts (LinkedIn, sector reports)
- **English secondary** for shareability and GCC reach
- Code-switching OK when authentic to the moment
- Translation discipline: never auto-translate sensitive content; human-review every Arabic↔English handoff

## Personal vs. Professional

- The founder is public-facing as a person
- Dealix is the business
- The founder posts about Dealix, the work, the operating model
- The founder does **not** post personal life content (family, religion, politics, lifestyle)
- The founder does **not** use Dealix to amplify personal opinions outside the work
- The founder does **not** mix business + personal in voice

## Vocabulary Defaults

Use:
- "We" (Dealix collective, even when founder-only) for the work
- "I" (founder personal) for opinions / decisions / mistakes
- "Customer" or "client" (interchangeable; we use both)
- "Sprint" (capitalized, proper noun) for our specific offer
- "Founder-led" for our motion this quarter

Don't use:
- "Users" (we're not a SaaS)
- "Stakeholders" (corporate-speak)
- "Leverage" as a verb (consultancy-speak)
- "Drive" as a verb for non-vehicles
- "Synergize", "evangelize", "ideate"

## Reply Voice

- Acknowledge the comment specifically
- Add a fact or a question
- Don't argue
- Don't justify the work to outsiders who aren't ICP
- Move on when appropriate

## Apology Voice (when something goes wrong)

- Direct ("we broke X")
- Specific ("at YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM")
- Owned ("I approved this — my call")
- Actionable ("here's what we changed so it won't happen again")
- Final ("thanks for surfacing this")

No hedging. No excuses. No marketing language in an apology.

## Refusal Voice (when saying no)

- Clear ("we don't do that")
- Friendly ("but here's what fits...")
- Final (no negotiation hooks)
- Referenced (link to OFFER.md or principle)

## Off-Limits Topics

- Saudi politics
- Religion
- Family / personal life
- Other founders' choices
- Industry drama
- Specific competitor names (except in private sales)
- AI ethics debates beyond what we publish in `AI_GOVERNANCE.md`

## Voice Calibration

- Quarterly: read 5 random posts aloud — do they sound like you?
- Anytime: if a post feels off-tone, hold and rewrite
- After any public misstep: log and adjust this doc

## What This Voice Refuses

- Performing for an audience the founder doesn't respect
- Voice drift toward whatever's currently trending
- Becoming an "AI thought leader" in voice (we're operators)
- Borrowing other founders' framings
- Sounding like ChatGPT (em-dashes everywhere, "delve into", "navigate the landscape")
