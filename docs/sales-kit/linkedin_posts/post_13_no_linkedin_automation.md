## English outline

At Dealix, doctrine #11 is explicit: "no_linkedin_automation". No
auto-connect, no auto-DM, no auto-comment, no scraping. Even for
post scheduling, we use LinkedIn's native UI only.

**Why:**

1. **Terms of Service:** LinkedIn explicitly warns against
   third-party automation. Account termination = years of network
   loss.
2. **Brand damage:** recipients spot auto-DM in seconds.
   Impression: "spammer."
3. **Network depreciation:** carefully built network is a strategic
   asset. Automation depletes it fast.
4. **Counter-effect:** those who succeed at LinkedIn in Saudi B2B
   = those who build real relationships with other founders.
   Automation is the opposite of that.

**What we do instead:**

- 3 manual posts/week on a specific cadence.
- One DM/week to a warm intro. Each DM starts with "{specific
  reason for connecting}".
- No automated comments on others' posts.
- No auto-connect requests.

**Results after 6 months (publishable):**
- Network grew from X to Y (founder fills in).
- Inbound DMs from real founders: Z (founder fills in).
- Zero account warnings.

**The rule:** network quality > network size. Every automation
trades size for quality.
