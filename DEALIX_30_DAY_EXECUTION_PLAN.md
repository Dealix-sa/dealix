# Dealix 30-Day Execution Plan

## Purpose
Turn Dealix from system setup into market execution and first revenue.

## Owner
Sami / Founder CEO.

## Review Cadence
Daily during first 30 days.

## North Star
First paid Revenue Sprint, then first delivery proof, then first retainer attempt.

## Week 1 — First Market Push
Targets:
- 25 leads.
- 25 founder-led DMs.
- 3 samples.
- 1 proposal.
- 1 payment / PO / written approval attempt.
- 1 weekly learning decision.

Focus:
- manual execution.
- no SaaS expansion.
- no new complex agents.

## Week 2 — Repeat and Improve
Targets:
- another 25 leads.
- another 25 DMs.
- refine best message.
- 3 more samples.
- 1–2 proposals.
- first paid sprint or stronger pipeline.

Focus:
- improve message based on replies.
- update ICP.
- improve proposal and payment follow-up.

## Week 3 — Delivery Proof
Targets:
- deliver first paid or approved sprint.
- complete QA checklist.
- request feedback.
- capture proof.
- identify retainer angle.

Focus:
- delivery quality.
- trust-safe claims.
- client success.

## Week 4 — Retainer Attempt and Productization Signals
Targets:
- retainer proposal or next sprint.
- 1 case study draft if approved.
- identify repeated workflows.
- update productization candidates.
- monthly strategy review.

Focus:
- retain.
- learn.
- productize only what repeated.

## Daily Operating Rules
- run daily CLI.
- update pipeline.
- complete one revenue action.
- review approvals.
- close day with daily gate.

## Weekly Operating Rules
- run weekly automation.
- generate weekly review.
- update one playbook.
- commit system improvement.

## Not Allowed During First 30 Days
- SaaS buildout.
- mobile app.
- enterprise dashboard.
- white-label.
- investor deck.
- complex autonomous agents.
- hiring before repeated workflow.

## Metrics
- DMs sent.
- reply rate.
- call rate.
- proposal rate.
- payment rate.
- delivery QA pass.
- learning decisions.
- playbook updates.

## Evidence
- pipeline tracker.
- revenue action log.
- proposal notes.
- delivery report.
- weekly reviews.
- Git commits.
