# Data Processing Addendum (DPA) — Outline

**Operational scaffold. Not legal advice.** Retain qualified counsel.

## 1. Roles
- Customer: data controller.
- Provider (Dealix): data processor.

## 2. Subject
Personal data processed by Provider in the course of delivering services under any SOW.

## 3. Categories of data
- Business contact data (names, emails, phone numbers of decision-makers).
- Operational metadata (lead status, response time, deal stage).
- (Optional) WhatsApp transcripts and customer support messages if Customer opts in.

## 4. Purpose
Solely to provide the contracted services. No secondary use.

## 5. Sub-processors
List maintained by Provider and shared on request. Includes PaaS hosting and optional LLM providers (only if Customer opts in to LLM-assist).

## 6. Sub-processor changes
Provider gives Customer 30 days written notice before adding a new sub-processor. Customer may object on reasonable grounds.

## 7. Security measures
- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+).
- Encryption at rest via PaaS provider's managed encryption.
- MFA on all admin access.
- Privileged audit logging.
- Quarterly access review.

## 8. International transfers
- Default data residency: KSA.
- Cross-border transfer requires explicit Customer consent and a compliant transfer mechanism.

## 9. Data subject rights
Customer is responsible for handling data subject requests. Provider will assist within commercially reasonable effort.

## 10. Personal data breach
Provider notifies Customer within 72 hours of confirmed incident.

## 11. Deletion / return
On termination, Provider returns or deletes personal data within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.

## 12. Audits
Customer may audit Provider's controls once per year with 30 days notice. Provider responds with a security questionnaire as a first step.

## 13. Liability
As stated in the MSA.

## 14. Governing law
Saudi Arabia.
