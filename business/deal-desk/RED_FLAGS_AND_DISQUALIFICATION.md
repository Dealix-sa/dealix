# Red Flags & Disqualification

## Hard disqualifiers (walk away)
- Customer demands outcome-contingent pricing ("pay only if X happens").
- Customer wants Dealix to send cold WhatsApp / mass email at scale.
- Customer wants Dealix to bypass platform terms of service (scraping, fake reviews, fake clicks).
- Customer wants Dealix to manage their financial / legal decisions autonomously.
- Customer cannot name a single decision-maker.
- Customer has hostile internal politics that block weekly reviews.
- Customer is unwilling to sign an MSA + DPA.

## Yellow flags (proceed with caution + extra deal desk review)
- Customer has churned 3+ vendors in the past 18 months.
- Customer scope keeps mutating across calls.
- Customer wants 60-day payment terms on a < 50,000 SAR deal.
- Customer wants Dealix to use their existing credentials for third-party tools.
- Customer wants a "guarantee".

## How to walk away professionally
1. Restate what Dealix can and cannot do.
2. Offer the diagnostic sprint as a small test.
3. If they decline, refer them to a partner agency that fits their need.
4. Send the disqualifier note from `business/sales/disqualification_template.md`.

## Saying no protects every other customer
A customer who pushes for unsafe practices will eventually damage the brand. Walking away is a permanent win.
