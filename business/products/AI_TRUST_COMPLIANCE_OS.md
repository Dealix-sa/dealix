# AI Trust & Compliance OS

## Positioning

Dealix builds a trust and compliance layer for Saudi companies using AI — ensuring PDPL compliance, data governance, consent management, audit trails, and responsible AI usage policies so the company can adopt AI without legal exposure or trust erosion.

## Problem

Saudi companies want to use AI but face three risks: PDPL non-compliance (personal data processed without legal basis), lack of governance (no audit trail for AI decisions or data access), and trust erosion (employees and customers do not trust AI because there are no policies or transparency). Companies either avoid AI from fear or adopt it recklessly. Both are losing positions.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi company adopting or planning to adopt AI tools (internal or customer-facing)
- Company handling personal data of Saudi residents (customers, employees, leads)
- Founder/management concerned about PDPL compliance and legal exposure
- Company with 10+ employees where AI usage needs governance
- Industry: clinics (patient data), real estate (client data), legal/accounting (client confidentiality), training (student data), logistics (customer data)

## Objection Handling

**Objection 1: "We are too small to worry about compliance."**
Response: PDPL applies to any entity processing personal data of Saudi residents, regardless of size. The risk is not the regulator knocking today — it is the customer who asks where their data is, the employee who feeds client data into ChatGPT, or the vendor who leaks your data. Compliance at small scale is simpler and cheaper than compliance after an incident.

**Objection 2: "We have a privacy policy, that should be enough."**
Response: A privacy policy is a document, not a system. The question is whether your actual practices match the policy, whether consent is recorded, whether AI decisions are logged, and whether you can respond to a data subject request. We close the gap between what the policy says and what actually happens.

**Objection 3: "This will slow down our AI adoption."**
Response: The opposite. Without a compliance layer, you either adopt AI recklessly (and risk exposure) or avoid it from fear. With a compliance layer, you can adopt AI confidently and faster because you know what is allowed, what is not, and what to do if something goes wrong. Compliance enables speed, not slows it.

## Risks and Limitations

- We are not a law firm and do not provide legal advice; formal legal review may be required for regulated industries
- PDPL regulations continue to evolve; compliance status may change as regulations update
- Compliance is an ongoing process, not a one-time certification
- Employee adherence to policies cannot be guaranteed by the system alone; culture and enforcement matter
- Vendor AI practices are partially outside our control; we assess but cannot audit internal vendor systems
- Incident response plans require testing; untested plans may fail under real conditions
