# Service Ladder (EN)

## Product Definition

The Service Ladder is Dealix's tiered product guide for Saudi B2B companies. It starts with a free or low-cost diagnostic, ascends to a short sprint, then to a full AI Operating System build, and finally to a monthly partnership. The design intent is that a client starts with a small step, trusts the value, and then commits to a larger step only after proof.

## Problem

Clients do not buy a full AI Operating System on the first meeting. They need a small, low-risk step to experience value before they commit. Without a clear service ladder, the client gets lost, stalls, or leaves for a competitor that offers an easier entry point.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi B2B companies that need a low-risk entry point
- Founders who want to trial before committing to a large contract
- Companies that need a diagnostic before investing in AI
- Teams that prefer to scale from small to large in stages

## Who Should Not Buy It

- Companies that want a full system on day one with no trial
- Teams that do not need a diagnostic or a short sprint
- Companies that cannot benefit from a low-cost entry point
- Buyers who have already committed to a full operating system elsewhere

## Business Outcome

A clear service ladder lets the client start small, build trust, and ascend naturally to larger engagements, with high retention and smooth transitions between stages.

## Data Required

- Current product and service list
- Pricing and delivery scope for each service
- Stage-to-stage conversion rates
- Reasons for non-conversion (if any)
- Past client feedback per stage

## Workflow

1. Document each service at a clear level
2. Order services from smallest to largest
3. Define transition criteria between stages
4. Prepare transition messages for each stage
5. Train the team on the service ladder
6. Monthly review of conversion rates
7. Improve retention points

## First Sales Message EN

Hello,

We have a clear service ladder. You start with a quick diagnostic, then a 7-day sprint, then a full operating system build, then a monthly partnership. Each step is small and proves value before you commit to more. We do not want you to pay big upfront. Can you spare 15 minutes so I can show you how the ladder works?

## Discovery Questions

- How do you evaluate new services before committing?
- Do you prefer a small trial before a large contract?
- What is your biggest concern about committing on day one?
- How do you decide to move from one service to another?
- What makes you stop at a particular stage?
- How do you measure success at each stage?

## Objection Handling

- "I do not want to commit to a large contract." — That is why we have a ladder. You start small and ascend naturally.
- "The diagnostic is enough." — The diagnostic shows you where the opportunity is. The sprint starts capturing it. Without execution, the diagnostic is wasted.
- "I do not have time for a sprint." — The sprint is only 7 days and we do most of the work. You approve and observe.
- "Prices are too high." — Each stage is low-risk. You can stop at any time. There is no long lock-in.

## Implementation SOP

1. Sign NDA and confirm scope
2. Document all services and pricing
3. Order the service ladder
4. Define transition criteria
5. Write transition messages
6. Train the team on the ladder
7. Monthly review of conversion rates
8. Deliver proof pack and acceptance sign-off
9. Offer the monthly partnership

## What Not To Promise

- Do not guarantee the client will enter every stage
- Do not guarantee specific conversion rates
- Do not use fake testimonials or fabricated case studies
- Do not guarantee ROI percentages
- Do not promise the client never needs to commit
- Do not promise guaranteed outcomes without execution
