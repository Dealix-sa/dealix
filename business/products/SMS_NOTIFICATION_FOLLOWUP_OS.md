# SMS Notification/Follow-up OS

## Positioning

Dealix builds a consent-based SMS notification and follow-up system for Saudi B2B companies — with explicit opt-in tracking, STOP/opt-out compliance, rate limiting, and human-approved message drafting. Every SMS is logged, every consent is recorded, every opt-out is processed immediately.

## Problem

SMS in Saudi B2B is used badly or not at all. Companies send promotional SMS without consent, without opt-out, and without tracking. Recipients are annoyed, complaints are filed, and the company's sender reputation degrades. Alternatively, companies avoid SMS entirely because they fear compliance issues, missing a high-open-rate channel. There is no middle ground between spam and silence.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi B2B company with a customer/lead base that has given or can give explicit consent
- Company wanting SMS for appointments, reminders, follow-ups, and notifications (not bulk marketing)
- Founder who wants compliant SMS without legal risk
- Industry: clinics (appointment reminders), real estate (viewing reminders), training (course reminders), logistics (delivery notifications), car dealers (service reminders), legal/accounting (deadline reminders)

## Objection Handling

**Objection 1: "SMS is outdated, everyone uses WhatsApp."**
Response: WhatsApp is dominant, but SMS has near-universal delivery and does not require the recipient to have the app or a data connection. For appointment reminders, delivery notifications, and time-critical alerts, SMS often outperforms WhatsApp. They work best together, not in competition.

**Objection 2: "We do not have consent records, can we still use this?"**
Response: You can, but we start by building the consent collection workflow first. We do not send SMS to contacts without recorded consent. This may mean re-confirming with your existing base before sending. It takes longer but protects you legally and builds trust.

**Objection 3: "SMS gateway costs are too high."**
Response: Gateway costs are per-message and passed through at cost — we do not mark them up. The question is whether each SMS earns its cost. For reminders that reduce no-shows or notifications that reduce follow-up calls, the ROI is usually clear. We start with a small batch to test before scaling.

## Risks and Limitations

- SMS without verified consent creates legal and reputational risk; we will not send without it
- SMS gateway per-message costs can accumulate; budget must be agreed upfront
- STOP/opt-out processing depends on gateway keyword support; some gateways have limitations
- Promotional/bulk SMS is high-risk and not included without verified consent infrastructure
- Delivery rates vary by gateway and network; 100% delivery is never guaranteed
- PDPL compliance must be verified for any personal data (phone numbers) in the system
