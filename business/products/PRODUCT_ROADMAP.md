# Product Roadmap

## Product Definition

The Product Roadmap is Dealix's phased plan for the AI Operating System product catalog. It defines what is available now, what is being built next, and what is planned for later, with timelines, dependencies, and the exit criteria for each phase. The roadmap keeps the catalog coherent: each new product connects to the service ladder, the delivery SOP, and the retainer path.

## Problem

Without a roadmap, the product catalog grows incoherently. New products appear ad hoc, dependencies are missed, and clients cannot tell what is available now versus promised later. The team builds without a spine, and buyers cannot plan their own adoption path.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi B2B companies planning multi-stage AI adoption
- Founders who need to know what is available now and what is next
- Internal Dealix team members aligning build and delivery
- Partners and integrators who need to see the catalog direction

## Who Should Not Buy It

- Companies that only want a single one-off engagement
- Buyers who need a fixed, contractually guaranteed roadmap with no change
- Teams that cannot plan beyond a single sprint
- Companies expecting every planned product to be available immediately

## Business Outcome

A clear, phased roadmap that lets clients plan adoption, lets the team build coherently, and keeps every new product tied to the service ladder, delivery SOP, and retainer path.

## Data Required

- Current product catalog and status per product
- Client demand signals and recurring requests
- Delivery capacity and team availability
- Dependency map between products
- Conversion and retention data per existing product
- Market and competitor movement in Saudi B2B AI

## Workflow

1. Inventory the current catalog and mark each product's status
2. Group products into phases based on dependency and demand
3. Assign timelines and exit criteria to each phase
4. Map each new product to the service ladder and retainer path
5. Review the roadmap monthly with the delivery team
6. Publish the roadmap to sales and clients
7. Adjust quarterly based on demand and delivery data

## SAR Pricing Range

The roadmap itself is an internal Dealix product. Products on the roadmap follow the standard pricing ladder:

- AI Revenue Diagnostic: SAR 0-1,500
- Founder Diagnostic: SAR 1,500-3,000
- 7-Day Revenue Command Room Sprint: SAR 5,000-12,000
- 14-Day Company Brain Sprint: SAR 15,000-35,000
- 30-Day AI Operating System Build: SAR 25,000-90,000
- Monthly AI Operations Partner Retainer: SAR 3,000-25,000/month
- Enterprise custom: quote-based

## Retainer Path

The roadmap is designed so every new product eventually connects to the Monthly AI Operations Partner Retainer (SAR 3,000-25,000/month). New products are not standalone; they feed the retainer once live.

## Upsell Path

- Each phase introduces products that upsell into the next phase
- Phase 1 products upsell into Phase 2 sprints
- Phase 2 sprints upsell into the 30-Day AI Operating System Build
- Any build upsells into the retainer and Enterprise custom

## Risks

- Timeline slippage due to delivery capacity constraints
- Demand signals shift and a planned product loses priority
- Dependencies block a phase from completing
- Roadmap published too aggressively and creates unmet expectations
- Over-building without conversion data

## Acceptance Criteria

- Roadmap documented with phases and timelines
- Exit criteria defined per phase
- Dependency map complete
- Monthly roadmap health report running
- Founder sign-off on proof pack

## Objection Handling

- "Roadmaps always slip." — Ours has exit criteria per phase and a monthly health report. We only commit a phase when its exit criteria are met.
- "We only need one product." — You can buy a single product. The roadmap just shows what else is available when you are ready.
- "We need everything now." — Phase 1 products are available now. Later phases are built on demand and proven delivery, not promises.
- "How do we know the roadmap is real?" — Each phase is tied to the service ladder and the delivery SOP. We do not list products we cannot deliver.

## Implementation SOP

1. Sign NDA and confirm scope
2. Inventory the current catalog and status
3. Group products into phases with timelines
4. Define exit criteria per phase
5. Map dependencies between products
6. Publish the roadmap to sales and clients
7. Run the monthly roadmap health review
8. Quarterly adjustment based on demand and delivery data
9. Deliver proof pack and acceptance sign-off

## What Not To Promise

- Do not promise a fixed, unchangeable roadmap
- Do not guarantee every planned product will ship on the listed date
- Do not promise guaranteed revenue or ROI from any roadmap product
- Do not use fake testimonials or fabricated case studies for planned products
- Do not promise a product is available when it is still in a future phase
- Do not promise the roadmap without the delivery SOP and service ladder backing it

## Timeline Guidance

- Phase 1: live now
- Phase 2: rolling delivery, each product available as it passes its Delivery SOP exit criteria
- Phase 3: following Phase 2 completion, driven by governance demand
- Phase 4: driven by Enterprise client demand and multi-entity scope

Timelines are reviewed monthly and adjusted quarterly. No date is committed until the phase's exit criteria are met and the product passes the Delivery SOP.
