# Controlled Live Outbound OS

## Positioning

Dealix can operate email and WhatsApp outbound for companies only under approval, verification, opt-out, rate-limit, and logging gates. We automate the operating system, not spam. Every send is approved, every target is verified, every opt-out is processed, every action is logged.

## Problem

Outbound is the most powerful and most dangerous sales channel. Done badly, it destroys sender reputation, triggers spam complaints, violates PDPL, and damages the brand. Most Saudi companies either avoid outbound entirely (leaving growth on the table) or do it recklessly (sending untargeted messages from personal accounts with no compliance). There is no controlled, professional, compliant outbound infrastructure designed for Saudi B2B.

## Objection Handling

**Objection 1: "We want fully automated outbound, not human approval for every message."**
Response: Fully automated outbound is how domains get blacklisted and companies get reported. We start with human approval for every message. As trust builds and patterns emerge, we can move to template-based pre-approval where you approve templates once and the system sends within those bounds. But we do not do blind automated sending — that is spam, not outbound.

**Objection 2: "25 emails and 10 WhatsApp messages a day is too slow for our growth targets."**
Response: 35 controlled, compliant, verified messages per day is 700+ per month. If your reply rate is even 5%, that is 35+ conversations per month from outbound alone. Speed without deliverability is zero. We start low to protect your domain and WhatsApp reputation, and scale as reputation proves. Impatience is the enemy of outbound.

**Objection 3: "We do not have a prospect list."**
Response: We can help you build one, but it takes time and must be done with verified sources. We do not work with scraped or purchased lists without verification. A good outbound system starts with a good list. If you cannot tell us where each prospect came from and why you are reaching out, we start with list building before sending.

## Delivery SOP

1. Intake call (60 min): audit outbound readiness, review prospect list, approve templates
2. Diagnosis (Day 1–2): DNS audit, list quality check, compliance assessment, channel readiness
3. Blueprint (Day 2–3): sending gates, template library, cadence, approval workflow — client approval
4. Build (Day 3–7): authentication setup, prospect queue, drafting engine, approval workflow, suppression list, logging
5. QA (Day 7): test all gates (approval, verification, opt-out, rate limit, logging), test sends
6. UAT (Day 7–8): client reviews first batch of drafts, approves, tests workflow
7. Launch (Day 8): first controlled batch sent (25 email / 10 WhatsApp max)
8. Training (Day 8–9): sales team trained on approval workflow, reply handling, compliance
9. Proof (Day 14 and Day 30): outbound report, reply rates, compliance audit, reputation status
10. Handoff (Day 30): outbound playbook, retainer decision

## Risks and Limitations

- Email domain reputation may already be damaged; recovery is not guaranteed
- WhatsApp Business API has rate limits and policy constraints outside our control
- Outbound reply rates are inherently low; this is a quality and volume game, not a magic bullet
- If the client bypasses approval gates, the compliance and reputation protections are voided
- Prospect list quality is the primary determinant of results; bad lists produce bad outcomes
- PDPL compliance for outbound requires verified consent or legitimate interest basis per use case
