# Company Brain OS

## Product Definition

A centralized AI knowledge brain for a Saudi B2B company that captures policies, SOPs, product info, pricing, past decisions, client history, and tribal knowledge in one searchable, governed system. The Company Brain lets any employee ask a question and get a trusted, source-backed answer instead of guessing or waiting for the founder.

## Problem

Company knowledge lives in the founder's head, scattered WhatsApp groups, random Google Docs, and personal notes. New employees ask the same questions repeatedly, decisions are lost, and the founder becomes a bottleneck for every operational question.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi B2B companies with 10–100 employees
- Founders overwhelmed by repeated operational questions
- Companies with high onboarding friction for new hires
- Teams that have documented some SOPs but no single source of truth
- Organizations that want to preserve decisions and context

## Who Should Not Buy It

- Companies with under 5 employees where the founder can answer everything directly
- Teams that already have a mature, adopted knowledge base with full governance
- Companies that cannot or will not document any internal processes
- Organizations unwilling to assign a human reviewer for AI answers

## Business Outcome

A single governed knowledge brain where employees get trusted, source-backed answers to operational questions, reducing founder bottleneck time and onboarding friction.

## Data Required

- Existing SOPs, policies, and process documents
- Product and pricing documentation
- Past key decisions and meeting notes
- Client history and account context
- Org chart and role definitions
- FAQs and repeated employee questions
- Any existing knowledge base or wiki content

## Workflow

1. Intake: collect all existing documents, SOPs, policies, and notes
2. Structure and tag knowledge into a governed brain
3. Build source-backed Q&A interface
4. Configure access permissions by role
5. Set up human review for uncertain answers
6. Run onboarding and adoption cycle
7. Weekly knowledge gap review

## AI Role

- Index and structure company knowledge
- Answer operational questions with source citations
- Detect knowledge gaps and flag missing documentation
- Summarize long documents into digestible answers
- Suggest updates when policies or pricing change

## Human Approval Role

- Review and approve AI answers before they become canonical
- Approve new knowledge entries and document updates
- Decide access permissions by role
- Resolve flagged knowledge gaps
- Sign off on onboarding content

## Objection Handling

- "We already have Google Drive / Notion." — Storage is not a brain. We add AI Q&A with source citations, access control, and gap detection so knowledge is actually used and trusted.
- "Our team is too small." — If onboarding or repeated questions are a pain, the brain helps. If you are under 5 people with zero documentation friction, start with the Founder Diagnostic first.
- "AI might give wrong answers." — Every uncertain answer is flagged for human review, and all answers cite their source so employees can verify.
- "Our knowledge is too sensitive." — We configure role-based access and work under NDA. The brain can run in your environment.
