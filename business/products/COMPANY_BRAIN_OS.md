# Company Brain OS

## Positioning

Dealix builds a private AI knowledge brain for a Saudi company — trained on its own documents, policies, offers, history, and operations — so any team member can ask a question and get an answer grounded in company truth, not generic AI hallucination.

## Problem

Company knowledge lives in scattered files, WhatsApp groups, founder's head, and outdated documents. New employees ask the same questions repeatedly. Sales reps do not know the latest pricing or offer details. Customer support gives inconsistent answers. The founder becomes a bottleneck because only they know everything. Generic AI tools like ChatGPT do not know the company's specifics and can hallucinate confidently.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi company with 10–100 employees
- Knowledge-intensive operations (training, legal, accounting, clinics, logistics)
- Founder/management tired of answering the same questions
- Multiple departments needing consistent information
- Existing documents (policies, SOPs, offers, FAQs) but no central brain
- Wants AI assistance grounded in their own data, not public internet

## Inputs Required

- Company documents (policies, SOPs, offer sheets, contracts templates, FAQs)
- Product/service catalog
- Pricing sheets and discount rules
- Historical project/case summaries (non-confidential, approved for use)
- Org chart and role definitions
- Brand voice and tone guidelines
- 60-minute intake call with operations lead
- Approval on which documents are in-scope vs confidential/excluded

## 7-Day Deliverable

- Document ingestion pipeline (approved documents only)
- Private knowledge base (vector store, company-isolated)
- Q&A interface (web chat, internal only)
- Access control (role-based: sales, ops, management, founder)
- Citation display (every answer shows source document)
- Human review workflow for flagged answers

## 14-Day Deliverable

- Department-specific knowledge views (sales brain, ops brain, support brain)
- Confidence scoring on answers (high/medium/low with reason)
- Feedback loop (thumbs up/down, correction submission)
- Updated document ingestion (version 2 of knowledge base)
- Usage analytics (questions asked, departments, satisfaction)

## 30-Day Deliverable

- Full knowledge base with versioning and audit trail
- Integration with Command Room OS (if deployed) for sales-specific queries
- Training session for all departments (live + recorded)
- Knowledge freshness policy (who updates, when, how)
- Monthly proof pack (questions answered, accuracy metrics, gaps found)
- Handoff document and access map

## Objection Handling

**Objection 1: "We can just use ChatGPT."**
Response: ChatGPT does not know your company. It will give confident but wrong answers about your pricing, policies, and offers. Our brain is grounded in your documents only, cites sources, and stays within access controls. Try asking ChatGPT for your latest discount policy — it cannot answer. Our brain can.

**Objection 2: "Our data is too sensitive for AI."**
Response: You choose which documents go in. Confidential contracts, client data, and financials can be excluded entirely. The brain only knows what you approve. We run company-isolated infrastructure and document every access. You can audit what was ingested at any time.

**Objection 3: "Our team is not technical enough for this."**
Response: The interface is a chat box. If your team can use WhatsApp, they can use the brain. No training on AI, no technical setup on their side. We handle everything and train them in one session.

## Delivery SOP

1. Intake call (60 min): identify departments, document scope, access roles
2. Diagnosis (Day 1–2): audit existing documents, classify in-scope vs excluded
3. Blueprint (Day 2–3): knowledge architecture, access matrix, approval from client
4. Build (Day 3–7): ingest documents, build vector store, create Q&A interface
5. QA (Day 7): test 50+ sample questions, validate citations and accuracy
6. UAT (Day 7–8): client team tests with real questions, provides feedback
7. Launch (Day 8): brain goes live for internal team
8. Training (Day 8–9): department-by-department session, recorded
9. Proof (Day 14 and Day 30): usage analytics, accuracy report, gap analysis
10. Handoff (Day 30): freshness policy, access map, retainer decision

## Risks and Limitations

- Answer quality depends on document quality; messy or outdated documents produce messy answers
- The brain cannot answer what is not in the documents; gaps must be filled by the client
- Some document types (scanned PDFs, images) may require manual transcription
- Access control adds complexity; misconfiguration can expose data to wrong roles
- The brain is not a replacement for human judgment in sensitive decisions
- PDPL compliance must be verified for any personal data in documents
