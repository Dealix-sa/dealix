# Delivery SOP

## Product Definition

The Delivery SOP is the standard operating procedure Dealix uses to deliver every AI Operating System engagement, from a 7-day sprint to a 30-day build and the monthly retainer that follows. The procedure follows five phases: Map, Design, Build, Operate, and Scale. Every product in the catalog is delivered through this same spine so a client experiences consistent quality, predictable handoffs, and a clear proof pack at every stage.

## Problem

AI engagements fail when delivery is ad hoc. Scope creeps, data is incomplete, approvals are missed, and the client cannot tell what was delivered versus promised. Without a standard delivery procedure, every engagement becomes a one-off, quality drops, and the team cannot scale delivery across multiple clients.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi B2B companies engaging Dealix for any sprint or build
- Founders and operations leads who need predictable, transparent delivery
- Teams that want a clear proof pack and acceptance sign-off
- Clients who will move to a monthly retainer after the build

## Who Should Not Buy It

- Companies that want a vendor to "just do it" with no process or sign-off
- Teams that refuse to share operational data for the Map phase
- Buyers who do not want any human approval step in delivery
- Companies expecting delivery without documented acceptance criteria

## Business Outcome

A repeatable delivery procedure that produces consistent quality, clear handoffs, governed AI use, and a proof pack at every stage, so every client knows exactly what was delivered and what comes next.

## Data Required

- Client scope confirmation and signed NDA
- Operational data relevant to the scoped product (sales, knowledge, outbound, compliance)
- Access to tools and accounts in scope
- Named client approver for each phase
- Current metrics and baselines for KPI tracking

## Workflow

The Delivery SOP runs in five phases. Each phase has entry criteria, activities, an approval gate, and an exit artifact.

### Phase 1: Map

1. Sign NDA and confirm scope
2. Run intake session with the client lead
3. Collect and clean relevant data
4. Document current state and baselines
5. Identify gaps and risks
6. Produce the Map report
7. Client approval gate on Map report

### Phase 2: Design

1. Translate Map findings into a delivery design
2. Define system structure, workflows, and approval points
3. Define roles: who drafts, who reviews, who approves
4. Configure access and permissions
5. Produce the Design spec
6. Client approval gate on Design spec

### Phase 3: Build

1. Implement the system per the Design spec
2. Load data, configure rules, and wire integrations
3. Set up AI drafting and human approval workflows
4. Run the first cycle with the client
5. Produce the Build record
6. Client approval gate on Build record

### Phase 4: Operate

1. Run the system live for the agreed period (daily reports, drafts, reviews)
2. Monitor KPIs and capture issues
3. Weekly review with the client
4. Iterate on rules and drafts based on feedback
5. Produce the Operate log
6. Client approval gate on Operate log

### Phase 5: Scale

1. Compile the proof pack with before/after evidence
2. Run acceptance review and sign-off
3. Present retainer handoff plan
4. Define scaling roadmap (additional systems, users, entities)
5. Produce the Scale plan
6. Client approval gate on Scale plan and retainer offer

## AI Role

- Draft reports, messages, and summaries at each phase
- Detect data gaps, risks, and anomalies during Map and Operate
- Generate the daily/weekly reports in Operate
- Suggest improvements during Scale
- Compile proof pack evidence

## Human Approval Role

- Approve the Map report before Design begins
- Approve the Design spec before Build begins
- Approve the Build record before Operate begins
- Approve every outbound message before send during Operate
- Sign off on the proof pack and Scale plan

## 7-Day Delivery

- Day 1-2: Map phase (intake, data, baselines)
- Day 3: Design phase (structure, workflows, approvals)
- Day 4-5: Build phase (system live, first cycle)
- Day 6: Operate phase (daily report, approval workflow)
- Day 7: Scale phase (proof pack, acceptance, retainer offer)

## 14-Day Delivery

- Day 1-3: Map phase (deeper intake, audit)
- Day 4-5: Design phase (full design spec)
- Day 6-9: Build phase (system, integrations, first cycles)
- Day 10-12: Operate phase (daily reports, weekly review, iterations)
- Day 13-14: Scale phase (proof pack, acceptance, retainer handoff)

## 30-Day Delivery

- Day 1-5: Map phase (full audit, multi-system intake)
- Day 6-9: Design phase (multi-system design spec)
- Day 10-20: Build phase (full multi-system build, integrations)
- Day 21-27: Operate phase (live cycles, KPI tracking, weekly reviews)
- Day 28-30: Scale phase (full proof pack, acceptance, retainer, scale roadmap)

- KPI before/after comparison (Scale)
- Approved message and action log

## KPIs

- On-time phase completion rate
- Client approval gate pass rate (first attempt vs rework)
- Daily report delivery rate during Operate
- Human approval turnaround time
- Proof pack completeness
- Client acceptance sign-off rate

## Upsell Path

- From a single sprint to a 30-Day AI Operating System Build (additional systems)
- From a build to additional systems (Revenue Command Room, Company Brain, Controlled Outbound, Trust Compliance)
- From retainer to Enterprise custom for multi-entity scaling

## Risks

- Client delays approval gates and stretches the timeline
- Incomplete data in Map limits Build quality
- Team skips a phase to save time and quality drops
- Over-reliance on AI drafts without human review in Operate
- Proof pack incomplete at Scale, blocking acceptance

## Acceptance Criteria

- All five phases completed with approval gates passed
- Map report, Design spec, Build record, Operate log, and Scale plan produced
- Proof pack complete with before/after evidence
- Client sign-off on acceptance
- Retainer handoff plan presented

## Objection Handling

- "This is too much process." — The procedure exists to protect you. Each gate is a checkpoint, not bureaucracy. You can stop or adjust at any gate.
- "We just want it built." — Build without Map and Design produces the wrong system. The Map phase is fast and prevents expensive rework.
- "We do not have time for approval gates." — Gates are short and async. The cost of skipping them is rework, which takes longer.
- "We do not need a proof pack." — The proof pack is how you prove internal ROI and justify the retainer. It is included at no extra cost.

## Implementation SOP

1. Sign NDA and confirm scope
2. Assign a delivery lead and client approver
3. Run Phase 1 (Map) and pass the approval gate
4. Run Phase 2 (Design) and pass the approval gate
5. Run Phase 3 (Build) and pass the approval gate
6. Run Phase 4 (Operate) and pass the approval gate
7. Run Phase 5 (Scale), deliver proof pack, and get sign-off
8. Present the retainer handoff plan
9. Archive the proof pack and delivery record

## What Not To Promise

- Do not promise guaranteed revenue or ROI from the delivery
- Do not promise delivery without client approval gates
- Do not promise auto-sending or auto-actions without human approval
- Do not use fake testimonials or fabricated case studies
- Do not promise a fixed timeline if the client delays approval gates
- Do not promise delivery quality if the client withholds data in Map
