# Pricing and Packaging

## Product Definition

This is the full pricing and packaging guide for Dealix's Saudi B2B AI Operating Systems. It defines every engagement tier from the free entry-level diagnostic up to enterprise custom builds, with SAR pricing ranges, what is included at each tier, and how a client moves from one package to the next. The goal is transparent, low-risk, staged pricing so a client never pays for more than they have proven.

## Problem

Saudi B2B buyers face unclear AI pricing, all-or-nothing contracts, and vendors who demand large upfront commitments before any proof. Without a clear packaging ladder, buyers stall, compare apples to oranges, or pick a cheaper vendor with no delivery substance. Dealix loses deals not on price but on clarity.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi B2B companies evaluating AI investment and needing a clear pricing path
- Founders and procurement leads who need staged, low-risk commitments
- Teams that need to compare packages on like-for-like scope
- Companies that want to start small and scale spend only after proof

## Who Should Not Buy It

- Companies looking for the cheapest option with no interest in delivery quality
- Buyers who want a one-shot fixed-price contract with no staged trial
- Teams that cannot share any operational data for a diagnostic
- Companies that expect guaranteed revenue figures before engagement

## Business Outcome

A transparent pricing and packaging ladder that lets a buyer start with a free or low-cost diagnostic, prove value in a short sprint, commit to a full build only after proof, and then move to a monthly partnership. Every package is scoped, priced in SAR, and tied to clear deliverables and acceptance criteria.

## SAR Pricing Range

The full Dealix pricing ladder in SAR:

| Package | SAR Range | Typical Buyer | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI Revenue Diagnostic | 0-1,500 SAR | Founder / Sales lead evaluating AI | 1-3 days |
| Founder Diagnostic | 1,500-3,000 SAR | Founder needing a decision-grade audit | 3-5 days |
| 7-Day Revenue Command Room Sprint | 5,000-12,000 SAR | Sales team with 5+ active deals | 7 days |
| 14-Day Company Brain Sprint | 15,000-35,000 SAR | 10-100 employee company needing knowledge centralization | 14 days |
| 30-Day AI Operating System Build | 25,000-90,000 SAR | Company committing to a full AI OS | 30 days |
| Monthly AI Operations Partner Retainer | 3,000-25,000 SAR/month | Any client post-sprint needing ongoing operation | Monthly |
| Enterprise custom | Quote-based | Large org or multi-entity group | Scoped per deal |

### What is included at each tier

- AI Revenue Diagnostic (0-1,500 SAR): lead and revenue audit, top 3 opportunity areas, one-page findings report. No build, no integration.
- Founder Diagnostic (1,500-3,000 SAR): decision-grade audit of sales, operations, and knowledge gaps, prioritized opportunity map, recommended next sprint. No build.
- 7-Day Revenue Command Room Sprint (5,000-12,000 SAR): unified pipeline view, follow-up queue, daily CEO report, draft message library, human approval workflow, proof pack.
- 14-Day Company Brain Sprint (15,000-35,000 SAR): knowledge intake, structured brain, source-backed Q&A, access permissions, human review workflow, gap report, onboarding pack.
- 30-Day AI Operating System Build (25,000-90,000 SAR): full multi-system build (pipeline, brain, outbound, compliance as scoped), integrations, daily reports, approval workflows, full proof pack, retainer handoff plan.
- Monthly AI Operations Partner Retainer (3,000-25,000 SAR/month): daily report generation, monitoring, draft message upkeep, weekly executive memos, monthly performance review.
- Enterprise custom (quote-based): scoped per deal, multi-entity, multi-system, custom integrations, dedicated delivery team, governance and compliance packaging.

## Retainer Path

After any sprint or build, the client transitions to the Monthly AI Operations Partner Retainer (3,000-25,000 SAR/month) for ongoing operation, monitoring, reporting, and optimization. Retainer scope is scaled to the number of systems live and the volume of daily activity.

## Upsell Path

- From diagnostic to 7-Day Revenue Command Room Sprint
- From 7-Day Sprint to 14-Day Company Brain Sprint
- From any sprint to 30-Day AI Operating System Build
- From any build to Monthly AI Operations Partner Retainer
- From retainer to Enterprise custom for multi-entity expansion

## Risks

- Underpricing a package relative to delivery cost
- Over-discounting erodes margin and perceived value
- Client perceives the ladder as too many steps
- Conversion rates drop between stages without clear reason
- Market shifts make a range uncompetitive

## Discovery Questions

- How do you currently budget for AI or operational tooling?
- What is your comfort level for a first-step commitment?
- Do you prefer a fixed scope or a flexible retainer?
- How do you evaluate pricing across vendors?
- What would make a pricing ladder easy to approve internally?
- Who needs to sign off on each pricing tier in your company?

## Objection Handling

- "Your prices are high." — Each tier is low-risk and scoped. You can stop at any stage. You only pay for more after you have proof.
- "We need a custom quote." — We have an Enterprise custom track for scoped, multi-entity deals.
- "We want one fixed price for everything." — The 30-Day AI Operating System Build is the closest to that, but we still recommend a diagnostic first so the scope is accurate.
- "Can we get a discount?" — Discounts follow an approved policy and are tied to scope reduction or multi-stage commitment, not arbitrary cuts.

## What Not To Promise

- Do not guarantee revenue or ROI from any package
- Do not use fake testimonials or fabricated case studies
- Do not promise fixed outcomes without execution
- Do not promise the lowest price in the market
- Do not promise a discount outside the approved policy
- Do not promise that every client will ascend the full ladder
