# WhatsApp/Inbox Follow-up OS

## Positioning

Dealix builds an AI-assisted follow-up system for WhatsApp and inbox that drafts, queues, and tracks follow-up messages — every message is human-approved before sending, nothing goes out automatically, and every conversation is tracked to closure.

## Problem

Follow-up is the weakest link in Saudi B2B sales. Leads come in, get one reply, then silence. Sales reps forget, get busy, or avoid uncomfortable follow-up. The founder does not know who was followed up and who was dropped. WhatsApp conversations disappear into personal phones. Email follow-ups are inconsistent. Deals are lost not because the offer is bad, but because nobody persisted.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi B2B company receiving 20+ leads/month via WhatsApp, email, or forms
- Sales team of 1–5 people who struggle with consistent follow-up
- Founder who wants follow-up discipline without micromanaging
- Company using WhatsApp Business as primary sales channel
- Deals with 7+ day sales cycles requiring multiple touchpoints
- Industry: real estate, training, B2B services, car dealers, clinics, logistics

## Objection Handling

**Objection 1: "We do not want automated WhatsApp messages, it feels robotic."**
Response: Nothing is sent automatically. AI drafts the message, a human reviews and approves it, then it sends. The draft is in your brand voice, in Arabic, and you can edit it before approval. The system ensures follow-up happens — it does not remove human judgment.

**Objection 2: "WhatsApp might ban us for automated messaging."**
Response: We respect WhatsApp's 24-hour service window and template requirements. Inside the window, human-approved replies are sent. Outside the window, only approved template messages are used. We do not do bulk blasts or spam. Every message is to a known lead with a conversation history.

**Objection 3: "Our team can handle follow-up themselves."**
Response: They can, but do they? The question is not capability, it is consistency. If your team followed up every lead on time, every time, you would not need this. Most teams start strong and fade after week two. The system maintains discipline without micromanagement.

## Delivery SOP

1. Intake call (60 min): map follow-up process, define cadence, approve initial templates
2. Diagnosis (Day 1–2): audit current follow-up behavior, identify dropped leads
3. Blueprint (Day 2–3): follow-up cadence, message templates, approval workflow
4. Build (Day 3–7): follow-up queue, AI drafting, conversation tracker, approval workflow
5. QA (Day 7): test drafting quality, approval flow, WhatsApp window compliance
6. UAT (Day 7–8): sales team tests with real leads, provides feedback on message quality
7. Launch (Day 8): system goes live for daily follow-up operations
8. Training (Day 8–9): sales team trained on approval workflow and tracker
9. Proof (Day 14 and Day 30): follow-up report, response rates, deals progressed
10. Handoff (Day 30): playbook, templates, retainer decision

## Risks and Limitations

- WhatsApp Business API has provider costs and rate limits outside our control
- 24-hour service window restricts when non-template messages can be sent
- Message drafting quality depends on context provided; sparse conversations produce generic drafts
- Team must actually approve messages; if approval is ignored, follow-up stalls
- WhatsApp number ban risk exists if platform policies are violated; we mitigate but cannot guarantee immunity
- Integration with certain WhatsApp providers may have technical limitations
