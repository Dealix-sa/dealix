# Customer Pain Radar

## Product Definition

An AI-assisted system that scans public sources, support tickets, sales conversations, and feedback channels to detect, classify, and prioritize customer pain points for Saudi B2B companies. The Customer Pain Radar ensures the company knows what hurts its customers before competitors do.

## Problem

Companies do not systematically listen for customer pain. Pain signals are scattered across support tickets, WhatsApp, sales calls, and social media. The founder hears about a recurring problem months late, after it has already cost deals.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi B2B companies with active customers and feedback channels
- Founders who want to detect pain before it costs deals
- Companies that receive support tickets, reviews, or complaints
- Teams that want to build product or service improvements from real pain

## Who Should Not Buy It

- Companies with no active customers or feedback channels
- Teams that already have a mature voice-of-customer system
- Companies unwilling to share customer feedback data
- Organizations that do not act on pain signals

## Business Outcome

A structured pain radar that detects, classifies, and prioritizes customer pain from multiple sources, so the company can act on pain before it costs deals or customers.

## Data Required

- Support ticket history
- WhatsApp and email customer conversations
- Sales call notes and summaries
- Public reviews and social mentions
- Customer feedback surveys (if available)
- Product or service categories to monitor

## Workflow

1. Intake: collect feedback sources and pain categories
2. Scan and classify pain signals from all sources
3. Prioritize pain by frequency, severity, and business impact
4. Generate pain radar report
5. Human review and action planning
6. Weekly pain review
7. Monthly pain trend analysis

## Objection Handling

- "We already listen to customers." — Listening is not systematic. We add multi-source scanning, classification, and prioritization so no pain signal is missed.
- "We do not have enough feedback data." — We start with what you have and build up. Even WhatsApp conversations are a rich pain source.
- "AI might misclassify pain." — Every classification is human-reviewed. AI scans, humans validate.
- "We do not have time to act on pain." — The radar prioritizes for you. You act on top pain first. Even knowing is an improvement.
