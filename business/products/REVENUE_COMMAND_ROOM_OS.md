# Revenue Command Room OS

## Positioning

Dealix builds an AI-powered command room for Saudi B2B companies to see their pipeline, follow-ups, proposals, next actions, and risk signals daily — in one place, updated automatically, approved by humans before anything goes out.

## Problem

Sales opportunities die silently. WhatsApp chats, email threads, sales notes, proposals, and follow-up reminders are scattered across tools and people. The founder cannot see the real pipeline. The sales team forgets to follow up. Proposals sit unanswered for weeks. Nobody knows which deals are at risk until they are already lost. Decisions are made on gut feeling, not on a live view of revenue operations.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi B2B company with 5–50 employees
- Founder/CEO who is involved in sales personally
- Sales team of 1–5 people using WhatsApp + email + spreadsheets
- 20+ active leads/deals per month
- No unified dashboard or CRM that the team actually uses
- Industry: clinics, real estate, training, logistics, B2B services, marketing agencies, car dealers, legal/accounting

## Inputs Required

- Access to WhatsApp Business / sales WhatsApp numbers (read-only export or API access)
- Access to email accounts used for sales (read-only or export)
- Current CRM or spreadsheet if any
- List of active deals and leads (even raw)
- Sales team names and roles
- Proposal templates currently in use
- 60-minute intake call with founder + sales lead

## 7-Day Deliverable

- Lead ledger (consolidated from WhatsApp, email, spreadsheets)
- Follow-up queue with owner and due date
- Proposal queue with status and aging
- Daily CEO snapshot report (draft, automated)
- Command Room dashboard (read-only web view)
- Draft outreach and follow-up messages (human-approved before send)
- Human approval workflow document

## 14-Day Deliverable

- Reply classification engine (interested / not now / not interested / needs info)
- Risk signal alerts (stalled deals, overdue follow-ups, aging proposals)
- Weekly executive memo (auto-generated draft)
- Sales playbook v1 (follow-up cadence, escalation rules)
- Command Room v2 with filters and saved views

## 30-Day Deliverable

- Daily operating report fully automated
- Proposal improvement recommendations (AI-assisted, human-approved)
- Monthly proof pack (what was built, what changed, what is next)
- Sales playbook v2 (refined based on 30 days of data)
- Team training session (live + recorded)
- Handoff document and access map

## KPIs

- Number of deals visible in Command Room (target: 100% of active deals by Day 14)
- Follow-up completion rate (target: 80%+ of due follow-ups completed within 24h)
- Proposal aging reduction (target: 30% reduction in proposals older than 7 days)
- CEO snapshot report delivered daily (target: 95%+ business days)
- Risk signals identified and acted on (target: 5+ per week in active pipeline)

3. How often does a lead go cold because nobody followed up?

## Objection Handling

**Objection 1: "We already have a CRM."**
Response: Most CRMs are bought but not used. The issue is not the tool, it is adoption and visibility. We do not replace your CRM — we connect it to WhatsApp, email, and proposals so the data is actually complete and the founder actually looks at it daily. Can your CEO open it right now and see which deals are at risk?

**Objection 2: "This is expensive for our size."**
Response: The question is how many deals you are losing per month because of scattered follow-ups. If one deal per month is saved, the sprint pays for itself. We start with a free or low-cost diagnostic so you see the gap before you commit. Pricing is transparent and phased — you do not pay for 30 days unless the 7-day sprint proves value.

**Objection 3: "We do not have time to set this up."**
Response: We do the build. Your team's time investment is a 60-minute intake call and approval of draft messages. Everything else is on us. The system is designed to save your team time, not add work.

## Delivery SOP

1. Intake call (60 min): founder + sales lead, confirm scope, access, timeline
2. Diagnosis (Day 1–2): audit current data sources, map deals, identify gaps
3. Blueprint (Day 2–3): document what will be built, get founder sign-off
4. Build (Day 3–7): lead ledger, follow-up queue, proposal queue, Command Room v1
5. QA (Day 7): internal quality check, data accuracy validation
6. UAT (Day 7–8): founder reviews Command Room, provides feedback
7. Launch (Day 8): Command Room goes live for daily use
8. Training (Day 8–9): live session with sales team, recorded
9. Proof (Day 14 and Day 30): proof pack documenting what was built and changed
10. Handoff (Day 30): access map, documentation, retainer decision

## Risks and Limitations

- Data quality depends on what the client provides; incomplete data means incomplete Command Room
- WhatsApp access may be limited by device/API constraints
- Team adoption is not guaranteed; the system is only useful if people use it
- AI-drafted messages require human review; we do not send anything without approval
- Dashboard is read-only initially; editing capabilities require retainer phase
- Pricing ranges assume single-entity; multi-entity costs more
