# Dealix Company Brain OS Expansion

## Positioning

Dealix builds client-specific operating systems for Saudi B2B companies.

The strongest package combines:

1. Company Brain OS
2. Revenue Command Room OS
3. Follow-up Recovery OS
4. Proposal Co-Pilot
5. Client Delivery OS
6. AI Trust OS

## Core promise

Turn scattered company signals into daily decisions, reviewed actions, and measurable proof.

## Modules

### Company Brain OS

Maps the company into revenue streams, customer segments, channels, bottlenecks, risks, priorities, and next actions.

Outputs:

- daily CEO decision
- weekly board memo
- opportunity map
- risk radar
- 30-day execution plan

### Revenue Command Room OS

Turns sales activity into a command room.

Outputs:

- hot opportunities
- overdue follow-ups
- proposal status
- owner and next action
- pipeline health
- daily revenue scorecard

### Follow-up Recovery OS

For companies losing opportunities in email, calls, or chat inboxes.

Outputs:

- conversation categories
- follow-up queue
- reviewed reply drafts
- escalation rules
- lost-opportunity log

### Proposal Co-Pilot

Helps the company negotiate better without weakening scope.

Outputs:

- objection handling
- price-floor discipline
- tradeable scope map
- closing questions
- proposal notes

### Client Delivery OS

Turns client projects into repeatable delivery.

Outputs:

- intake
- scope card
- acceptance criteria
- delivery board
- proof pack
- renewal opportunity

### AI Trust OS

Keeps AI useful and controlled.

Outputs:

- policy
- approval gates
- audit trail
- safe-use playbook
- data handling rules

## How to sell it to any company

Start from the company's visible pain:

| Company signal | Pain to test | Offer angle |
|---|---|---|
| many branches | inconsistent follow-up | command room per branch |
| many inquiries | lost leads | follow-up recovery |
| many proposals | weak closing | proposal command room |
| growing team | unclear ownership | company brain |
| regulated work | AI risk | trust and approval OS |
| service delivery | inconsistent outputs | client delivery OS |
