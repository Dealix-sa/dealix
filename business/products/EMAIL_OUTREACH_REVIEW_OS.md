# Email Outreach Review OS

## Positioning

Dealix builds a controlled email outreach system that drafts, reviews, and sends B2B cold and warm email — with SPF/DKIM/DMARC readiness checks, unsubscribe compliance, suppression lists, rate limiting, and human approval before any message goes out.

## Problem

Email outreach in Saudi B2B is either nonexistent or done badly. Companies send from personal Gmail with no authentication, no unsubscribe, no suppression list, and no tracking. Deliverability is terrible. Emails land in spam. The founder cannot tell if emails were opened, replied to, or ignored. There is no review process, so messages go out with errors, wrong names, or non-compliant claims. The company risks reputation damage and legal exposure.

## Ideal Customer

- Saudi B2B company doing or wanting to do cold/warm email outreach
- 100+ target prospects in a defined sector
- Founder or sales lead who wants controlled, reviewed, compliant outreach
- Company with a domain they control (can set DNS records)
- No existing email infrastructure or ineffective existing setup
- Industry: B2B services, logistics, training, marketing agencies, legal/accounting

## Objection Handling

**Objection 1: "We can just use Mailchimp or Gmail."**
Response: Tools are not the problem — configuration and compliance are. Mailchimp with no DMARC and no suppression list still lands in spam. We set up the full authentication stack, review every email before it sends, and track deliverability. The tool is the last piece, not the first.

**Objection 2: "Cold email is spam, we do not want to be spammers."**
Response: Done badly, it is spam. Done right, it is targeted, sourced, compliant outreach to verified prospects with a real reason and an easy opt-out. We require a source URL for every prospect, human approval for every message, and immediate suppression on any opt-out. You control who, what, and how many.

**Objection 3: "25 emails a day is too slow."**
Response: 25 controlled, reviewed, compliant emails that reach the inbox are worth more than 500 that land in spam. We start low to warm up the domain and protect deliverability. Volume increases as reputation builds. Speed without deliverability is wasted effort.

## Delivery SOP

1. Intake call (60 min): audit domain, confirm prospect list, approve templates
2. Diagnosis (Day 1–2): DNS audit, deliverability assessment, list quality check
3. Blueprint (Day 2–3): authentication plan, template library, sending schedule, approval workflow
4. Build (Day 3–7): SPF/DKIM/DMARC setup, unsubscribe deployment, suppression list, drafting engine, tracking
5. QA (Day 7): authentication verification, test sends, inbox placement check, unsubscribe test
6. UAT (Day 7–8): client reviews draft emails, approves first batch, tests approval workflow
7. Launch (Day 8): first controlled batch sent (max 25/day)
8. Training (Day 8–9): sales team trained on drafting review, reply handling, suppression
9. Proof (Day 14 and Day 30): deliverability report, open/reply rates, suppression compliance
10. Handoff (Day 30): outreach playbook, retainer decision

## Risks and Limitations

- Domain reputation may already be damaged; recovery takes time and is not guaranteed
- Inbox placement depends on recipient providers (Gmail, Outlook, etc.) and is never 100% guaranteed
- Cold email response rates are inherently low; this is a numbers and quality game
- Rate limiting is necessary to protect deliverability; impatience undermines the system
- Prospect list quality is the client's responsibility; bad lists produce bad results
- PDPL compliance must be verified for any personal data in prospect lists
