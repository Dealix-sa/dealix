# Founder Doctrine

## The Hard Truth

The repo is not the company. The product is not the company. The company is the repeatable ability to create customer value, prove it, retain it, and turn repeated patterns into assets.

## Strategic Law

If a project only makes money and creates no asset, Dealix sold time.
If a project makes money and creates proof, template, feature, playbook, benchmark, or trust, Dealix built company value.

## Operating Doctrine

1. Sell simple.
2. Deliver manually first.
3. Prove value.
4. Repeat the same workflow.
5. Productize the repeated pain.
6. Create a retainer.
7. Build platform modules only after repetition.
8. Protect trust harder than revenue.

## What To Build First

Build what helps close, deliver, prove, or retain.

## What Not To Build First

Do not build full SaaS, agent marketplace, complex dashboards, or abstract architecture before three paid repetitions of a service.
