# Dealix Operating Doctrine

The single non-negotiable doctrine that governs how Dealix operates as a company. Every script, document, agent, and decision must conform to this doctrine.

## Purpose
Define the operating model that turns Dealix from a collection of files into a running company. The doctrine is the contract between the founder, the AI, and every system in the company. If a script or playbook contradicts the doctrine, the doctrine wins.

## Owner
Sami (Founder).

## Review Cadence
Weekly during the first 90 days, then monthly.

## Inputs
- Founder strategic intent.
- Customer reality (paid evidence, refusals, friction).
- Operating signals from the five loops.
- Weekly Intelligence Review output.

## Outputs
- The five operating loops below.
- The autonomy boundary between AI and founder.
- The escalation rules for unbounded decisions.

## Rules
- AI prepares, Founder approves. No external action, payment, message, or commitment leaves the company without founder approval until the autonomy level is explicitly raised.
- No customer-visible artifact without evidence linked.
- No claim without a source.
- Private operating data never crosses into the public repository.
- Every loop produces a written artifact every cycle.

## Metrics
- Number of approved actions per week.
- Number of doctrine violations caught by verifiers.
- Loop completion rate (5/5 loops complete each week).
- Founder review latency on the decision queue.

## Evidence
- The five loop artifacts produced each week.
- The approval log in the private ops repo.
- The friction log of violations and fixes.
- The weekly CEO review.

## The Five Operating Loops

### 1. Revenue Loop
Sourcing → Outreach → Qualification → Proposal → Paid → Retainer.

The loop runs daily. AI prepares the next move (drafts, lists, proposals). Founder approves and sends. Every contact is logged in the pipeline tracker. Every outcome (reply, refusal, payment) is logged with a one-line reason.

### 2. Delivery Loop
Intake → Sample → Sprint → Proof Pack → Handover → Retainer eligibility.

Once a customer pays, the delivery loop runs against the offer playbook for that rung of the offer ladder. Every artifact produced is reviewed against the QA checklist before it is sent.

### 3. Trust Loop
Approval Matrix → Autonomy Policy → Action Log → Suppression List → Audit.

Every action that touches the outside world (message, payment, commitment) is classified by approval level (A0–A3 or Never) and autonomy level (L0–L4). The log is immutable and reviewed weekly.

### 4. Learning Loop
Signal → Experiment → Result → Decision → Doctrine update.

Every week, the Weekly Intelligence Review captures what happened, what worked, what failed, what is the current bottleneck, and what will change. Decisions update the operating documents — the doctrine is a living artifact.

### 5. CEO Loop
Daily Brief → Decisions → Approvals → Weekly Review → Strategic adjustment.

The founder reads one daily brief in the morning, makes the decisions queued for them, approves the actions that need approval, and writes the weekly review on the last working day of each week. No more meetings with self.

## The Autonomy Contract

| Level | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| L0 Manual | Founder does everything | First customer message of a new sector |
| L1 Assisted | AI drafts, founder edits and sends | Outreach DMs, proposals |
| L2 Semi-Auto | AI sends with founder pre-approval per batch | Follow-ups inside an approved sequence |
| L3 Auto | AI acts inside approved scope, logs result | Internal report compilation |
| L4 Prohibited | Never AI | External payments, contracts, public statements |

## The Approval Matrix

| Tier | Description | Required For |
|---|---|---|
| A0 | No approval needed | Internal drafts, research notes |
| A1 | Self-review by founder | Outbound messages, proposals under 5K SAR |
| A2 | Founder + one peer reviewer | Public statements, proposals over 5K SAR |
| A3 | Founder + customer/legal sign-off | Contracts, refunds, public partnerships |
| Never | Forbidden | Cold scraping, mass DMs, automated payments, claims without evidence |
